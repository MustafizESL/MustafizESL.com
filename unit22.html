Unit 22: Passive Voice: Present Continuous and Present Perfect

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use the passive voice in the present continuous and present perfect tenses.

Overview: This unit focuses on the formation and usage of the passive voice in different contexts.

Explanation

Present Continuous Passive: Use "am/is/are being" followed by the past participle of the verb.

  • Example: The car is being repaired. / The house is being painted.

Present Perfect Passive: Use "has/have been" followed by the past participle of the verb.

  • Example: The car has been repaired. / The house has been painted.

Examples

Present Continuous Passive:

  • The book is being read by Sarah.
  • The cake is being decorated.
  • The room is being cleaned.

Present Perfect Passive:

  • The book has been read by Sarah.
  • The cake has been decorated.
  • The room has been cleaned.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 22.1: What's happening?

  • 1. The car is being fixed.
  • 2. A new road is being constructed.
  • 3. The floors are being mopped.
  • 4. The lawn is being mowed.

Exercise 22.2: What is happening or what has happened? Use the present continuous (is/are being..) or the present perfect (has/have been..)

  • 1. (the office / clean) The office is being cleaned.
  • 2. (the clothes / iron) The clothes have been ironed.
  • 3. (the door / paint) The door has been painted.
  • 4. (the windows / wash) The windows are being washed.
  • 5. (the car / repair) The car has been repaired.
  • 6. (the walls / paint) The walls are being painted.
  • 7. (the trees / trim) The trees have been trimmed.
  • 8. (they / invite / to the meeting) They have been invited to the meeting.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use the passive voice in both the present continuous and present perfect tenses. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in these tenses.
