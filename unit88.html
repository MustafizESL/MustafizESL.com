Unit 88: Using Comparatives with "Than"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use comparatives with "than" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using comparatives to compare two people or things, emphasizing differences with "than."

Explanation

Comparatives with "Than": Comparatives are used to compare two things or people, often followed by "than" to show the difference.

  • Example: Liz is older than Ben.

Examples

Using Comparatives with "Than":

  • Liz is older than Ben.
  • Ben is a better swimmer than Liz.
  • Liz is taller than Ben.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 88.1: Write sentences about Liz and Ben. Use "than"

  • Liz:
    • 1. I'm 26.
    • 2. I'm not a very good swimmer.
    • 3. I'm 1 metre 68 tall.
    • 4. I start work at 8 o'clock.
    • 5. I don't work very hard.
    • 6. I haven't got much money.
    • 7. I'm a very good driver.
    • 8. I'm not very patient.
    • 9. I'm not a very good dancer.
    • 10. I'm very intelligent.
    • 11. I speak French very well.
    • 12. I don't go to the cinema very much.
  • Ben:
    • 1. I'm 24.
    • 2. I'm a very good swimmer.
    • 3. I'm 1 metre 63 tall.
    • 4. I start work at 8.30.
    • 5. I work very hard.
    • 6. I've got a lot of money.
    • 7. I'm not a very good driver.
    • 8. I'm very patient.
    • 9. I'm a good dancer.
    • 10. I'm not very intelligent.
    • 11. I don't speak French very well.
    • 12. I go to the cinema a lot.
  • Sentences:
    • 1. Liz is older than Ben.
    • 2. Ben is a better swimmer than Liz.
    • 3. Liz is taller than Ben.
    • 4. Liz starts work earlier than Ben.
    • 5. Ben works harder than Liz.
    • 6. Ben has got more money than Liz.
    • 7. Liz is a better driver than Ben.
    • 8. Ben is more patient than Liz.
    • 9. Ben is a better dancer than Liz./ Ben dances better than Liz.
    • 10. Liz is more intelligent than Ben.
    • 11. Liz speaks French better than Ben. / Liz speaks better French than Ben./ Liz's French is better than Ben's.
    • 12. Ben goes to the cinema more than Liz. / Ben goes to the cinema more often than Liz.

Exercise 88.2: Complete the sentences. Use "than"

  • 1. He isn't very tall. You're taller than him. (OR taller than he is)
  • 2. She isn't very old. You're older than her. / ... than she is.
  • 3. I don't work very hard. You work harder than me. / ... than I do.
  • 4. He doesn't watch TV very much. You watch TV more than him. / ... than he does.
  • 5. I'm not a very good cook. You're a better cook than me. / ... than I am. or You cook better than me. / ... than I do.
  • 6. We don't know many people. You know more people than us. / ... than we do.
  • 7. They haven't got much money. You've got more money than them. / ... than they have.
  • 8. I can't run very fast. You can run faster than me. / ... than I can.
  • 9. She hasn't been here very long. You've been here longer than her. / ... than she has.
  • 10. They didn't get up very early. You got up earlier than them. / ... than they did.
  • 11. He wasn't very surprised. You were more surprised than him. / ... than he was.

Exercise 88.3: Complete the sentences with "a bit" or "much" + comparative (older/better etc.)

  • 1. Emma is 25. Gary is 24.
    • Emma is a bit older than Gary.
  • 2. Jack's mother is 52. His father is 69.
    • Jack's mother is much younger than his father.
  • 3. My camera cost £100. Yours cost £96.
    • My camera cost a bit more than yours. / than your camera. / My camera was a bit more expensive than your camera.
  • 4. Yesterday I felt terrible. Today I feel OK.
    • I feel much better today than yesterday. / than I did yesterday. / than I felt yesterday.
  • 5. Today the temperature is 12 degrees. Yesterday it was 11 degrees.
    • It’s a bit warmer today than yesterday. / than it was yesterday.
  • 6. Sarah is an excellent tennis player. I'm not very good.
    • Sarah is a much better tennis player than me / than I am.
    • Sarah is much better at tennis than me / than I am.
    • Sarah plays tennis much better than me / than I do.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use comparatives with "than" to compare two things or people. We formed comparatives by adding "-er" for one-syllable adjectives and using "more" for longer adjectives. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
