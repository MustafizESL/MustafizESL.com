Unit 15: Present Perfect: has/have + past participle

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use the present perfect tense to describe actions that have happened at an unspecified time in the past or actions that have an effect on the present.

Overview: This unit focuses on the formation and usage of the present perfect tense.

Explanation

Forming the Present Perfect: Use "has" or "have" followed by the past participle of the verb.

  • Example: I have finished my homework. / She has seen that movie.

Examples

Positive Statements:

  • I have read the book.
  • She has cooked dinner.
  • They have traveled to Spain.

Negative Statements:

  • I have not (haven't) read the book.
  • She has not (hasn't) cooked dinner.
  • They have not (haven't) traveled to Spain.

Questions:

  • Have you read the book?
  • Has she cooked dinner?
  • Have they traveled to Spain?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 15.1: What has happened? Choose from the box:

  • go to sleep, paint the wall, write a letter, finish the homework, fix the car, wash the dishes
  • 1. He has painted the wall.
  • 2. She has written a letter.
  • 3. They have gone to sleep.
  • 4. I have finished the homework.
  • 5. He has fixed the car.
  • 6. She has washed the dishes.

Exercise 15.2: Complete the sentences with a verb from the box:

  • break, buy, decide, finish, forget, go, invite, read, see, not/see, take, tell, not/tell
  • 1. 'Can I have a look at your book?' 'Yes, I have finished it.'
  • 2. I have bought some new clothes. Do you want to see them?
  • 3. 'Where is John?' 'He's not here. He has gone out.'
  • 4. I'm looking for Emily. Have you seen her?
  • 5. Look! Someone has broken the vase.
  • 6. 'Does Anna know that you're coming?' 'Yes, I have told her.'
  • 7. I can't find my keys. Someone has taken them.
  • 8. 'Where is my phone?' 'I don’t know. I haven’t seen it.'
  • 9. I'm looking for Tom. Where has he gone?
  • 10. I know that person, but I have forgotten their name.
  • 11. Mike is having a party tonight. He has invited a lot of people.
  • 12. What are you going to do? Have you decided?
  • 13. A: Does Mary know about the meeting tomorrow? B: I don’t think so. I haven’t told her.
  • 14. 'Do you want this magazine?' 'No, I have read it, thanks.'

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use the present perfect tense to describe actions that have happened at an unspecified time in the past or actions that have an effect on the present. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in the present perfect tense.
