Unit 16: Present Perfect with "just," "already," and "yet"

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use the present perfect tense with the adverbs "just," "already," and "yet" to describe actions that have recently happened or actions that are expected to happen.

Overview: This unit focuses on the formation and usage of the present perfect tense with "just," "already," and "yet."

Explanation

Forming the Present Perfect: Use "has" or "have" followed by the past participle of the verb.

  • Example: I have finished my homework. / She has seen that movie.

Using "just": Indicates that the action happened a short time ago.

  • Example: They have just arrived.

Using "already": Indicates that the action happened sooner than expected or before now.

  • Example: She has already finished her work.

Using "yet": Used in negative sentences and questions to talk about something expected to happen.

  • Example: Have you finished your homework yet? / I haven't finished it yet.

Examples

Positive Statements:

  • I have just eaten lunch.
  • She has already finished her project.
  • They have just returned from their trip.

Negative Statements:

  • I have not (haven't) finished my homework yet.
  • He has not (hasn't) called me yet.
  • They have not (haven't) left the house yet.

Questions:

  • Have you finished your homework yet?
  • Has she called you yet?
  • Have they left the house yet?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 16.1: Write a sentence with "just":

  • 1. They have just finished their meal.
  • 2. He has just taken a shower.
  • 3. They have just moved to a new house.
  • 4. The game has just begun.

Exercise 16.2: Complete the sentences. Use "already" + present perfect:

  • 1. What time is Sarah arriving? She's already arrived.
  • 2. Do your colleagues want to join us? No, they've already made other plans.
  • 3. Don’t forget to send the email. I've already sent it.
  • 4. When is Tom leaving for his trip? He's already left.
  • 5. Do you want to watch the movie? I've already watched it.
  • 6. When does Anna start her new project? She's already started.

Exercise 16.3: Write a sentence with "just" or a negative sentence with "yet":

  • 1. (he/go/out) He hasn’t gone out yet.
  • 2. (the bus/arrive) The bus has just arrived.
  • 3. (the meeting/start) The meeting has just started.
  • 4. (she/open/her gift) She hasn’t opened her gift yet.
  • 5. (they/finish/their homework) They’ve just finished their homework.
  • 6. (it/stop/snowing) It hasn’t stopped snowing yet.

Exercise 16.4: Write questions with "yet":

  • 1. Your friend has applied for a new job. Perhaps she has received a response. You ask her: Have you received a response yet?
  • 2. Your friend has ordered a new laptop. Perhaps it has been delivered. You ask him: Has your laptop been delivered yet?
  • 3. Your friend needs to renew her passport. Perhaps she has done it. You ask her: Have you renewed your passport yet?
  • 4. Mark was looking for a new apartment. Perhaps he has found one. You ask a friend about Mark: Has Mark found a new apartment yet?

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use the present perfect tense to describe actions that have recently happened or actions that are expected to happen. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in the present perfect tense with "just," "already," and "yet."
