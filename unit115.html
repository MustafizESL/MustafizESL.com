Unit 115: Using Phrasal Verbs with Objects

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to correctly use phrasal verbs with objects, which combine verbs with prepositions like "in," "out," "up," etc., to describe actions involving objects.

Overview: This unit focuses on using these phrasal verbs with objects to form meaningful sentences, making sentences clearer and more descriptive.

Explanation

Using Phrasal Verbs with Objects: Phrasal verbs can be separable or inseparable when used with objects. Separable phrasal verbs allow the object to be placed between the verb and the preposition or after the preposition.

  • Example: I turned on the radio. / I turned the radio on.

Examples

Using Phrasal Verbs with Objects in Sentences:

  • He turned on the light.
  • She took off her hat. / She took her hat off.
  • He put down his bag. / He put his bag down.
  • She picked up the magazine. / She picked the magazine up.
  • He put on his sunglasses. / He put his sunglasses on.
  • She turned off the tap. / She turned the tap off.

Using Phrasal Verbs with Objects in Three Different Ways:

  • I turned on the radio. / I turned the radio on. / I turned it on.
  • He put on his jacket. / He put his jacket on. / He put it on.
  • She took off her glasses. / She took her glasses off. / She took them off.
  • I picked up the phone. / I picked the phone up. / I picked it up.
  • They gave back the key. / They gave the key back. / They gave it back.
  • We turned off the light. / We turned the light off. / We turned them off.

Using Verbs with "It" or "Them":

  • I wanted to watch something on television, so I turned it on.
  • My new lamp doesn't work. I'm going to take it back to the shop.
  • There were some gloves on the floor, so I picked them up and put them on the table.
  • The heating was on but it was too warm, so I switched it off.
  • Thank you for lending me these books. I won't forget to bring them back.

Using Verbs from the Box with It/Them/Me:

  • They knocked a lot of houses down when they built the new road.
  • That music is very loud. Can you turn it down?
  • I knocked over a glass and broke it.
  • What does this word mean? Here's a dictionary. You can look it up.
  • I want to keep these magazines. Please don't throw them away.
  • I tried on a pair of shoes in the shop, but I didn't buy them.
  • I visited a school last week. One of the teachers showed me round.
  • 'Do you play the piano?' 'No, I started to learn, but I gave it up.'
  • Somebody gave me a form and told me to fill it in.
  • Smoking isn't allowed here. Please put your cigarette out.
