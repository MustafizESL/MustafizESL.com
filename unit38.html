Unit 38: Using "There was" and "There were"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "there was" and "there were" to describe the presence or absence of things in the past.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "there was" for singular nouns and "there were" for plural nouns to talk about what existed or did not exist in the past.

Explanation

Using "There was" for Singular Nouns: Use "there was" to indicate the presence of a single item in the past.

  • Example: There was a cat on the sofa.

Using "There were" for Plural Nouns: Use "there were" to indicate the presence of multiple items in the past.

  • Example: There were several books on the table.

Using Negative Forms: Use "there wasn't" for singular nouns and "there weren't" for plural nouns to indicate the absence of something in the past.

  • Example: There wasn't a phone in the room. / There weren't any chairs left.

Examples

Affirmative:

  • There was a light bulb on the shelf.
  • There were many people at the concert.

Negative:

  • There wasn't a mirror in the bathroom.
  • There weren't any cookies in the jar.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 38.1: What was in the room last week? Choose from the box and write sentences with "there was..." or "there were..."

  • 1. There was a clock on the wall near the window.
  • 2. There was a carpet on the floor.
  • 3. There were three pictures on the wall near the door.
  • 4. There was a table in the middle of the room.
  • 5. There were some flowers on the table.
  • 6. There were some books on the shelves.
  • 7. There was an armchair in the corner near the door.
  • 8. There was a sofa opposite the armchair.

Exercise 38.2: Write "there was / there wasn't / was there" or "there were / there weren't / were there"

  • 1. I was hungry, but there wasn't anything to eat.
  • 2. Were there any messages for me yesterday?
  • 3. I opened the envelope, but it was empty. There was nothing in it.
  • 4. 'We stayed at a nice hotel.' 'Really? Was there a swimming pool?'
  • 5. 'Did you buy any bread?' 'No, there weren't any in the store.'
  • 6. The wallet was empty. There wasn't any money in it.
  • 7. 'Were there many guests at the party?' 'No, just a few.'
  • 8. We didn't visit the museum. There wasn't enough time.
  • 9. I'm sorry I'm late. There was a lot of traffic.
  • 10. Twenty years ago, there weren't many tourists here. Now there are a lot.

Exercise 38.3: Write "there + is / are / was / were / has been / have been / will be"

  • 1. There was a great concert last night.
  • 2. There are 24 hours in a day.
  • 3. There was a meeting at the office last Monday, but I couldn't attend.
  • 4. 'Where can I buy a map?' 'There's a store down the street.'
  • 5. 'Why are those people outside the bank?' 'There's been a protest.'
  • 6. When we arrived at the cinema, there was a line outside.
  • 7. When you arrive tomorrow, there will be someone to greet you.
  • 8. Ten years ago, there were 300 students at the school. Now there are over a thousand.
  • 9. Last week, I visited my hometown. It's very different now. There have been many changes.
  • 10. I think everything will be fine. I don't think there will be any issues.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "there was" and "there were" to describe the presence or absence of things in the past. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to talk about what existed or did not exist in the past.
