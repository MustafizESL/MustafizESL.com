Unit 49: Answering Questions with "I don’t know" and Asking "Do you know...?"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "I don’t know" to answer questions and how to ask questions using "Do you know...?" to gather specific information.

Overview: This unit focuses on forming responses with "I don’t know" and creating questions using "Do you know...?" to inquire about various details.

Explanation

Answering with "I don’t know": Use "I don’t know" followed by a question word (where, when, why, etc.) to answer questions when you lack information.

  • Example: Have your friends come home? I don’t know where they've gone.

Asking with "Do you know...?": Use "Do you know...?" followed by a question to ask for specific information.

  • Example: Do you know if they've got a car?

Examples

Answering with "I don’t know":

  • Is the castle very old? I don’t know how old it is.
  • Will Paul be here soon? I don’t know when he'll be here.

Asking with "Do you know...?":

  • Do you know if they've got a car?
  • Do you know what Laura wants?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 49.1: Answer these questions with "I don’t know where/when/why..."

  • 1. Have your friends come home? (where)
    • I don’t know where they've gone.
  • 2. Is Kate in her office? (where)
    • I don’t know where she is.
  • 3. Is the castle very old? (how old)
    • I don’t know how old it is.
  • 4. Will Paul be here soon? (when)
    • I don’t know when he'll be here.
  • 5. Was he angry because I was late? (why)
    • I don’t know why he was angry.
  • 6. Has Sally lived here a long time? (how long)
    • I don’t know how long she has lived here.

Exercise 49.2: Complete the sentences

  • 1. (How do airplanes fly?) Do you know how airplanes fly?
  • 2. (Where does Susan work?) I don't know where Susan works.
  • 3. (What did Peter say?) Do you remember what Peter said?
  • 4. (Why did he go home early?) I don't know why he went home early.
  • 5. (What time does the meeting begin?) Do you know what time the meeting begins?
  • 6. (How did the accident happen?) I don't remember how the accident happened.

Exercise 49.3: Which is right? (Choose the correct sentence)

  • 1. Do you know what time it is?
  • 2. Why are you going away?
  • 3. I don't know where they are going.

Exercise 49.4: Write questions with "Do you know if...?"

  • 1. (Have they got a car?) Do you know if they've got a car?
  • 2. (Are they married?) Do you know if they are married?
  • 3. (Does Sue know Bill?) Do you know if Sue knows Bill?
  • 4. (Will Gary be here tomorrow?) Do you know if Gary will be here tomorrow?
  • 5. (Did he pass his exam?) Do you know if he passed his exam?

Exercise 49.5: Write questions beginning with "Do you know...?"

  • 1. (What does Laura want?) Do you know what Laura wants?
  • 2. (Where is Paula?) Do you know where Paula is?
  • 3. (Is she working?) Do you know if she is working today?
  • 4. (What time does she start work?) Do you know what time she starts work?
  • 5. (Are the shops open tomorrow?) Do you know if the shops are open tomorrow?
  • 6. (Where do Sarah and Tim live?) Do you know where Sarah and Tim live?
  • 7. (Did they go to Jane's party?) Do you know if they went to Jane's party?

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to answer questions with "I don’t know" and ask questions using "Do you know...?" to gather specific information. We practiced forming responses and questions using these structures to enhance communication skills.
