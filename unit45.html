Unit 45: Forming Questions with "Who" and "What"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to form questions with "who" and "what" to ask about the subject or object.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "who" and "what" to form questions that inquire about people and things as the subject or object of a sentence.

Explanation

Questions with "Who" and "What" as the Subject: Use "who" and "what" to ask about the subject of a sentence.

  • Example: Somebody broke the window. Who broke the window?

Questions with "Who" and "What" as the Object: Use "who" and "what" to ask about the object of a sentence.

  • Example: I bought something. What did you buy?

Examples

Questions with "Who" and "What" as the Subject:

  • Somebody wants to see you. Who wants to see me?
  • Something made me ill. What made you ill?

Questions with "Who" and "What" as the Object:

  • I phoned somebody. Who did you phone?
  • Jane did something. What did Jane do?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 45.1: Make questions with "who" or "what." In these questions, "who/what" is the subject

  • 1. Somebody broke the window. Who broke the window?
  • 2. Something fell off the shelf. What fell off the shelf?
  • 3. Somebody wants to see you. Who wants to see me?
  • 4. Somebody took my umbrella. Who took your umbrella? / Who took it?
  • 5. Something made me ill. What made you ill?
  • 6. Somebody is coming. Who is/ Who's coming?

Exercise 45.2: Make questions with "who" or "what" (subject or object)

  • 1. I bought something. What did you buy?
  • 2. Somebody lives in this house. Who lives in this house?
  • 3. I phoned somebody. Who did you phone?
  • 4. Something happened last night. What happened last night?
  • 5. Somebody knows the answer. Who knows the answer?
  • 6. Somebody did the washing-up. Who did the washing-up?
  • 7. Jane did something. What did Jane do? / What did she do?
  • 8. Something woke me up. What woke you up?
  • 9. Somebody saw the accident. Who saw the accident?
  • 10. I saw somebody. Who did you see?
  • 11. Somebody has got my pen. Who has got your pen? / Who has got it? or Who's got it?
  • 12. This word means something. What does this word mean? / What does it mean?

Exercise 45.3: You want the missing information (xxxxx). Write questions with "who" or "what"

  • 1. I lost xxxxx yesterday, but fortunately xxxxx found it and gave it back to me.
    • What did you lose?
    • Who found it?
  • 2. Xxxxx phoned me last night. She wanted xxxxx.
    • Who phoned you?
    • What did she want?
  • 3. I needed some advice, so I asked xxxxx. He said xxxxx.
    • Who did you ask?
    • What did he say?
  • 4. I hear that xxxxx got married last week. Xxxxx told me.
    • Who got married?
    • Who told you?
  • 5. I met xxxxx on my way home this evening. She told me xxxxx.
    • Who did you meet?
    • What did she tell you?
  • 6. Steve and I played tennis yesterday. Xxxxx won. After the game we xxxxx.
    • Who won?
    • What did you do (after the game)?
  • 7. It was my birthday last week and I had some presents. Xxxxx gave me a book and Catherine gave me xxxxx.
    • Who gave you a/the book?
    • What did Catherine give you?

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to form questions with "who" and "what" to ask about the subject or object of a sentence. We practiced forming questions using these structures to enhance communication skills.
