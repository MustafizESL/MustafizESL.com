Unit 40: Completing Sentences with Verbs and Negations

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to complete sentences using appropriate verbs, negations, and auxiliary verbs.

Overview: This unit focuses on using the correct forms of verbs and negations to complete sentences accurately.

Explanation

Using Auxiliary Verbs: Use auxiliary verbs like "is," "have," "can," etc., to complete sentences.

  • Example: Kate wasn't hungry, but we were.

Using Negations: Use negative forms like "isn't," "haven't," "can't," etc., to complete sentences.

  • Example: My sister can play the piano, but I can't.

Using Auxiliary Verbs for Emphasis: Use auxiliary verbs like "do," "does," "did," etc., for emphasis in sentences.

  • Example: I don't like hot weather, but Sue does.

Examples

Positive Auxiliary Verbs:

  • I'm not married, but my brother is.
  • Bill can't help you, but I can.

Negative Forms:

  • Sam is working today, but I'm not.
  • Mark has been to China, but I haven't.

Emphatic Auxiliary Verbs:

  • I don't like hot weather, but Sue does.
  • You had breakfast this morning, but I didn't.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 40.1: Complete these sentences. Use only one verb (is/have/can etc.) each time

  • 1. Kate wasn't hungry, but we were.
  • 2. I'm not married, but my brother is.
  • 3. Bill can't help you, but I can.
  • 4. I haven't seen the film, but Tom has.
  • 5. Karen won’t be here, but Chris will.
  • 6. You weren’t late, but I was.

Exercise 40.2: Complete these sentences with a negative verb (isn't/haven't/can't etc.)

  • 1. My sister can play the piano, but I can't.
  • 2. Sam is working today, but I'm not.
  • 3. I was working, but my friends weren’t.
  • 4. Mark has been to China, but I haven't.
  • 5. I am ready to go, but Tom isn't.
  • 6. I've got a key, but Sally hasn't.

Exercise 40.3: Complete these sentences with do/does/did or don't/doesn't/didn't

  • 1. I don't like hot weather, but Sue does.
  • 2. Sue likes hot weather, but I don't.
  • 3. My mother wears glasses, but my father doesn't.
  • 4. You don't know Paul very well, but I do.
  • 5. I didn't enjoy the party, but my friends did.
  • 6. I don't watch TV much, but Peter does.
  • 7. Kate lives in London, but her parents don't.
  • 8. You had breakfast this morning, but I didn't.

Exercise 40.4: Complete the sentences. Write about yourself and other people

  • 1. I didn't go out last night, but my friends did.
  • 2. I like sport, but my sister doesn't.
  • 3. I don't eat meat, but Jenny does.
  • 4. I'm from the UK, but my husband isn't.
  • 5. I haven't been to Canada, but Jenny has.

Exercise 40.5: Put in a verb, positive or negative

  • 1. 'Are you tired?' 'I was earlier, but I'm not now.'
  • 2. Steve is happy today, but he wasn't yesterday.
  • 3. The post office isn't open yet, but the shops are.
  • 4. I haven't got a telescope, but I know somebody who has.
  • 5. I would like to help you, but I am afraid I can't.
  • 6. I don't usually go to work by car, but I did yesterday.
  • 7. A: Have you ever been to the United States? B: No, but Sandra has. She went there on holiday last year.
  • 8. 'Do you and Chris watch TV a lot?' 'I do, but Chris doesn't.'
  • 9. 'I've been invited to Sam's wedding, but Kate hasn't.'
  • 10. 'Do you think Sara will pass her driving test?' 'Yes, I'm sure she will.'
  • 11. 'Are you going out tonight?' 'I might. I don't know for sure.'

Exercise 40.6: Answer these questions about yourself. Use Yes, I have. / No, I'm not. etc.

  • 1. Are you American? No, I'm not.
  • 2. Have you got a car? Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.
  • 3. Do you feel OK? Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
  • 4. Is it snowing? Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.
  • 5. Are you hungry? Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
  • 6. Do you like classical music? Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
  • 7. Will you be in Paris tomorrow? Yes, I will. / No, I won't.
  • 8. Have you ever broken your arm? Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.
  • 9. Did you buy anything yesterday? Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.
  • 10. Were you asleep at 3:00 a.m.? Yes, I was. / No, I wasn't.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to complete sentences using appropriate verbs, negations, and auxiliary verbs. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance our communication skills.
