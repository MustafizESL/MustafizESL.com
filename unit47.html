Unit 47: Asking Questions with "What," "Which," "Who," and "How"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to form questions using "what," "which," "who," and "how" to inquire about different types of information.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "what," "which," "who," and "how" to ask about specific details, choices, and descriptions.

Explanation

Questions with "What": Use "what" to ask about general information or details.

  • Example: I've got a new laptop. What brand is it?

Questions with "Which": Use "which" to ask about a choice from a known set of options.

  • Example: You can choose between tea and coffee. Which do you prefer?

Questions with "Who": Use "who" to ask about a person.

  • Example: Someone left a message. Who left the message?

Questions with "How": Use "how" to ask about the manner, condition, or degree of something.

  • Example: Are you 1 meter 75? How tall are you?

Examples

Questions with "What":

  • I like reading books. What genre do you enjoy?
  • I've got a new phone. What model is it?

Questions with "Which":

  • Which route shall we take?
  • Which dessert do you want to try?

Questions with "Who":

  • Who is responsible for this task?
  • Who is the author of this book?

Questions with "How":

  • How old is your sister?
  • How far is it to the nearest park?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 47.1: Write questions with "what"

  • 1. I've got a new bicycle. (brand?) What brand is it?
  • 2. I need a job. (type?) What type of job are you looking for?
  • 3. I've bought a new coat. (color?) What color is it?
  • 4. I got up early today. (time?) What time did you get up?
  • 5. I enjoy movies. (genre?) What genre of movies do you like?
  • 6. I'm planning to buy a computer. (model?) What model are you considering?

Exercise 47.2: Complete the questions. Use "which..."

  • 1. Which direction should we head?
  • 2. Which bag is yours?
  • 3. Which movie are we going to watch?
  • 4. Which train goes to the downtown area?

Exercise 47.3: Write "what/which/who"

  • 1. What is that person’s name?
  • 2. Which path should we follow? Left or right?
  • 3. You can have juice or soda. Which one do you want?
  • 4. What date is it today? 'Monday.'
  • 5. This office is nice. Which desk is yours?
  • 6. What is your favorite hobby?
  • 7. Which costs more, beef or chicken?
  • 8. Who is taller, Jack or Mike?
  • 9. What type of phone do you have?
  • 10. A: I have several watches. B: Which watch do you wear most often?
  • 11. What is your nationality?

Exercise 47.4: Complete the questions with "How + adjective or adverb" (high/long etc.)

  • 1. How high is the Burj Khalifa? About 828 meters.
  • 2. How far is it to the airport? Approximately 10 kilometers from here.
  • 3. How old is your cat? She’s 3 years old.
  • 4. How often do the trains run? Every 15 minutes.
  • 5. How deep is the lake? 30 meters.
  • 6. How long have you been working here? Almost five years.

Exercise 47.5: Write questions with "How...?"

  • 1. Are you 1 meter 65? 1.75? How tall are you?
  • 2. Is this package one kilogram? Two? How heavy is this package?
  • 3. Are you 25 years old? 28? How old are you?
  • 4. Did you spend $40? $60? How much did you spend?
  • 5. Do you exercise daily? Weekly? How often do you exercise?
  • 6. Is it 800 miles from London to Berlin? 1000? How far is it from London to Berlin?

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to ask questions using "what," "which," "who," and "how" to inquire about different types of information. We practiced forming questions using these structures to enhance communication skills.
