Unit 18: Expressing Duration: How long have you ... ?

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use the present perfect tense to describe actions or situations that started in the past and continue in the present. They will also learn to compare the present simple, present continuous, present perfect, and present perfect continuous tenses.

Overview: This unit focuses on expressing duration using the present perfect tense with the phrase "How long have you ...?" and comparing it with other tenses.

Explanation

Present Perfect Tense: Use "has" or "have" followed by the past participle of the verb.

  • Example: I have worked here for three years. / She has studied at this school since 2015.

Present Simple vs. Present Perfect:

  • Present Simple: Describes a current state or habitual action.
  • Example: Mary lives in New York.
  • Present Perfect: Describes a state that started in the past and continues in the present.
  • Example: She has lived in New York for five years.

Present Continuous vs. Present Perfect Continuous:

  • Present Continuous: Describes an action happening right now.
  • Example: They are playing soccer.
  • Present Perfect Continuous: Describes an action that started in the past and is still happening now.
  • Example: They have been playing soccer for an hour.

Examples

Present Perfect:

  • I have known her for a long time.
  • They have been friends since childhood.
  • She has lived in this city for five years.

Present Simple:

  • I know her well.
  • They are friends.
  • She lives in this city.

Present Continuous:

  • I am learning Spanish.
  • It is snowing now.
  • They are watching television.

Present Perfect Continuous:

  • I have been learning Spanish for six months.
  • It has been snowing all day.
  • They have been watching television for an hour.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 18.1: Complete these sentences:

  • 1. Anna is in France. She has been in France since Monday.
  • 2. I know Robert. I have known him for ten years.
  • 3. Emma and Luke are married. They have been married since 2010.
  • 4. Brian is unwell. He has been unwell for the past few days.
  • 5. We live on Elm Street. We have lived/have been living there for a long time.
  • 6. Catherine works in a bank. She has worked/has been working there for seven years.
  • 7. Alan has a headache. He has had a headache since this morning.
  • 8. I'm learning German. I have been learning German for six months.

Exercise 18.2: Make questions with "How long...?":

  • 1. Jane is on holiday. How long has she been on holiday?
  • 2. Scott and Julia are in Italy. How long have they been there?
  • 3. I know Emma. How long have you known her? or... known Emma?
  • 4. Diana is learning Spanish. How long has she been learning Spanish?
  • 5. My sister lives in New Zealand. How long has she lived in New Zealand? How long has she been living...?
  • 6. I'm a nurse. How long have you been a nurse?
  • 7. It is snowing. How long has it been snowing?

Exercise 18.3: Complete the sentences with: for ten minutes - all day - all her life - for seven years - since he was 22 - since Tuesday:

  • 1. They have been married for seven years.
  • 2. She has lived in Wales all her life.
  • 3. They have been on vacation since Tuesday.
  • 4. The sun has been shining all day.
  • 5. She has been waiting for ten minutes.
  • 6. He has had a beard since he was 22.

Exercise 18.4: Which is right?

  • 1. Anna has lived in Australia since March.
  • 2. John and I are colleagues. I know him very well.
  • 3. John and I are colleagues. I've known him for a long time.
  • 4. A: Sorry I'm late. How long have you been waiting? B: Not long. Only ten minutes.
  • 5. Martin works in a cafe now. He enjoys his job a lot.
  • 6. Ruth is reading a novel. She has been reading it for two hours.
  • 7. 'How long have you lived in this apartment?' 'About five years.'
  • 8. 'Is that a new bag?' 'No, I've had this bag for a long time.'
  • 9. Tom is in Japan at the moment. He has been there for the last three days.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use the present perfect tense to describe actions or situations that started in the past and continue in the present. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in the present perfect tense.
