Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to form various types of questions.

Overview: This unit focuses on forming questions using different structures and auxiliary verbs.

Explanation

Yes/No Questions: Use auxiliary verbs like "do," "does," "did," etc., to form yes/no questions.

  • Example: I can swim. Can you swim?

Information Questions: Use question words like "where," "what," "why," etc., to ask for specific information.

  • Example: I want to go out. Where do you want to go?

Examples

Yes/No Questions:

  • I work hard. (and Jack?) Does Jack work hard?
  • I like my job. (and you?) Do you like your job?

Information Questions:

  • I want to go out. Where do you want to go?
  • I'm reading. What are you reading?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 44.1: Write questions

  • 1. I can swim. (and you?) Can you swim?
  • 2. I work hard. (and Jack?) Does Jack work hard?
  • 3. I was late this morning. (and you?) Were you late this morning?
  • 4. I've got a key. (and Kate?) Has Kate got a key?
  • 5. I'll be here tomorrow. (and you?) Will you be here tomorrow?
  • 6. I'm going out this evening. (and Paul?) Is Paul going out this evening?
  • 7. I like my job. (and you?) Do you like your job?
  • 8. I live near here. (and Nicole?) Does Nicole live near here?
  • 9. I enjoyed the film. (and you?) Did you enjoy the film?
  • 10. I had a good holiday. (and you?) Did you have a good holiday?

Exercise 44.2: You are talking to a friend about driving. Write the full questions

  • 1. (have / a car?) Have you got a car? Yes, I have.
  • 2. (use / a lot?) Do you use it a lot? Yes, nearly every day.
  • 3. (use / yesterday?) Did you use it yesterday? Yes, to go to work.
  • 4. (enjoy driving?) Do you enjoy driving? Not very much.
  • 5. (a good driver?) Are you a good driver? I think I am.
  • 6. (ever / have / an accident?) Have you ever had an accident? No, never.

Exercise 44.3: Make questions with these words. Put the words in the right order

  • 1. (has / gone / where / David?) Where has David gone?
  • 2. (working / Rachel / is / today?) Is Rachel working today?
  • 3. (the children / what / are / doing?) What are the children doing?
  • 4. (made / is / how / cheese?) How is cheese made?
  • 5. (to the party / coming / is / your sister?) Is your sister coming to the party?
  • 6. (you / the truth / tell / don't / why?) Why don't you tell the truth?
  • 7. (your guests / have / yet / arrived?) Have your guests arrived yet?
  • 8. (leave / what time / your train / does?) What time does your train leave?
  • 9. (to work / Emily / why go / didn't?) Why didn't Emily go to work?
  • 10. (your car / in the accident / was / damaged?) Was your car damaged in the accident?

Exercise 44.4: Complete the questions

  • 1. I want to go out. Where do you want to go?
  • 2. Kate and Paul aren't going to the party. Why aren't they going?
  • 3. I'm reading. What are you reading?
  • 4. Sue went to bed early. What time did she go (to bed)?
  • 5. My parents are going on holiday. When are they going (on holiday)?
  • 6. I saw Tom a few days ago. Where did you see him?
  • 7. I can't come to the party. Why can't you come (to the party)?
  • 8. Tina has gone away. Where has she gone?
  • 9. I need some money. How much (money) do you need?
  • 10. Angela doesn't like me. Why doesn't she like you?
  • 11. It rains sometimes. How often does it rain?
  • 12. I did the shopping. When did you do it? / When did you do the shopping?

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to form various types of questions. We practiced forming questions using these structures to enhance communication skills.
