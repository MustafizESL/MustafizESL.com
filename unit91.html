Unit 91: Using "Enough"

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "enough" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "enough" to indicate sufficiency in quantity or degree and how to form sentences using "enough."

Explanation

Using "Enough": "Enough" is used to indicate that there is as much or as many as needed. It can be used with nouns, adjectives, or adverbs.

  • Example: She hasn't got enough money.

Examples

Using "Enough" with Nouns:

  • There aren't enough chairs.
  • She hasn't got enough paint.

Using "Enough" with Adjectives:

  • He isn’t tall enough.
  • The car isn’t big enough.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 91.1: Look at the pictures and complete the sentences. Use "enough" + these words: chairs - money - paint - wind

  • 1. She hasn't got enough money.
  • 2. There aren't enough chairs.
  • 3. She hasn't got enough paint.
  • 4. There isn't enough wind.

Exercise 91.2: Look at the pictures and complete the sentences. Use these adjectives + "enough": big - long - strong - tall

  • 1. He isn’t tall enough.
  • 2. The car isn’t big enough.
  • 3. His legs aren't long enough.
  • 4. He isn’t strong enough.

Exercise 91.3: Complete the sentences. Use "enough" with these words: big - eat - loud - milk - old - practise - space - time - tired

  • 1. 'Is there enough milk in your coffee?' 'Yes, thank you.'
  • 2. Can you hear the radio? Is it loud enough for you?
  • 3. He can leave school if he wants – he's old enough.
  • 4. When I visited New York last year, I didn't have enough time to see all the things I wanted to see.
  • 5. This house isn't big enough for a large family.
  • 6. Tina is very thin. She doesn't eat enough.
  • 7. My office is very small. There isn't enough space.
  • 8. It's late, but I don't want to go to bed now. I'm not tired enough.
  • 9. Lisa isn't a very good tennis player because she doesn't practise enough.

Exercise 91.4: Complete the sentences. Use "enough" with these words

  • 1. We haven't got enough money to buy a new car. (money/buy)
  • 2. This knife isn't sharp enough to cut tomatoes. (sharp/cut)
  • 3. The water wasn't warm enough to go swimming. (warm/go)
  • 4. Have we got enough bread to make sandwiches? (bread/make)
  • 5. We played well, but not well enough to win the game. (well/win)
  • 6. I don't have enough time to read newspapers. (time/read)

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "enough" to indicate sufficiency in quantity or degree. "Enough" can be used with nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
