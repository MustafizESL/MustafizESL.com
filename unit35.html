Unit 35: Commands, Offers, and Suggestions

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use commands, offers, and suggestions in different contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using various structures to give commands, make offers, and give suggestions.

Explanation

Commands: Use imperative verbs to give commands or instructions.

  • Example: Come in! / Don't drink the water!

Offers: Use "would you like" to offer something to someone.

  • Example: Would you like a cup of tea?

Suggestions: Use "let's" to make suggestions.

  • Example: Let's go for a swim.

Examples

Commands:

  • Be quiet!
  • Don't touch that!

Offers:

  • Would you like a slice of cake?
  • Would you like some coffee?

Suggestions:

  • Let's take a break.
  • Let's watch a movie.

Exercise 35.2: Complete the sentences. Use "let's" with these suggestions: go for a swim - go to a restaurant - take a taxi - wait a little - watch TV

  • 1. Do you want to play tennis? No, let's go for a swim.
  • 2. Do you want to walk home? No, let's take a taxi.
  • 3. Shall I put a CD on? No, let's watch TV.
  • 4. Shall we have dinner at home? No, let's go to a restaurant.
  • 5. Would you like to go now? No, let's wait a little.

  • 4. Do you want me to close the window? No, don't close the window.
  • 5. Shall I call you tonight? No, don't call me tonight.
  • 6. Do you think we should wait for Andy? No, let's not wait for Andy. / No, don't let's wait for Andy.

  • 8. Shall we go by bus? No, let's not go by bus. / No, don't let's go by bus.
