Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use the present simple tense to describe habitual actions and general truths.

Overview: This unit covers the formation and usage of the present simple tense.

Explanation

Forming the Present Simple: The present simple tense is formed using the base form of the verb for all subjects except for the third person singular (he, she, it), which adds an -s or -es to the base form.

  • I/You/We/They work
  • He/She/It works

Examples

Positive Statements:

  • I play tennis.
  • You read books.
  • He/She/It watches TV.
  • We enjoy music.
  • They travel often.

Negative Statements:

  • I do not (don't) like coffee.
  • You do not (don't) work here.
  • He/She/It does not (doesn't) go to school.
  • We do not (don't) play football.
  • They do not (don't) watch movies.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 5.1: Write the correct form of the verb for the third person singular:

  • 1. (study) She ___ every night. (studies)
  • 2. (fix) He ___ cars. (fixes)
  • 3. (carry) She always ___ a bag. (carries)
  • 4. (kiss) He ___ his kids goodnight. (kisses)
  • 5. (teach) She ___ mathematics. (teaches)
  • 6. (enjoy) He ___ fishing. (enjoys)

Exercise 5.2: Fill in the blanks with the correct form of the verb:

  • 1. I ___ (love) to read books.
  • 2. They ___ (visit) their grandparents every weekend.
  • 3. She ___ (cook) dinner every evening.
  • 4. He ___ (play) basketball on Fridays.
  • 5. We ___ (attend) meetings every Monday.
  • 6. You ___ (write) emails every day.

Exercise 5.3: Change these sentences to negative statements:

  • 1. I go to the gym every day. → I do not (don't) go to the gym every day.
  • 2. She sings beautifully. → She does not (doesn't) sing beautifully.
  • 3. They travel a lot. → They do not (don't) travel a lot.
  • 4. He knows the answer. → He does not (doesn't) know the answer.
  • 5. We eat out often. → We do not (don't) eat out often.

Exercise 5.4: Write questions using the present simple tense:

  • 1. (you / like / ice cream) ___ Do you like ice cream?
  • 2. (he / play / football) ___ Does he play football?
  • 3. (they / work / in an office) ___ Do they work in an office?
  • 4. (she / watch / TV) ___ Does she watch TV?
  • 5. (we / need / more time) ___ Do we need more time?
  • 6. (you / go / to the gym) ___ Do you go to the gym?

Exercise 5.5: Write sentences about your daily routine using the present simple tense:

  • 1. (wake up) ___ I wake up at 7 AM.
  • 2. (have breakfast) ___ I have breakfast at 8 AM.
  • 3. (go to work) ___ I go to work at 9 AM.
  • 4. (have lunch) ___ I have lunch at 12 PM.
  • 5. (finish work) ___ I finish work at 5 PM.
  • 6. (dinner) ___ I have dinner at 7 PM.
  • 7. (go to bed) ___ I go to bed at 10 PM.

Summary

Review: Summarize the key points learned in the unit.

  • The present simple tense is used to describe habitual actions and general truths.
  • Positive and negative forms of the present simple tense.
