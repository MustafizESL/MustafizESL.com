Unit 13: Past Continuous: was/were -ing

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use the past continuous tense to describe actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past.

Overview: This unit focuses on the formation and usage of the past continuous tense.

Explanation

Forming the Past Continuous: Use "was" or "were" followed by the -ing form of the verb.

  • Example: I was watching TV. / They were playing soccer.

Examples

Positive Statements:

  • I was reading a book.
  • She was cooking dinner.
  • They were playing basketball.

Negative Statements:

  • I was not (wasn't) reading a book.
  • She was not (wasn't) cooking dinner.
  • They were not (weren't) playing basketball.

Questions:

  • Were you reading a book?
  • Was she cooking dinner?
  • Were they playing basketball?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 13.1: Write two sentences for each scenario:

  • 1. David was at the gym. He was lifting weights.
  • 2. Emma and Lisa were at the park. They were having a picnic.
  • 3. Alex was in the library. He was studying for his exams.
  • 4. Anna was in her garden. She was planting flowers.
  • 5. And you? I was at home. I was reading a novel.

Exercise 13.2: Tom did a lot of things yesterday morning. Complete the sentences:

  • 1. At 7:00 AM he was jogging in the park.
  • 2. At 9:00 AM he was having breakfast.
  • 3. At 10:00 AM he was talking on the phone.
  • 4. At 11:00 AM he was working on his computer.
  • 5. At 12:00 PM he was eating lunch.
  • 6. At 1:00 PM he was shopping for groceries.

Exercise 13.3: Complete the questions. Use was/were-ing. Use what/where/why if necessary.

  • 1. (you/visit) Where were you visiting in 2005? In Spain.
  • 2. (you/eat) What were you eating at noon? I was eating a sandwich.
  • 3. (it/snow) Was it snowing when you left the house? Yes, it was.
  • 4. (Sarah/run) Why was she running so fast? Because she was in a hurry.
  • 5. (Mark/wear) Was Mark wearing glasses yesterday? Yes, he was.

Exercise 13.4: You saw Sam in the park yesterday afternoon. What was he doing? Write positive or negative sentences:

  • 1. He was wearing sunglasses.
  • 2. He wasn’t reading a book.
  • 3. He was playing with a dog.
  • 4. He wasn’t talking on the phone.
  • 5. He was drinking water.
  • 6. He wasn’t riding a skateboard.
  • 7. He was listening to music.
  • 8. He wasn’t eating an apple.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use the past continuous tense to describe actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in the past continuous tense.
