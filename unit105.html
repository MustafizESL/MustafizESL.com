Unit 105: Using "Before," "After," "During," and "While"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to correctly use "before," "after," "during," and "while" to describe the timing of actions and events.

Overview: This unit focuses on using these prepositions and conjunctions to indicate the timing and sequence of actions and events, making sentences clearer and more logical.

Explanation

Using "Before": Use "before" to indicate that one action or event happens earlier than another.

  • Example: Everybody was nervous before the exam.

Using "After": Use "after" to indicate that one action or event happens later than another.

  • Example: I usually work four hours in the morning, and another three hours after lunch.

Using "During": Use "during" to indicate that something happens within a specific period of time.

  • Example: Anna went to evening classes to learn German. She learnt a lot during the course.

Using "While": Use "while" to indicate that two actions or events happen at the same time.

  • Example: Would you like to sit down while you are waiting?

Examples

Using "Before" and "After":

  • The film was really boring. We left before the end.
  • My aunt and uncle lived in London before they went to Australia.
  • I felt sick after eating too much chocolate.

Using "During":

  • Anna went to evening classes to learn German. She learnt a lot during the course.
  • Somebody broke a window during the night. Did you hear anything?

Using "While":

  • Would you like to sit down while you are waiting?
  • Gary called while you were out.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 105.1: Complete the sentences. Choose from the boxes.

  • 1. Everybody was nervous before the exam.
  • 2. I usually work four hours in the morning, and another three hours after lunch.
  • 3. The film was really boring. We left before the end.
  • 4. Anna went to evening classes to learn German. She learnt a lot during the course.
  • 5. My aunt and uncle lived in London before they went to Australia.
  • 6. Somebody broke a window during the night. Did you hear anything?
  • 7. Would you like to sit down while you are waiting?
  • 8. 'Are you going home after the concert?' 'Yes, I have to get up early tomorrow.'

Exercise 105.2: Write "during," "while," or "for."

  • 1. We didn't speak while we were eating.
  • 2. We didn't speak during the meal.
  • 3. Gary called while you were out.
  • 4. I stayed in Rome for five days.
  • 5. Sally didn't read any newspapers while she was on holiday.
  • 6. The students looked very bored during the lesson.
  • 7. I fell out of bed while I was asleep.
  • 8. Last night I watched TV for three hours.
  • 9. I don't usually watch TV during the day.
  • 10. Do you ever watch TV while you are having dinner?

Exercise 105.3: Complete the sentences. Use -ing (doing, having, etc.).

  • 1. After doing the shopping, they went home.
  • 2. I felt sick after eating too much chocolate.
  • 3. I'm going to ask you a question. Think carefully before answering it.
  • 4. I felt awful when I got up this morning. I felt better after having/taking a shower.
  • 5. After finishing/doing my work, I left the office and went home.
  • 6. Before going/travelling to a foreign country, you should try and learn a little of the language.

Exercise 105.4: Write sentences with "before + -ing" and "after + -ing."

  • 1. After doing the shopping, they went home.
  • 2. John worked in a bookshop for two years after leaving school.
  • 3. Before going to sleep, I read for a few minutes.
  • 4. After walking for three hours, we were very tired.
  • 5. Let's have a cup of coffee before going out.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "before" to indicate that one action or event happens earlier than another, "after" to indicate that one action or event happens later than another, "during" to indicate that something happens within a specific period of time, and "while" to indicate that two actions or events happen at the same time. We practiced forming sentences using these prepositions and conjunctions to enhance clarity and logic in writing.
