Unit 85: Adjectives and Nouns

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use adjectives and nouns correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using adjectives to describe nouns and the proper order of words in sentences.

Explanation

Adjectives: Adjectives describe or modify nouns.

  • Example: green jacket, sharp knife.

Nouns: Nouns are the names of people, places, things, or ideas.

  • Example: house, languages.

Word Order: The typical word order in English is subject + verb + object. Adjectives usually come before nouns.

  • Example: They live in a new house.

Examples

Using Adjectives and Nouns:

  • I like that green jacket.
  • Fire-fighting is a dangerous job.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 85.1: Put the words in the right order

  • 1. They live in a new house.
  • 2. I like that green jacket.
  • 3. Do you like classical music?
  • 4. I had a wonderful holiday.
  • 5. We went to a Japanese restaurant.

Exercise 85.2: The words in the box are adjectives (black/foreign etc.) or nouns (air/job etc.). Use an adjective and a noun to complete each sentence

  • Box: air - clouds - foreign - holiday - job - languages - sharp - black - dangerous - fresh - hot - knife - long - water
  • 1. Do you speak any foreign languages?
  • 2. Look at those black clouds. It's going to rain.
  • 3. Sue works very hard, and she's very tired. She needs a long holiday.
  • 4. I would like to have a shower, but there's no hot water.
  • 5. Can you open the window? We need some fresh air.
  • 6. I need a sharp knife to cut these onions.
  • 7. Fire-fighting is a dangerous job.

Exercise 85.3: Write sentences for the pictures. Choose from the boxes

  • Box-1: feel(s) - look(s) - sound(s) - look(s) - (smell) - taste(s)
  • Box-2: happy - ill - nice - horrible - new - surprised
  • 1. You sound happy.
  • 2. It looks new.
  • 3. I feel ill.
  • 4. You look surprised.
  • 5. They smell nice.
  • 6. It tastes horrible.

Exercise 85.4: A and B don't agree. Complete B's sentences. Use feel/look etc.

  • 1. You look tired. Do I? I don't feel tired. (feel)
  • 2. This is a new coat. Is it? It doesn't look new. (look)
  • 3. I'm American. Are you? You don't sound American. (sound)
  • 4. You look cold. Do I? I don’t feel cold. (feel)
  • 5. These bags are heavy. Are they? They don’t look heavy. (look)
  • 6. That soup looks good. Maybe, but it doesn’t taste good. (taste)

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use adjectives to describe or modify nouns, nouns as the names of people, places, things, or ideas, and the typical word order in English (subject + verb + object) with adjectives usually coming before nouns. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
