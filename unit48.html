Unit 48: Asking Questions with "How long" and Writing Sentences with "It took"

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to ask questions with "How long" and write sentences with "It took" to describe the duration of actions.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "How long" to inquire about the time required for different activities and using "It took" to describe the time taken for various actions.

Explanation

Questions with "How long": Use "How long" to ask about the duration of an action or event.

  • Example: How long does it take by plane from London to Amsterdam?

Sentences with "It took": Use "It took" to describe the time taken to complete an action or event.

  • Example: It took me three days to read the book.

Examples

Questions with "How long":

  • How long does it take by bus from the city center to the airport?
  • How long does it take by train from Paris to Berlin?

Sentences with "It took":

  • It took us 20 minutes to walk home.
  • It took me six months to learn to drive.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 48.1: Look at the pictures and write questions with "How long"

  • 1. How long does it take by plane from London to Amsterdam?
  • 2. How long does it take by car from Milan to Rome?
  • 3. How long does it take by train from Paris to Berlin?
  • 4. How long does it take by bus from the city center to the airport?

Exercise 48.2: How long does it take to do these things? Write full sentences

  • 1. Fly from your city/country to London:
    • It takes two hours to fly from Madrid to London.

    Example answers:

  • 2. Fly from your city/country to New York:
    • It takes eight hours to fly from London to New York.
  • 3. Study to be a doctor in your country:
    • It takes six years to study to be a doctor in Japan.
  • 4. Walk from your home to the nearest shop:
    • It takes 10 minutes to walk from my home to the nearest shop.
  • 5. Get from your home to the nearest airport:
    • It takes 45 minutes to get from my home to the nearest airport.

Exercise 48.3: Write questions with "How long did it take..."

  • 1. (Jane found a job.) How long did it take her to find a job?
  • 2. (I walked to the station.) How long did it take you to walk to the station?
  • 3. (Tom painted the bathroom.) How long did it take him to paint the bathroom?
  • 4. (I learned to ski.) How long did it take you to learn to ski?
  • 5. (They repaired the computer.) How long did it take them to repair the computer?

Exercise 48.4: Read the situations and write sentences with "It took"

  • 1. I read a book last week. I started reading it on Monday. I finished it three days later.
    • It took me three days to read the book.
  • 2. We walked home last night. We left at 10 o'clock and we arrived home at 10.20.
    • It took us 20 minutes to walk home.
  • 3. I learned to drive last year. I had my first driving lesson in January. I passed my driving test six months later.
    • It took me six months to learn to drive.
  • 4. Mark drove to London yesterday. He left home at 7 o'clock and got to London at 10.
    • It took Mark three hours to drive to London.
  • 5. Lisa began looking for a job a long time ago. She got a job last week.
    • It took Lisa several months to find a job.
  • 6. Write a sentence about yourself:
    • It took me two hours to complete my homework.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to ask questions with "How long" and write sentences with "It took" to describe the duration of actions. We practiced forming questions and sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
