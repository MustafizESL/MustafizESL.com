Unit 84: Using "A Little" and "A Few"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "a little" and "a few" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "a little" and "a few" to refer to small quantities, and the difference between the two.

Explanation

Using "A Little": Use "a little" with uncountable nouns to refer to a small quantity.

  • Example: Can I have a little milk in my coffee?

Using "A Few": Use "a few" with countable nouns to refer to a small number.

  • Example: I need a few minutes to finish this.

Examples

Using "A Little" and "A Few":

  • Can I have a little milk in my coffee?
  • I need a few minutes to finish this.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 84.1: Answer the questions with "a little" or "a few"

  • 1. 'Have you got any money?' 'Yes, a little.'
  • 2. 'Have you got any envelopes?' 'Yes, a few.'
  • 3. 'Do you want sugar in your coffee?' 'Yes, a little.'
  • 4. 'Did you take any photographs when you were on holiday?' 'Yes, a few.'
  • 5. 'Does your friend speak English?' 'Yes, a little.'
  • 6. 'Are there any good restaurants in this town?' 'Yes, a few.'

Exercise 84.2: Write "a little" or "a few" + these words: chairs - days - fresh air - friend - milk - Russian - times - years

  • 1. Martin speaks Italian well. He lived in Italy for a few years.
  • 2. Can I have a little milk in my coffee, please?
  • 3. 'When did Julia go away?' 'A few days ago.'
  • 4. 'Do you speak any foreign languages?' 'I can speak a little Russian.'
  • 5. 'Are you going out alone?' 'No, I'm going with a few friends.'
  • 6. 'Have you ever been to Mexico?' 'Yes, a few times.'
  • 7. There wasn't much furniture in the room - just a table and a few chairs.
  • 8. I'm going out for a walk. I need a little fresh air.

Exercise 84.3: Complete the sentences. Use "very little" or "very few" + these words: coffee - hotels - mistakes - people - rain - time - work

  • 1. Your English is very good. You make very few mistakes.
  • 2. I drink very little coffee. I don't like it.
  • 3. The weather here is very dry in summer. There is very little rain.
  • 4. It's difficult to find a place to stay in this town. There are very few hotels.
  • 5. Hurry up. We've got very little time.
  • 6. The town is very quiet at night. Very few people go out.
  • 7. Some people in the office are very lazy. They do very little work.

Exercise 84.4: Write "little" / "a little" or "few" / "a few"

  • 1. There was little food in the fridge. It was nearly empty.
  • 2. 'When did Sarah go out?' 'A few minutes ago.'
  • 3. I can't decide now. I need a little time to think about it.
  • 4. There was little traffic, so we arrived earlier than we expected.
  • 5. The bus service isn't very good at night - there are few buses after 9 o'clock.
  • 6. 'Would you like some soup?' 'Yes, a little, please.'
  • 7. I'd like to practise my English more, but I have little opportunity.

Exercise 84.5: Right or wrong. Change the sentences where necessary. Write "OK" if the sentence is correct

  • 1. We're going away for a few days next week. (OK)
  • 2. Everybody needs a little luck. (OK)
  • 3. I can't talk to you now - I've got a few things to do. (OK)
  • 4. I eat very little meat - I don't like it very much. (OK)
  • 5. Excuse me, can I ask you a few questions? (Corrected: questions instead of question)
  • 6. There were few people on the bus - it was nearly empty. (OK)
  • 7. Martin is a very private person. Few people know him well. (OK)

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "a little" with uncountable nouns to refer to a small quantity, and "a few" with countable nouns to refer to a small number. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
