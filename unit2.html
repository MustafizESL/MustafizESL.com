Unit 2: Questions with am/is/are

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to form and answer questions using the verbs am, is, and are.

Overview: This unit introduces the formation of questions with the verb "to be" (am, is, are) and provides practice in forming and answering these questions.

Explanation

Forming Questions: The verb "to be" is placed at the beginning of the sentence to form a question.

  • Am I...?
  • Are you...?
  • Is he/she/it...?
  • Are we...?
  • Are they...?

Examples

Questions and Answers:

  • Where is the book? It’s on the table.
  • Is your jacket red? No, it’s blue.
  • Is Michael from Paris? No, he’s from Berlin.
  • Am I early? Yes, you are.
  • Where is Anna from? She’s from New York.
  • What color is your car? It’s white.
  • Are you tired? No, I’m not.
  • How is Jane? She’s fine.
  • Who is that man? He’s my uncle.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 2.1: Match the questions with the answers:

  • 1. Where is the phone? ___ In your pocket.
  • 2. Is your bike new? ___ No, it’s old.
  • 3. Is Sarah from Canada? ___ No, she’s from Australia.
  • 4. Am I correct? ___ Yes, you are.
  • 5. Where is John? ___ At the office.
  • 6. What color is the sky? ___ Blue.
  • 7. Are you tired? ___ No, I’m not.
  • 8. How is Robert? ___ Very well.
  • 9. Who is that man? ___ My brother.

Exercise 2.2: Form questions using the given words:

  • (is / your / friend / here) ___ Is your friend here?
  • (are / you / tired) ___ Are you tired?
  • (is / it / raining) ___ Is it raining?
  • (are / the stores / open / today) ___ Are the stores open today?
  • (where / you / from / are) ___ Where are you from?
  • (are / you / interested / in music) ___ Are you interested in music?
  • (is / near / the school / the library) ___ Is the library near the school?
  • (are / your / children / at home) ___ Are your children at home?
  • (why / you / late / are) ___ Why are you late?

Exercise 2.3: Complete the sentences using What, Who, Where, or How:

  • 1. ___ How are your friends? They’re doing great.
  • 2. ___ Where is the bus stop? At the corner.
  • 3. ___ How old is your cat? Two years old.
  • 4. ___ How much is this shirt? $20.
  • 5. ___ What is your favorite hobby? Painting.
  • 6. ___ Who is that woman? She’s my teacher.
  • 7. ___ What color is your car? Green.

Exercise 2.4: Write the questions:

  • (name?) ___ What’s your name? ___ David.
  • (Canadian?) ___ Are you Canadian? ___ No, I’m American.
  • (how old?) ___ How old are you? ___ I’m 25.
  • (a student?) ___ Are you a student? ___ Yes, I am.
  • (married?) ___ Are you married? ___ No, I’m single.
  • (brother a doctor?) ___ Is your brother a doctor? ___ No, he’s an engineer.
  • (from?) ___ Where is he from? ___ He’s from Japan.
  • (his name?) ___ What’s his name? ___ Hiroshi.
  • (How old?) ___ How old is he? ___ He’s 30.

Exercise 2.5: Write short answers (Yes, I am. / No, he isn’t. etc.):

  • 1. Are you happy? ___ Yes, I am.
  • 2. Are you cold? ___ No, I’m not.
  • 3. Is it warm today? ___ Yes, it is.
  • 4. Are your shoes new? ___ No, they aren’t.
  • 5. Is it late now? ___ Yes, it is.
  • 6. Are you a doctor? ___ Yes, I am.

Summary

Review: Summarize the key points learned in the unit.

  • Forming questions with "am/is/are" by placing the verb at the beginning.
  • Answering questions with "am/is/are" in positive and negative forms.
