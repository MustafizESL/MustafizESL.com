Unit 112: Using Prepositions to Describe Feelings and Relationships

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to correctly use prepositions like "of," "with," "in," etc., to describe feelings and relationships.

Overview: This unit focuses on using these prepositions to express emotions, relationships, and states of being, making sentences clearer and more descriptive.

Explanation

Using Prepositions to Describe Feelings and Relationships: Prepositions like "of," "with," "in," "to," "for," "about," etc., are used to describe feelings, relationships, and states of being.

  • Example: He's afraid of dogs.

Examples

Using Prepositions in Sentences:

  • He's afraid of dogs.
  • She's interested in science.
  • She's married to a footballer.
  • She's very good at languages.
  • He's fed-up with the weather.
  • A: Can I help you? B: Thanks, that's very kind of you.

Using Prepositions to Complete Sentences:

  • I'm not interested in sport.
  • I'm not very good at sport.
  • I like Sarah. She's always very kind to me.
  • I'm sorry about your broken window. It was an accident.
  • He's very brave. He isn't afraid of anything.
  • It was very nice of Jane to let us stay in her apartment.
  • Life today is very different from/to life 50 years ago.
  • Are you interested in politics?
  • I feel sorry for her, but I can't help her.
  • Chris was angry about me for what happened.
  • These boxes are very heavy. They are full of books.
  • I'm sorry for getting angry with you yesterday.

Using Prepositions with Verbs:

  • I'm not very good at telling stories. (good/tell)
  • I wanted to go to the cinema, but Paula wasn't interested in going. (interested/go)
  • Sue isn't very good at getting up in the morning. (good/get)
  • Let's go! I'm fed up with waiting. (fed up/wait)
  • I'm sorry for/about waking you up in the middle of the night. (sorry/wake)
  • Sorry I'm late! Thank you for waiting. (thank you/wait)

Using "Without -ing":

  • Tom left without saying goodbye.
  • Sue walked past me without speaking.
  • Don't do anything without asking me first.
  • I went out without locking the door.

Using Prepositions to Describe Yourself:

  • I'm interested in sport.
  • I'm afraid of the dark.
  • I'm not very good at drawing.
  • I'm not interested in cars.
  • I'm fed up with living here.
