Unit 12: Negative Forms and Questions: I didn’t ... Did you ... ?

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to form negative sentences and questions in the past simple tense.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "didn't" for negative sentences and "did" for questions in the past simple tense.

Explanation

Forming Negative Sentences: Use "did not" or "didn't" followed by the base form of the verb.

  • Example: I did not (didn't) go to the party.

Forming Questions: Use "did" followed by the subject and the base form of the verb.

  • Example: Did you go to the party?

Examples

Negative Sentences:

  • I didn't see the movie.
  • She didn't finish her homework.
  • They didn't go to the concert.

Questions:

  • Did you see the movie?
  • Did she finish her homework?
  • Did they go to the concert?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 12.1: Complete these sentences with the verb in the negative:

  • 1. I called Mark, but I didn’t call Sarah. (call)
  • 2. They visited the museum, but they didn’t visit the art gallery. (visit)
  • 3. We prepared for the trip, but we didn’t pack our bags. (prepare)
  • 4. She baked a cake, but she didn’t bake cookies. (bake)
  • 5. John studied math, but he didn’t study history. (study)

Exercise 12.2: Write questions with Did...?

  • 1. I read a book last weekend. How about you? Did you read a book last weekend?
  • 2. I cooked dinner last night. How about you? Did you cook dinner last night?
  • 3. I traveled to Paris last summer. How about you? Did you travel to Paris last summer?
  • 4. I cleaned my room yesterday. How about you? Did you clean your room yesterday?
  • 5. I practiced the piano. How about you? Did you practice the piano?

Exercise 12.3: What did you do yesterday? Write positive or negative sentences.

  • 1. (go shopping) I went shopping or I didn’t go shopping.
  • 2. (wake up early) I woke up early or I didn’t wake up early.
  • 3. (make breakfast) I made breakfast or I didn’t make breakfast.
  • 4. (call a friend) I called a friend or I didn’t call a friend.
  • 5. (go for a run) I went for a run or I didn’t go for a run.
  • 6. (watch a movie) I watched a movie or I didn’t watch a movie.

Exercise 12.4: Write B's questions. Use: arrive, cost, go, go to bed late, happen, have a nice time, stay, win:

  • 1. A: We visited Italy last month. B: Where did you go? A: We went to Rome and Venice.
  • 2. A: I was late for the class. B: What time did you arrive? A: At 10 AM.
  • 3. A: I played chess this afternoon. B: Did you win? A: Yes, I won.
  • 4. A: I enjoyed my vacation. B: Great! Where did you stay? A: In a beach resort.
  • 5. A: We took a cab home. B: How much did it cost? A: Fifteen dollars.
  • 6. A: I'm feeling sleepy today. B: Did you go to bed late? A: Yes, I stayed up reading.
  • 7. A: We had a picnic yesterday. B: Did you have a nice time? A: Yes, it was lovely.
  • 8. A: The door is broken. B: How did it happen? A: It got stuck.

Exercise 12.5: Put the verb in the correct form - positive, negative, or question.

  • 1. We attended the concert, but the music wasn’t great. We didn’t enjoy it. (enjoy)
  • 2. Lisa bought some groceries yesterday - apples, milk, and bread. (buy)
  • 3. Did it snow last night? No, it was clear. (snow)
  • 4. We were tired, so we didn’t go hiking. (go)
  • 5. It was cold outside, so I wore a jacket. (wear)
  • 6. Did you visit Tom this afternoon? No, I didn’t have time. (have)
  • 7. I broke my phone this morning. How did you do that? (do)
  • 8. Why weren’t you at the conference yesterday? I didn’t know about it. (know)

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use the past simple tense to describe actions that happened in the past. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in the past simple tense.
