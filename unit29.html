Unit 29: Using "Might" for Possibility

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "might" to express possibility and uncertainty.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "might" to talk about possible future actions and events.

Explanation

Using "Might" for Possibility: Use "might" + base form of the verb to express something that is possible but not certain.

  • Example: I might go to the cinema.

Examples

Possibility:

  • It's possible that I'll go to the party. → I might go to the party.
  • It's possible that it will rain tomorrow. → It might rain tomorrow.

Negative Possibility:

  • It's possible that Mark will not come. → Mark might not come.
  • It's possible that I won't finish my work. → I might not finish my work.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 29.1: Write sentences with "might"

  • 1. (It's possible that I'll travel to Spain) I might travel to Spain.
  • 2. (It's possible that I'll see you next week) I might see you next week.
  • 3. (It's possible that Sarah will miss the call) Sarah might miss the call.
  • 4. (It's possible that it will rain this afternoon) It might rain this afternoon.
  • 5. (It's possible that I'll arrive late) I might arrive late.

Write sentences with "might not"

  • 6. (It's possible that John will not come to the meeting) John might not come to the meeting.
  • 7. (It's possible that I won't finish the book today) I might not finish the book today.

Exercise 29.2: Somebody is asking you about your plans. You have some ideas, but you are not sure. Choose from the list and write sentences with "I might"

  • 1. Where are you going for your vacation? I'm not sure. I might go to Italy.
  • 2. What are you doing this weekend? I don't know. I might go hiking.
  • 3. When will you visit your friends? I'm not sure. I might visit them on Saturday.
  • 4. What are you going to have for lunch? I don't know. I might have pizza.
  • 5. How are you going to get home tonight? I'm not sure. I might take the train.
  • 6. I hear you received a bonus. What are you going to do with it? I haven't decided yet. I might buy a new laptop.

Exercise 29.3: You ask Lisa questions about her plans for tomorrow. Sometimes she is sure, but usually she is not sure

  • 1. Are you playing tennis tomorrow? Yes, in the morning.
  • 2. Are you going out tomorrow evening? Maybe.
  • 3. Are you going to get up early? Possibly.
  • 4. Are you working tomorrow? No, I'm not.
  • 5. Will you be at home tomorrow afternoon? Perhaps.
  • 6. Are you going to watch a movie? I might.
  • 7. Are you going out in the evening? Yes, I am.
  • 8. Are you going shopping? Maybe.

Now write about Lisa. Use "might" where necessary:

  • 1. She's playing tennis tomorrow morning.
  • 2. She might go out tomorrow evening.
  • 3. She might get up early.
  • 4. She isn't working tomorrow.
  • 5. She might be at home tomorrow afternoon.
  • 6. She might watch a movie.
  • 7. She's going out in the evening.
  • 8. She might go shopping.

Exercise 29.4: Write three things that you might do tomorrow

  • 1. I might read a novel.
  • 2. I might meet a friend for coffee.
  • 3. I might cook a new recipe for dinner.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "might" to express possibility and uncertainty. We practiced forming sentences using "might" to talk about possible future actions and events.
