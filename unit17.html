Unit 17: Present Perfect: Have you ever...?

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use the present perfect tense to ask and answer questions about experiences.

Overview: This unit focuses on the formation and usage of the present perfect tense with "Have you ever...?" questions and how to respond to them.

Explanation

Forming the Present Perfect Questions: Use "Have" or "Has" followed by the subject and the past participle of the verb.

  • Example: Have you ever visited Paris?

Answering Present Perfect Questions: Use "Yes, I have" or "No, I haven't" along with additional details if needed.

  • Example: Yes, I have visited Paris twice. / No, I haven't visited Paris.

Examples

Questions:

  • Have you ever eaten sushi?
  • Has she ever climbed a mountain?
  • Have they ever visited the Great Wall of China?

Positive Statements:

  • I have eaten sushi.
  • She has climbed a mountain.
  • They have visited the Great Wall of China.

Negative Statements:

  • I have not (haven't) eaten sushi.
  • She has not (hasn't) climbed a mountain.
  • They have not (haven't) visited the Great Wall of China.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 17.1: You are asking Helen questions beginning "Have you ever...?" Write the questions:

  • 1. (Paris?) Have you ever been to Paris?
  • 2. (play / tennis?) Have you ever played tennis?
  • 3. (Japan?) Have you ever been to Japan?
  • 4. (lose / your phone?) Have you ever lost your phone?
  • 5. (ride / a horse?) Have you ever ridden a horse?
  • 6. (win / a competition?) Have you ever won a competition?
  • 7. (Los Angeles?) Have you ever been to Los Angeles?
  • 8. (drive / a truck?) Have you ever driven a truck?
  • 9. (break / your arm?) Have you ever broken your arm?

Exercise 17.2: Write sentences about Helen (Look at her answers in Exercise 17.1):

  • 1. (Los Angeles) She's been to Los Angeles twice.
  • 2. (Japan) She's been to Japan once.
  • 3. (win / a competition) She's never won a competition.
  • 4. (ride / a horse) She's ridden a horse a few times.

Now write about yourself. How often have you done these things?

  • 5. (Los Angeles) I've never been to Los Angeles.
  • 6. (play / basketball) I've played basketball many times.
  • 7. (drive / a motorcycle) I've never driven a motorcycle.
  • 8. (be / late for an appointment) I've been late for an appointment a few times.

Exercise 17.3: John is 70 years old. He has had an adventurous life. What has he done?

  • 2. He has done a lot of exciting activities.
  • 3. He has traveled to every continent.
  • 4. He has been married twice.
  • 5. He has written several novels.
  • 6. He has met numerous famous people.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use the present perfect tense to ask and answer questions about experiences. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in the present perfect tense.
