Unit 89: Comparatives with "As... As"

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use comparatives with "as... as" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using comparatives to express equality or inequality between two things or people, emphasizing differences and similarities with "as... as" and "than."

Explanation

Comparatives with "As... As": Use "as... as" to express that two things or people are equal in some way.

  • Example: Rome isn’t as old as Athens.

Examples

Using Comparatives with "As... As" and "Than":

  • A is bigger than C, but not as big as B.
  • Athens is older than Rome.
  • Your room isn't as big as mine.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 89.1: Look at the pictures and write sentences about A, B, and C

  • 1. A is bigger than C, but not as big as B.
  • 2. A is longer than B, but not as long as C.
  • 3. C is heavier than A, but not as heavy as B.
  • 4. A is older than C, but not as old as B.
  • 5. B has got more money than C, but not as much as A. or... but less (money) than A.
  • 6. C works harder than A, but not as hard as B.

Exercise 89.2: Write sentences with "as... as"

  • 1. Athens is older than Rome. → Rome isn’t as old as Athens.
  • 2. My room is bigger than yours. → Your room isn't as big as mine. / ... as my room.
  • 3. You got up earlier than me. → I didn't get up as early as you. / ... as you did.
  • 4. We played better than them. → They didn't play as well as us. / ... as we did.
  • 5. I've been here longer than you. → You haven't been here as long as me. / ... as I have.
  • 6. She's more nervous than him. → He isn't as nervous as her. / ... as she is.

Exercise 89.3: Write "as" or "than"

  • 1. Athens is older than Rome.
  • 2. I don't watch TV as much as you.
  • 3. You eat more than me.
  • 4. I'm more tired today than I was yesterday.
  • 5. Joe isn't as intelligent as he thinks.
  • 6. Belgium is smaller than Switzerland.
  • 7. Brazil isn't as big as Canada.
  • 8. I can't wait longer than an hour.

Exercise 89.4: Complete the sentences about Julia, Andy, and Laura. Use "the same age / the same street" etc.

  • Julia:
    • I'm 22.
    • I live in Hill Street.
    • I got up at 7.15.
    • I haven't got a car.
  • Andy:
    • I'm 24.
    • I live in Baker Street.
    • I got up at 7.15.
    • My car is dark blue.
  • Laura:
    • I'm 24.
    • I live in Hill Street.
    • I got up at 7.45.
    • I've got a car. It's dark blue.
  • Sentences:
    • 1. (age) Andy is the same age as Laura.
    • 2. (street) Julia lives in the same street as Laura.
    • 3. (time) Julia got up at the same time as Andy.
    • 4. (colour) Andy's car is the same colour as Laura's.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use comparatives with "as... as" to express that two things or people are equal in some way. We also learned how to use "than" to show differences between two things or people. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
