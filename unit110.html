Unit 110: Using Prepositions to Describe Movement and Direction

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to correctly use prepositions to describe movement and direction.

Overview: This unit focuses on using prepositions to indicate movement from one place to another and to describe the direction of that movement, making sentences clearer and more precise.

Explanation

Using Prepositions for Movement and Direction: Prepositions like past, under, up, down, along, into, out of, over, through, across, off, round/around, from, on, and into are used to describe the direction and movement from one place to another.

  • Example: Go past the church.

Examples

Using Prepositions to Describe Directions:

  • Go past the church.
  • Go under the bridge.
  • Go up the hill.
  • Go down the steps.
  • Go along this street.
  • Go into the hotel.
  • Go out of the hotel.
  • Go over the bridge.
  • Go through the park.

Using Prepositions to Describe Movement:

  • The dog swam across the river.
  • A book fell off the shelf.
  • A plane flew over the village.
  • A woman got out of the car.
  • A girl ran across the road.
  • A car came round/around the corner.
  • They drove through the village.
  • They got on the train.
  • The moon travels round/around the earth.
  • They got into the house through a window.

Using Prepositions to Complete Sentences:

  • I looked out of the window and watched the people in the street.
  • My house is very near here. It's just round/around the corner.
  • 'Where's my phone?' 'You put it in your bag.'
  • How far is it from here to the airport?
  • We walked round/around the museum for an hour and saw a lot of interesting things.
  • You can put your coat on/over the back of the chair.
  • In tennis, you have to hit the ball over the net.
  • Silvia took a key out of her bag and opened the door.
