Unit 43: Making Sentences Negative

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to make sentences negative and write about someone based on their answers.

Overview: This unit focuses on using negative forms of verbs to create negative sentences and writing positive or negative sentences about someone based on their answers.

Explanation

Making Sentences Negative: Use negative forms like "isn't," "haven't," "don't," etc., to make sentences negative.

  • Example: He is gone. He isn't gone.

Writing About Someone: Based on the answers provided by someone, write positive or negative sentences about them.

  • Example: Are you married? No. He isn't married.

Examples

Making Sentences Negative:

  • I have eaten. I haven't eaten.
  • She is working. She isn't working.

Writing About Someone:

  • Do you like coffee? No. He doesn't like coffee.
  • Can you swim? Yes. He can swim.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 43.1: Make these sentences negative

  • 1. He's gone away. He hasn't gone away.
  • 2. They're married. They aren't/ They're not married.
  • 3. I've had dinner. I haven’t had dinner.
  • 4. It's cold today. It isn't cold today.
  • 5. We'll be late. We won't be late.
  • 6. You should go. You shouldn’t go.

Exercise 43.2: Make these sentences negative. Use don't/doesn’t/didn’t

  • 1. She saw me. She didn’t see me.
  • 2. I like cheese. I don’t like cheese.
  • 3. They understood. They didn't understand.
  • 4. He lives there. He doesn't live there.
  • 5. Go away! Don't go away.
  • 6. I did the shopping. I didn't do the shopping.

Exercise 43.3: Make these sentences negative

  • 1. She can swim. She can't swim.
  • 2. They've arrived. They haven’t arrived.
  • 3. I went to the bank. I didn’t go to the bank.
  • 4. He speaks German. He doesn’t speak German.
  • 5. We were angry. We weren’t angry.
  • 6. He'll be pleased. He won't be pleased.
  • 7. Phone me tonight. Don't phone me tonight.
  • 8. It rained yesterday. It didn’t rain yesterday.
  • 9. I could hear them. I couldn’t hear them.
  • 10. I believe you. I don’t believe you.

Exercise 43.4: Complete these sentences with a negative verb (isn't/haven't/don't etc.)

  • 1. They aren't rich. They haven't got much money.
  • 2. 'Would you like something to eat?' 'No, thank you. I'm not hungry.'
  • 3. I can't find my glasses. Have you seen them?
  • 4. Steve doesn't use email much. He prefers to talk on the phone.
  • 5. We can walk to the station from here. It isn't very far.
  • 6. 'Where's Jane?' 'I haven't seen her today.'
  • 7. Be careful! Don't fall.
  • 8. We went to the cinema last night. I didn't like the film very much.
  • 9. I've been to Japan many times, but I haven't been to Korea.
  • 10. Julia won't be here tomorrow. She is going away.
  • 11. 'Who broke that window?' 'Not me. I didn't do it.'
  • 12. We didn't see what happened. We weren’t looking at the time.
  • 13. Lisa bought a new coat a few days ago, but she hasn't worn it yet.
  • 14. You shouldn't/ mustn't drive so fast. It's dangerous.

Exercise 43.5: You ask Gary questions. He answers 'Yes' or 'No'. Write sentences about Gary, positive or negative

  • 1. Are you married? No. He isn't married.
  • 2. Do you live in London? Yes. He lives in London.
  • 3. Were you born in London? No. He wasn't born in London.
  • 4. Do you like London? No. He doesn't like London.
  • 5. Would you like to live in the country? Yes. He'd like to live in the country.
  • 6. Can you drive? Yes. He can drive.
  • 7. Have you got a car? No. He hasn't got a car.
  • 8. Do you read newspapers? No. He doesn't read newspapers.
  • 9. Are you interested in politics? No. He isn't interested in politics.
  • 10. Do you watch TV most evenings? Yes. He watches TV most evenings.
  • 11. Did you watch TV last night? No. He didn't watch TV last night.
  • 12. Did you go out last night? Yes. He went out last night.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to make sentences negative and write about someone based on their answers. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
