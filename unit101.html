Unit 101: Relative Clauses with "Who" and "Which"

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use relative clauses with "who" and "which" correctly to describe people and things.

Overview: This unit focuses on using relative clauses to provide additional information about people and things, making sentences more detailed and informative.

Explanation

Relative Clauses: Relative clauses are used to give more information about a noun in the main clause. They often begin with "who" for people and "which" for things.

  • Example: A thief is a person who steals things.

Examples

Using "Who":

  • A thief is a person who steals things.
  • I met a woman who can speak six languages.

Using "Which":

  • What's the name of the river which flows through the town?
  • Where is the picture which was hanging on the wall?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 101.1: Choose from the boxes and write sentences: A ... is a person who ...

  • 1. A thief is a person who steals things.
  • 2. A butcher is a person who sells meat.
  • 3. A musician is a person who plays a musical instrument.
  • 4. A patient is a person who is ill in hospital.
  • 5. A dentist is a person who takes care of your teeth.
  • 6. A fool is a person who does stupid things.
  • 7. A genius is a person who is very intelligent.
  • 8. A liar is a person who doesn’t tell the truth.

Exercise 101.2: Make one sentence from two

  • 1. The man who phoned didn’t give his name.
  • 2. The woman who opened the door was wearing a yellow dress.
  • 3. Most of the students who took the exam passed (it).
  • 4. The policeman who stopped our car wasn’t very friendly.

Exercise 101.3: Write "who" or "which"

  • 1. I met a woman who can speak six languages.
  • 2. What's the name of the man who has just started work in your office?
  • 3. What's the name of the river which flows through the town?
  • 4. Where is the picture which was hanging on the wall?
  • 5. Do you know anybody who wants to buy a car?
  • 6. You always ask questions which are difficult to answer.
  • 7. I have a friend who is very good at repairing cars.
  • 8. I think everybody who went to the party enjoyed it very much.
  • 9. Why does he always wear clothes which are too small for him?

Exercise 101.4: Right or wrong? Correct the mistakes

  • 1. A thief is a person who steals things.
  • 2. An aeroplane is a machine that flies.
  • 3. A coffee maker is a machine that/which makes coffee.
  • 4. Have you seen the money that was on the table?
  • 5. I don't like people who/that never stop talking.
  • 6. I know somebody who can help you.
  • 7. I know somebody who works in that shop.
  • 8. Correct the sentences that/which are wrong.
  • 9. My neighbour bought a car that/which cost £4,000.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use relative clauses to provide additional information about people and things. We practiced forming sentences using "who" for people and "which" for things to enhance the detail and informativeness of writing.
