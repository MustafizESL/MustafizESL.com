Unit 104: Using "From... to," "Until," and "Since"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to correctly use "from... to," "until," "since," and "for" to describe periods of time and durations.

Overview: This unit focuses on using these prepositions and conjunctions to indicate time periods, durations, and specific points in time.

Explanation

Using "From... to": Use "from... to" to indicate the start and end of a time period.

  • Example: Alex lived in Canada from 1991 to 1999.

Using "Until": Use "until" to indicate a continuous action or state up to a specific point in time.

  • Example: Alex lived in Canada until 1999.

Using "Since": Use "since" to indicate the starting point of an action or state that continues to the present.

  • Example: Alex has lived in England since 1999.

Using "For": Use "for" to indicate the duration of an action or state.

  • Example: Alex lived in Canada for eight years.

Examples

Using "From... to":

  • Alex lived in Canada from 1991 to 1999.
  • Clare worked in a hotel from 1998 to 2001.

Using "Until":

  • Alex lived in Canada until 1999.
  • Karen lived in France until 2003.

Using "Since":

  • Alex has lived in England since 1999.
  • Clare has worked in a restaurant since 2001.

Using "For":

  • Alex lived in Canada for eight years.
  • Clare has worked in a restaurant for 22 years.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 104.1: Read the information and complete the sentences. Use "from... to," "until," "since."

  • 1. Alex lived in Canada from 1991 to 1999.
  • 2. Alex lived in Canada until 1999.
  • 3. Alex has lived in England since 1999.
  • 4. Karen lived in France until 2003.
  • 5. Karen has lived in Switzerland since 2003.
  • 6. Clare worked in a hotel from 1998 to 2001.
  • 7. Clare has worked in a restaurant since 2001.
  • 8. Adam was a teacher from 1992 to 1998.
  • 9. Adam has been a journalist since 1998.

Exercise 104.2: Write sentences with "for."

  • 1. Alex lived in Canada for eight years.
  • 2. Alex has lived in England for 24 years.
  • 3. Karen has lived in Switzerland for 20 years.
  • 4. Clare worked in a hotel for three years.
  • 5. Clare has worked in a restaurant for 22 years.
  • 6. Adam was a teacher for six years.
  • 7. Adam has been a journalist for 25 years.

Exercise 104.3: Complete the sentences. Use "from... to," "until," "since," or "for."

  • 1. Sue and Dave have been married since 1968.
  • 2. I was tired this morning. I stayed in bed until 10 o'clock.
  • 3. We waited for Sue for half an hour, but she didn't come.
  • 4. 'Have you just arrived?' 'No, I've been here since half past seven.'
  • 5. 'How long did you stay at the party last night?' 'Until midnight.'
  • 6. Dan and I are good friends. We have known each other for ten years.
  • 7. I'm tired. I'm going to lie down for a few minutes.
  • 8. Don't open the door of the train until the train stops.
  • 9. This is my house. I've lived here since I was seven years old.
  • 10. Jack has gone away. He'll be away until Wednesday.
  • 11. Next week I'm going to Paris for three days.
  • 12. I usually finish work at 5.30, but sometimes I work until six.
  • 13. 'How long have you known Anna?' 'Since we were at school together.'
  • 14. Where have you been? I've been waiting for you for twenty minutes.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "from... to" to indicate the start and end of a time period, "until" to indicate a continuous action or state up to a specific point in time, "since" to indicate the starting point of an action or state that continues to the present, and "for" to indicate the duration of an action or state. We practiced forming sentences using these prepositions and conjunctions to enhance clarity and precision in writing.
