Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use possessive pronouns and the word "whose" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using possessive pronouns to indicate ownership and relationships, and how to use "whose" to ask questions about possession.

Explanation

Using Possessive Pronouns: Possessive pronouns indicate ownership or relationship.

  • Example: It's my bag. It's mine.

Common Possessive Pronouns: Mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs.

  • Example: Their house is beautiful. It's theirs.

Using "Whose": Use "whose" to ask questions about possession.

  • Example: Whose car is this? It's theirs.

Examples

Using Possessive Pronouns:

  • It's your money. It's yours.
  • They're her shoes. They're hers.

Using "Whose":

  • Whose books are these? Yours or mine?
  • Whose camera is this? It's hers.
