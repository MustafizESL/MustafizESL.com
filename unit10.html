Unit 10: Learning was/were

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "was" and "were" to describe past states and actions.

Overview: This unit covers the formation and usage of "was" and "were" in the past simple tense.

Explanation

Forming "was" and "were": "Was" and "were" are the past forms of the verb "to be". "Was" is used with the subjects I, he, she, and it. "Were" is used with the subjects you, we, and they.

  • I/he/she/it was
  • You/we/they were

Examples

Positive Statements:

  • I was tired yesterday.
  • He was happy with his results.
  • She was at the party last night.
  • It was a sunny day.
  • You were very helpful.
  • We were at the park.
  • They were excited about the trip.

Negative Statements:

  • I was not (wasn't) tired yesterday.
  • He was not (wasn't) happy with his results.
  • She was not (wasn't) at the party last night.
  • It was not (wasn't) a sunny day.
  • You were not (weren't) very helpful.
  • We were not (weren't) at the park.
  • They were not (weren't) excited about the trip.

Questions:

  • Was I tired yesterday? Yes, I was. / No, I wasn't.
  • Was he happy with his results? Yes, he was. / No, he wasn't.
  • Was she at the party last night? Yes, she was. / No, she wasn't.
  • Was it a sunny day? Yes, it was. / No, it wasn't.
  • Were you very helpful? Yes, you were. / No, you weren't.
  • Were we at the park? Yes, we were. / No, we weren't.
  • Were they excited about the trip? Yes, they were. / No, they weren't.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 10.1: Complete the sentences with "was" or "were":

  • 1. I ___ at home yesterday. (was)
  • 2. They ___ at the beach last weekend. (were)
  • 3. She ___ very happy with the gift. (was)
  • 4. We ___ tired after the long walk. (were)
  • 5. He ___ not (wasn't) at the meeting. (was)
  • 6. You ___ late for the appointment. (were)

Exercise 10.2: Rewrite the sentences in the negative form:

  • 1. I was at the gym. → I was not (wasn't) at the gym.
  • 2. They were on vacation. → They were not (weren't) on vacation.
  • 3. She was excited. → She was not (wasn't) excited.
  • 4. We were at the movies. → We were not (weren't) at the movies.
  • 5. He was here. → He was not (wasn't) here.
  • 6. You were ready. → You were not (weren't) ready.

Exercise 10.3: Form questions using "was" or "were":

  • 1. (I / at the party) Was I at the party?
  • 2. (he / on the train) Was he on the train?
  • 3. (she / at work) Was she at work?
  • 4. (it / rainy) Was it rainy?
  • 5. (we / late) Were we late?
  • 6. (you / happy) Were you happy?
  • 7. (they / at school) Were they at school?

Exercise 10.4: Answer the questions using "was" or "were":

  • 1. Were you at the park yesterday? ___ Yes, I was. / No, I wasn't.
  • 2. Was she happy with her results? ___ Yes, she was. / No, she wasn't.
  • 3. Were they at the party? ___ Yes, they were. / No, they weren't.
  • 4. Was he at the meeting? ___ Yes, he was. / No, he wasn't.
  • 5. Was it a sunny day? ___ Yes, it was. / No, it wasn't.
  • 6. Were we ready to leave? ___ Yes, we were. / No, we weren't.

Exercise 10.5: Write sentences about past events using "was" and "were":

  • 1. (yesterday, at the park) ___ I was at the park yesterday.
  • 2. (last week, on vacation) ___ They were on vacation last week.
  • 3. (this morning, very tired) ___ She was very tired this morning.
  • 4. (last night, at the concert) ___ We were at the concert last night.
  • 5. (yesterday, at the meeting) ___ He was at the meeting yesterday.
  • 6. (last weekend, at the beach) ___ You were at the beach last weekend.

Exercise 10.6: Write questions from these words + was/were. Put the words in the right order:

  • 1. Why ___ late this morning? (were/you) ___ The bus was late.
  • 2. ___ the meeting difficult? (was) ___ No, it was straightforward.
  • 3. Where ___ Sam and Kate last month? (were) ___ They were on a road trip.
  • 4. How much ___ your new laptop? (was) ___ It was expensive.
  • 5. Why ___ you upset yesterday? (were) ___ Because I lost my keys.
  • 6. ___ the event successful last week? (was) ___ Yes, it was amazing.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "was" and "were" to describe past states and actions. We also practiced forming positive and negative statements, as well as questions using "was" and "were".
