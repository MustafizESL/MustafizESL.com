Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to correctly use phrasal verbs, which combine verbs with prepositions like "in," "out," "up," etc., to describe actions and movements.

Overview: This unit focuses on using these phrasal verbs to form meaningful sentences, making sentences clearer and more descriptive.

Explanation

Using Phrasal Verbs: Phrasal verbs combine a verb with a preposition or an adverb. These combinations create new meanings that are often idiomatic.

  • Example: I went to the window and looked out.

Examples

Using Phrasal Verbs in Sentences:

  • I went to the window and looked out.
  • The door was open, so we went in.
  • He heard a plane, so he looked up.
  • She got on her bike and rode off/away.
  • I said hello, and he turned round/around.
  • The bus stopped, and she got off.
  • There was a free seat, so she sat down.
  • A car stopped, and two men got out.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use phrasal verbs to describe actions and movements. We practiced forming sentences using these phrasal verbs to enhance clarity and descriptiveness in writing.
