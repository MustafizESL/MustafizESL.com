Unit 30: Can, Can't, Could, and Couldn't

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "can", "can't", "could", and "couldn't" to express ability and possibility.

Overview: This unit focuses on using these modal verbs in various contexts to talk about abilities and possibilities.

Explanation

Using "Can" and "Can't" for Present Ability: Use "can" to talk about things someone is able to do and "can't" for things someone is not able to do.

  • Example: I can swim. / I can't swim.

Using "Could" and "Couldn't" for Past Ability: Use "could" to talk about things someone was able to do in the past and "couldn't" for things someone was not able to do.

  • Example: I could swim when I was younger. / I couldn't swim when I was younger.

Examples

Present Ability:

  • She can play the guitar.
  • He can't speak French.

Past Ability:

  • They could climb trees when they were kids.
  • I couldn't ride a bike until I was ten.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 30.1: Ask Steve if he can do the things

  • 1. Can you swim?
  • 2. Can you dance?
  • 3. Can you play basketball?
  • 4. Can you run a marathon?
  • 5. Can you drive a motorcycle?
  • 6. Can you cook?

Write sentences about yourself. Use "I can" or "I can't"

  • 7. I can/can't swim.
  • 8. I can/can't dance.
  • 9. I can/can't play basketball.
  • 10. I can/can't drive a motorcycle.
  • 11. I can/can't cook.

Exercise 30.2: Complete these sentences. Use "can" or "can't" + one of these verbs: see - hear - lift - attend

  • 1. I'm sorry, but we can't come to your event next week.
  • 2. I love this hotel room. You can see the city from the balcony.
  • 3. You are speaking very softly. I can't hear you.
  • 4. Have you seen my wallet? I can't find it.

Exercise 30.3: Complete these sentences. Use "can't" or "couldn't" + one of these verbs: decide - eat - find - reach - finish

  • 1. I was exhausted, but I couldn't sleep.
  • 2. I wasn’t hungry yesterday. I couldn't finish my dinner.
  • 3. Emma doesn't know what to choose. She can't decide.
  • 4. I wanted to talk to John yesterday, but I couldn't find him.
  • 5. Laura can't go to the party next Saturday. She has other plans.
  • 6. Steve couldn't attend the meeting last week. He was ill.

Exercise 30.4: Complete the sentences. Use "mustn't" or "don't need to" + one of these words: forget - go - hurry - lose - wait

  • 1. I don't need to go home yet. I can stay a bit longer.
  • 2. We have plenty of time. We don't need to hurry.
  • 3. Keep these documents safe. You mustn't lose them.
  • 4. I'm not ready yet, but you don't need to wait for me. You can leave now and I'll join you later.
  • 5. We mustn't forget to lock the door before we leave.
  • 6. I must contact Emily, but I don't need to call her — I can send her a message.

Exercise 30.5: What do you say in these situations? Use "can" or "could"

  • 1. (open) Could you open the window, please?
  • 2. (pass) Can you pass me the book, please?
  • 3. (turn off) Could you turn off the light, please?
  • 4. (borrow) Can I borrow your pen, please?
  • 5. (use) Could I use your laptop, please?
  • 6. (have) Can I have some water, please?

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "can", "can't", "could", and "couldn't" to express ability and possibility. We practiced forming sentences using these modal verbs to express abilities and possibilities in different contexts.
