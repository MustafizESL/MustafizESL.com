Unit 33: Using "Have to" and "Has to" for Obligation

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "have to" and "has to" to express obligation and necessity.

Overview: This unit focuses on using these phrases in various contexts to talk about things that are necessary to do.

Explanation

Using "Have to" and "Has to" for Present Obligation: Use "have to" for plural subjects and "has to" for singular subjects to talk about things that are necessary to do.

  • Example: I have to work today. / She has to study for the exam.

Using "Had to" for Past Obligation: Use "had to" to talk about things that were necessary to do in the past.

  • Example: I had to finish my homework yesterday.

Examples

Present Obligation:

  • We have to submit the report by Monday.
  • He has to prepare for the presentation tomorrow.

Past Obligation:

  • They had to leave the meeting early.
  • She had to call her friend to confirm the plans.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 33.1: Complete the sentences. Use "have to" or "has to" + these verbs: do - hit - read - speak - travel - wear

  • 1. My vision isn't perfect. I have to wear contact lenses.
  • 2. At the end of the semester, all the students have to complete a final project.
  • 3. Sarah is a literature major. She has to read a lot of classic novels.
  • 4. Albert struggles with English. You have to speak very clearly to him.
  • 5. Kate is frequently on the move. She has to travel extensively for her job.
  • 6. In volleyball, you have to hit the ball over the net.

Exercise 33.2: Complete the sentences. Use "have to" or "has to" + these verbs: answer - buy - change - go - walk

  • 1. We had to walk home last night because there were no taxis available.
  • 2. It's getting late. I have to leave now. I'll see you tomorrow.
  • 3. I went to the market after work yesterday. I had to buy some fresh produce.
  • 4. The bus doesn't go directly to the city center. You have to change buses at the main station.
  • 5. We took a test yesterday. We had to answer three out of five questions.

Exercise 33.3: Complete the questions. Some are present and some are past

  • 1. I have to wake up early tomorrow. What time do you have to get up?
  • 2. George had to wait a long time. How long did he have to wait?
  • 3. Liz has to go somewhere. Where does she have to go?
  • 4. We had to pay a large amount. How much did you have to pay?
  • 5. I have to complete some tasks. What exactly do you have to do?

Exercise 33.4: Write sentences with "don't/doesn't/didn't have to..."

  • 1. Why are you leaving? You don't have to leave.
  • 2. Why is Sue still waiting? She doesn't have to stay.
  • 3. Why did you wake up so early? You didn't have to wake up early.
  • 4. Why is Paul working overtime? He doesn't have to work so hard.
  • 5. Why do you want to rush? We don't have to rush.

Exercise 33.5: Which is correct? Sometimes "must" and "have to" are both correct. Sometimes only one is correct

  • 1. It's a fantastic movie. You must see / have to see it.
  • 2. Julia won't be at work this afternoon. She has to visit the doctor.
  • 3. You can't park your car here without paying. You have to pay.
  • 4. I didn't have any cash last night, so I had to borrow some.
  • 5. I eat too much fast food. I really must stop / have to stop.
  • 6. Paul is running late. He has to meet someone in five minutes.
  • 7. What's wrong? You must tell / have to tell me. I want to help.

Exercise 33.6: Write some things that you (or your friends or family) have to do or had to do

  • 1. (every day) I have to commute to work every day.
  • 2. (every day) My friend has to take her dog for a walk every day.
  • 3. (yesterday) I had to attend a meeting yesterday.
  • 4. (tomorrow) I have to go grocery shopping tomorrow.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "have to" and "has to" to express obligation and necessity. We practiced forming sentences using these phrases to talk about things that are necessary to do.
