Unit 67: Countable and Uncountable Nouns

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use countable and uncountable nouns correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on identifying and using countable and uncountable nouns, and how to use "a" or "an" with countable nouns.

Explanation

Countable Nouns: Nouns that can be counted and have both singular and plural forms.

  • Example: a spoon, two spoons.

Uncountable Nouns: Nouns that cannot be counted and do not have a plural form.

  • Example: water, money.

Examples

Countable Nouns:

  • a bucket, a wallet, an envelope.

Uncountable Nouns:

  • salt, water, toothpaste.
