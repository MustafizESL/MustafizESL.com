Unit 78: Using "Nobody/No-One" and "Nothing"

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "nobody/no-one" and "nothing" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "nobody/no-one" and "nothing" to express the absence of people or things, and how to use them in sentences appropriately.

Explanation

Using "Nobody/No-One": Use "nobody" or "no-one" to refer to the absence of people.

  • Example: There's nobody in the office.

Using "Nothing": Use "nothing" to refer to the absence of things.

  • Example: There's nothing in the bag.

Using "Anybody/Anyone" and "Anything": Use "anybody" or "anyone" and "anything" in negative statements and questions to refer to unspecified people or things.

  • Example: I haven’t got anything to do.

Examples

Using "Nobody/No-One" and "Nothing":

  • There's nothing in the bag.
  • There's nobody in the office.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 78.1: Rewrite these sentences with "nobody/no-one" or "nothing"

  • 1. There isn't anything in the bag. -> There's nothing in the bag.
  • 2. There isn't anybody in the office. -> There's nobody in the office.
  • 3. I haven’t got anything to do. -> I've got nothing to do.
  • 4. There isn't anything on TV. -> There's nothing on TV.
  • 5. There wasn't anyone at home. -> There was no-one at home.
  • 6. We didn't find anything. -> We found nothing.

Exercise 78.2: Rewrite these sentences with "anybody/anyone" or "anything"

  • 1. There's nothing in the bag. -> There isn't anything in the bag.
  • 2. There Was Nobody on the bus. -> There wasn't anybody on the bus.
  • 3. I've got nothing to read. -> I haven't got anything to read.
  • 4. I've got no-one to help me. -> I haven't got anyone to help me.
  • 5. She heard nothing. -> She didn't hear anything.
  • 6. We've got nothing for dinner. -> We haven't got anything for dinner.

Exercise 78.3: Answer these questions with "nobody/no-one" or "nothing"

  • 1a. What did you say? -> Nothing.
  • 2a. Who saw you? -> Nobody.
  • 3a. What do you want? -> Nothing.
  • 4a. Who did you meet? -> Nobody. /No-one.
  • 5a. Who knows the answer? -> Nobody. /No-one.
  • 6a. What did you buy? -> Nothing.
  • 7a. What happened? -> Nothing.
  • 8a. Who was late? -> Nobody. /No-one.

Now answer the same questions with full sentences. Use "nobody/no-one/nothing" or "anybody/anyone/anything":

  • 1b. I didn’t say anything.
  • 2b. Nobody saw me.
  • 3b. I don’t want anything.
  • 4b. I didn’t meet anybody/anyone.
  • 5b. Nobody/No-one knows the answer.
  • 6b. I didn’t buy anything.
  • 7b. Nothing happened.
  • 8b. Nobody/No-one was late.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "nobody/no-one" and "nothing" to express the absence of people or things. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
