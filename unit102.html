Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to form and use relative clauses to make sentences more descriptive and informative.

Overview: This unit focuses on combining sentences using relative clauses to provide additional information about people, objects, and places.

Explanation

Relative Clauses: Relative clauses provide more information about a noun in the main clause. They often begin with relative pronouns such as "who," "whom," "whose," "which," and "that."

  • Example: Have you seen the photographs Helen took?

Examples

Combining Sentences:

  • Have you seen the photographs Helen took?
  • I've lost the pen you gave me.
  • The bag I was carrying was very heavy.
