Unit 58: Using "Have" and "Have Got"

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "have" and "have got" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on the differences between "have" and "have got" and how to use them in sentences appropriately.

Explanation

Using "Have": Use "have" to indicate possession, relationships, illnesses, and actions.

  • Example: I have a headache.

Using "Have Got": Use "have got" to indicate possession and relationships.

  • Example: I've got a car.

Examples

Using "Have":

  • I didn't have time to do the shopping yesterday.
  • Gary had a cold last week.

Using "Have Got":

  • Has Lisa got a car? (OR Does Lisa have a car?)
  • He hasn’t got a key. (OR He doesn’t have a key.)

Practice Exercises

Exercise 58.1: Write the correct form of "have" or "have got"

  • 1. I didn’t have time to do the shopping yesterday. (I / not / have)
  • 2. 'Has Lisa got (OR Does Lisa have) a car?' 'No, she can't drive.' (Lisa / have?)
  • 3. He can't open the door. He hasn’t got / He doesn’t have a key. (He / not / have)
  • 4. Gary had a cold last week. He's better now. (Gary / have)
  • 5. What's wrong? Have you got / Do you have a headache? (you / have?)
  • 6. We wanted to go by taxi, but we didn’t have enough money. (we / not / have)
  • 7. Liz is very busy. She hasn’t got/ she doesn’t have much free time. (she / not / have)
  • 8. Did you have any problems when you were on holiday? (you / have?)

Exercise 58.2: What are these people doing? Choose from the list

  • a bath - breakfast - a cup of tea - dinner - a good time - a rest
  • 1. They're having breakfast.
  • 2. She's/ She is having a cup of tea.
  • 3. He's/ He is having a rest.
  • 4. They're / They are having a good time.
  • 5. They're / They are having dinner.
  • 6. He's / He is having a bath.

Exercise 58.3: What do you say in these situations? Use "have"

  • 1. Emily is going on holiday. What do you say to her before she goes?
    • Have a nice holiday!
  • 2. You meet Claire at the airport. She has just got off her plane. Ask her about the flight.
    • Did you have a good flight?
  • 3. Tim is going on a long trip. What do you say to him before he leaves?
    • Have a nice/good trip!
  • 4. It's Monday morning. You are at work. Ask Paula about her weekend.
    • Did you have a nice/good weekend?
  • 5. Paul has just come home after playing tennis with a friend. Ask him about the game.
    • Did you have a good game?

Exercise 58.4: Complete the sentences. Use "have/had" and choose from the list

  • an accident - a glass of water - a look - a walk - a party - something to eat
  • 1. We had a party a few days ago. We invited 50 people.
  • 2. 'Shall we have something to eat?' 'No, I'm not hungry.'
  • 3. I was thirsty, so I had a glass of water.
  • 4. I like to get up early and have a walk.
  • 5. Tina is a very good driver. She has never had an accident.
  • 6. There's something wrong with the engine of my car. Can you have a look at it?

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "have" and "have got" correctly in various contexts. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
