Unit 82: Using "Both," "Either," and "Neither"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "both," "either," and "neither" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using these words to refer to two people or things and their use in affirmative and negative contexts.

Explanation

Using "Both": Use "both" to refer to two people or things together.

  • Example: Both cities are beautiful.

Using "Either": Use "either" to refer to one or the other of two people or things.

  • Example: Either of these books is good.

Using "Neither": Use "neither" to refer to not one nor the other of two people or things.

  • Example: Neither team played well.

Examples

Using "Both," "Either," and "Neither":

  • Both cities are beautiful.
  • Either day is fine with me.
  • Neither of the answers is correct.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 82.1: Write "both/either/neither". Use "of" where necessary

  • 1. Last year I went to Paris and Rome. I liked both cities very much.
  • 2. There were two pictures on the wall. I didn’t like either of them.
  • 3. It was a good football match. Both teams played well.
  • 4. It wasn’t a good football match. Neither team played well.
  • 5. 'Is your friend English or American?' 'Neither. She's Australian.'
  • 6. We went away for two days, but the weather wasn’t good. It rained both days.
  • A: I bought two newspapers. Which one do you want?
    • B: Either. It doesn’t matter which one.
  • 8. I invited Donna and Mike to the party, but neither of them came.
  • 'Do you go to work by car or by bus?'
    • 'Neither. I always walk.'
  • 'Which jacket do you prefer, this one or that one?'
    • 'I don't like either of them.'
  • 'Do you work or are you a student?'
    • 'Both. I work and I'm a student too.'
  • 12. Paula and I didn’t know the time because neither of us had a watch.
  • 13. Helen has got two sisters and a brother. Both sisters are married.
  • 14. Helen has got two sisters and a brother. I've met her brother, but I haven’t met either of her sisters.

Exercise 82.2: Complete the sentences for the pictures. Use "Both..." or "Neither..."

  • 1. Both cups are empty.
  • 2. Both windows are open.
  • 3. Neither man is wearing a hat. (or Neither of them is wearing a hat.)
  • 4. Both men have (got) beards. (or Both of them have (got) beards.)
  • 5. Both buses go to the airport. (or Both buses are going to the airport.)
  • 6. Neither answer is right.

Exercise 82.3: A man and a woman answered some questions. Their answers were the same. Write sentences with "Both/Neither of them..."

  • Box:
  • 1. Are you married? No - No
    • Neither of them is married.
  • 2. How old are you? 21-21
    • Both of them are 21.
  • 3. Are you a student? Yes-Yes
    • Both of them are students.
  • 4. Have you got a car? No-No
    • Neither of them has (got) a car.
  • 5. Where do you live? London - London
    • Both of them live in London.
  • 6. Do you like cooking? Yes-Yes
    • Both of them like cooking.
  • 7. Can you play the piano? No-No
    • Neither of them can play the piano.
  • 8. Do you read newspapers? Yes - Yes
    • Both of them read newspapers.
  • 9. Are you interested in sport? No - No
    • Neither of them is interested in sport.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "both" to refer to two people or things together, "either" to refer to one or the other of two people or things, and "neither" to refer to not one nor the other of two people or things. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
