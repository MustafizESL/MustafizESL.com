Unit 74: Using This/That/These/Those

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "this," "that," "these," and "those" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using these demonstrative pronouns to indicate specific items that are near or far, singular or plural.

Explanation

Using This/These: Use "this" for a singular item that is near, and "these" for plural items that are near.

  • Example: This book is mine. These books are mine.

Using That/Those: Use "that" for a singular item that is far, and "those" for plural items that are far.

  • Example: That car is his. Those cars are his.

Examples

Using This/That/These/Those:

  • Do you like these shoes?
  • Who lives in that house?
  • How much are these postcards?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 74.1: Complete the sentences. Use this/that/these/those + these words: birds - house - plate - postcards - seat - shoes

  • 1. Do you like these shoes?
  • 2. Who lives in that house?
  • 3. How much are these postcards?
  • 4. Look at those birds!
  • 5. Excuse me, is this seat free?
  • 6. These plates are dirty.

Exercise 74.2: Write questions: Is this/that your...? or Are these/those your...?

  • 1. Is this your bag?
  • 2. Is that your umbrella?
  • 3. Is this your book?
  • 4. Are those your books?
  • 5. Is that your bicycle/bike?
  • 6. Are these your keys?
  • 7. Are these your keys? (duplicate)
  • 8. Is this your watch?
  • 9. Are those your glasses?
  • 10. Are these your gloves?

Exercise 74.3: Complete the sentences with this is or that’s or that

  • 1. A: I'm sorry I'm late.
    • B: That's all right.
  • 2. A: I can't come to the party tomorrow.
    • B: Oh, that's a pity. Why not?
  • 3. on the phone
    • SUE: Hello, Jane. This is Sue.
    • JANE: Oh, hi Sue. How are you?
  • 4. A: You're lazy.
    • B: That's not true!
  • 5. A: Beth plays the piano very well.
    • B: Does she? I didn’t know that.
  • 6. Mark meets Paul's sister, Helen.
    • PAUL: Mark, this is my sister, Helen.
    • MARK: Hi, Helen.
  • 7. A: I'm sorry. I was angry yesterday.
    • B: That's OK. Forget it!
  • 8. A: You're a friend of Tim's, aren’t you?
    • B: Yes, that's right.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "this," "that," "these," and "those" to refer to specific items that are near or far, singular or plural. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
