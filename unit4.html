Unit 4: Forming Questions with Present Continuous

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to form and answer questions using the present continuous tense.

Overview: This unit introduces the formation of questions with the present continuous tense and provides practice in forming and answering these questions.

Explanation

Forming Questions: To form questions in the present continuous tense, invert the subject and the auxiliary verb "to be" (am, is, are) before the main verb ending in -ing.

  • Am I...?
  • Are you...?
  • Is he/she/it...?
  • Are we...?
  • Are they...?

Examples

Questions and Answers:

  • Are you reading a book? Yes, I am.
  • Is she going to the store? No, she isn’t.
  • Are they playing soccer? Yes, they are.
  • Is it raining outside? Yes, it is.
  • Are we having dinner now? No, we aren’t.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 4.1: Form questions using the given prompts:

  • 1. (you / reading / a book) ___ Are you reading a book?
  • 2. (she / going / to the market) ___ Is she going to the market?
  • 3. (they / watching / a movie) ___ Are they watching a movie?
  • 4. (it / raining / outside) ___ Is it raining outside?
  • 5. (we / eating / lunch) ___ Are we eating lunch?
  • 6. (he / playing / guitar) ___ Is he playing the guitar?

Exercise 4.2: Complete the sentences using these verbs: studying, walking, listening, cooking, driving, playing:

  • 1. What are you ___? (studying)
  • 2. Where is she ___? (walking)
  • 3. What are you ___? (listening to)
  • 4. Why is he ___? (cooking)
  • 5. Who is ___ to the concert? (going)
  • 6. What are they ___? (playing)

Exercise 4.3: Rearrange the words to form questions:

  • 1. (is / your / doing / what / friend) ___ What is your friend doing?
  • 2. (are / they / playing / where) ___ Where are they playing?
  • 3. (she / why / is / crying) ___ Why is she crying?
  • 4. (you / what / eating / are) ___ What are you eating?
  • 5. (he / going / is / where) ___ Where is he going?
  • 6. (are / listening / they / to / what) ___ What are they listening to?

Exercise 4.4: Write questions and short answers:

  • 1. Are you reading a magazine? ___ No, I’m not.
  • 2. Is she writing an essay? ___ Yes, she is.
  • 3. Are they playing chess? ___ No, they aren’t.
  • 4. Is it snowing right now? ___ No, it isn’t.
  • 5. Are you wearing a hat? ___ Yes, I am.
  • 6. Is he painting a picture? ___ Yes, he is.

Exercise 4.5: Complete the questions and answers in a conversation:

  • A: ___ (what / you / doing)?
  • B: I’m reading a book.
  • A: ___ (you / enjoying / it)?
  • B: Yes, I am. It’s very interesting.
  • A: ___ (where / you / sitting)?
  • B: I’m sitting in the living room.
  • A: ___ (anyone / else / reading / too)?
  • B: No, I’m the only one.

Summary

Review: Summarize the key points learned in the unit.

  • Forming questions with the present continuous tense by inverting the subject and the auxiliary verb.
  • Answering questions in positive and negative forms.
