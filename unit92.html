Unit 92: Using "Too" and "Enough"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "too" and "enough" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "too" to indicate an excess amount or degree and "enough" to indicate sufficiency in quantity or degree.

Explanation

Using "Too": "Too" is used to indicate that there is more than is necessary or acceptable. It can be used with adjectives or adverbs.

  • Example: The music is too loud.

Using "Enough": "Enough" is used to indicate that there is as much or as many as needed. It can be used with nouns, adjectives, or adverbs.

  • Example: He isn’t tall enough.

Examples

Using "Too":

  • The box is too heavy.
  • She's driving too fast.

Using "Enough":

  • There isn't enough wind.
  • The car isn’t big enough.

Exercise 92.3: Complete the sentences. Use "too" or "enough" with these words

  • 1. I couldn't work. I was too tired. (tired)
  • 2. Can you turn the radio up, please? It isn’t loud enough. (loud)
  • 3. I don't want to walk home. It's too far. (far)
  • 4. Don't buy anything in that shop. It's too expensive. (expensive)
  • 5. You can't put all your things in this bag. It isn’t/ It’s not big enough. (big)

  • 8. I can't talk to you now. I'm too busy. (busy)
  • 9. I thought the film was boring. It was too long. (long)

Exercise 92.4: Complete the sentences. Use "too" (adjective) + "to..."

  • 1. (I'm not going out / cold) It's too cold to go out.
  • 2. (I'm not going to bed / early) It's too early to go to bed.
  • 3. (they're not getting married / young) They're too young to get married.
  • 4. (nobody goes out at night / dangerous) It's too dangerous to go out at night.
  • 5. (don't phone Sue now / late) It's too late to phone Sue (now).
  • 6. (I didn't say anything / surprised) I was too surprised to say anything.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "too" to indicate an excess amount or degree and "enough" to indicate sufficiency in quantity or degree. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
