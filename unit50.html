Unit 50: Reporting Speech with "He/She/They said that..."

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to report speech using "He/She/They said that..." and distinguish between "say/said" and "tell/told".

Overview: This unit focuses on transforming direct speech into reported speech and using the correct forms of "say" and "tell" in sentences.

Explanation

Reporting Speech: Use "He/She/They said that..." to report what someone else has said.

  • Example: "I've lost my watch." He said that he had lost his watch.

Using "say/said" and "tell/told": Use "say/said" to report what someone says without specifying a listener, and use "tell/told" to specify who was informed.

  • Example: She said she was tired. / She told me she was tired.

Examples

Reporting Speech:

  • "I'm very busy." She said that she was very busy.
  • "I can't go to the party." She said that she couldn't go to the party.

Using "say/said" and "tell/told":

  • He said he was tired.
  • Jack told me that you were ill.

Exercise 50.1: Read what these people say and write sentences with "He/She/They said that..."

  • 1. "I've lost my watch."
    • He said that he had lost his watch.
  • 2. "I'm very busy."
    • She said that she was very busy.
  • 3. "I can't go to the party."
    • She said that she couldn't go to the party.
  • 4. "I have to go out."
    • He said that he had to go out.
  • 5. "I'm learning Russian."
    • He said that he was learning Russian.
  • 6. "I don't feel very well."
    • She said that she didn't feel very well.
  • 7. "We'll be home late."
    • They said that they would be home late.
  • 8. "I've just come back from holiday."
    • She said that she had just come back from holiday.
  • 9. "I'm going to buy a new computer."
    • She said that she was going to buy a new computer.

Exercise 50.2: Use the pictures to complete the sentences

  • 3. Mike: "I need it."
    • I wanted to borrow Mike's ladder, but he said that he needed it.
  • 4. Hannah: "I don't want to go."
    • Hannah was invited to the party, but she said that she didn't want to go.
  • 5. Susan: "You can have it."
    • Susan told me she didn't want the picture. She said that I could have it.
  • 6. Martin: "I'll send you a postcard."
    • Martin has just gone away on holiday. He said that he would send me a postcard.
  • 7. Nicole: "Where's Robert? He's gone home."
    • I was looking for Robert. Nicole said that he had gone home.
  • 8. David: "I want to watch TV."
    • "Why did David stay at home?" "He said that he wanted to watch TV."
  • 9. Mary: "I'm going to the cinema."
    • "Has Mary gone out?" "I think so. She said that she was going to the cinema."
