Unit 1: Understanding am/is/are

Lesson Overview

This unit covers essential grammar and vocabulary needed to understand the verbs "am", "is", and "are".

Key Points:

  • Introduction to the verb forms: am, is, are.
  • How to use these verbs effectively in different sentences.
  • Practice exercises to reinforce learning.

Explanation

The verb "to be" changes form depending on the subject of the sentence:

  • I am (I'm)
  • You are (You're)
  • He/She/It is (He's/She's/It's)
  • We are (We're)
  • They are (They're)

Examples

Positive Statements:

  • I am a student. (I'm a student.)
  • You are my friend. (You're my friend.)
  • He is a teacher. (He's a teacher.)
  • She is happy. (She's happy.)
  • It is a cat. (It's a cat.)
  • We are ready. (We're ready.)
  • They are here. (They're here.)

Negative Statements:

  • I am not tired. (I'm not tired.)
  • You are not late. (You're not late.)
  • He is not angry. (He's not angry.)
  • She is not busy. (She's not busy.)
  • It is not broken. (It's not broken.)
  • We are not lost. (We're not lost.)
  • They are not here. (They're not here.)

Practice Exercises

Exercise 1.1: Fill in the blanks with am, is, or are:

  • The sky ___ clear today. (am/is/are)
  • I ___ happy to see you. (am/is/are)
  • This book ___ interesting. (am/is/are)
  • Those apples ___ fresh. (am/is/are)
  • Look! There ___ a bird on the tree. (am/is/are)
  • My friends and I ___ going to the park. (am/is/are)
  • Sarah ___ at the library. Her friends ___ at the café. (am/is/are)
  • I ___ a student. My brother ___ an engineer. (am/is/are)

Exercise 1.2: Complete the sentences:

  • John ___ tired. He's resting now. (am/is/are)
  • I'm not sleepy, but I ___ bored. (am/is/are)
  • Mrs. Smith ___ a kind person. She's very helpful. (am/is/are)
  • These shoes aren't new, but they ___ comfortable. (am/is/are)
  • The weather ___ beautiful today. It's warm and breezy. (am/is/are)
  • 'You're late.' 'No, I ___ not. I ___ on time!' (am/is/are)
  • Michael isn't here. He ___ at the gym. (am/is/are)
  • 'Here ___ your book.' 'Oh, thank you very much.' (am/is/are)

Exercise 1.3: Write sentences about yourself:

  • (name?) My name is [Your Name].
  • (from?) I am from [Your Country].
  • (age?) I am [Your Age].
  • (job?) I am a [Your Job].
  • (favourite colour or colours?) My favourite colour is [Your Favourite Colour].
  • (interested in...?) I am interested in [Your Interest].

Exercise 1.4: Write sentences for the pictures:

  • She ___ thirsty. (is)
  • They ___ cold. (are)
  • He ___ hot. (is)
  • He ___ afraid. (is)
  • They ___ hungry. (are)
  • She ___ angry. (is)

Exercise 1.5: Write true sentences, positive or negative. Use is/isn’t or are/aren’t:

  • (it / raining today) It ___ raining today. (is/isn’t)
  • (it / sunny today?) It ___ sunny today. (is/isn’t)
  • (my feet / cold) My feet ___ cold. (are/aren’t)
  • (France / a big country) France ___ a big country. (is/isn’t)
  • (gold / cheap) Gold ___ cheap. (is/isn’t)
  • (Tokyo / in Europe) Tokyo ___ in Europe. (is/isn’t)

Speaking Practice

Role Play: Pair up and practice asking and answering questions using am/is/are.

Example:

  • Student A: Are you a student?
  • Student B: Yes, I am. Are you a student too?
  • Student A: Yes, I am.

Quiz

Multiple Choice:

  • 1. He ___ a good singer.
    • a) am
    • b) is
    • c) are
  • 2. They ___ happy to see you.
    • a) am
    • b) is
    • c) are

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use the verb "to be" (am, is, are) correctly in various sentence structures. We practiced forming positive statements and negative statements.
