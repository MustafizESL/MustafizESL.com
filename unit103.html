Unit 103: Using "At," "On," and "In" for Time Expressions

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to correctly use "at," "on," and "in" for various time expressions.

Overview: This unit focuses on using prepositions of time to indicate specific times, days, months, and periods, making sentences clearer and more precise.

Explanation

Using "At": Use "at" for precise times and specific points of time.

  • Examples: at 8 o'clock, at half past two, at night, at the weekend.

Using "On": Use "on" for specific days and dates.

  • Examples: on Monday, on 6 June, on Christmas Day.

Using "In": Use "in" for longer periods of time such as months, years, seasons, and parts of the day.

  • Examples: in the morning, in 1997, in September, in winter.

Examples

Using "At":

  • I'll see you at 7.30 tomorrow evening.
  • I often go away at the weekend.

Using "On":

  • I'm starting my new job on 3 July.
  • Jane's birthday is on Friday morning.

Using "In":

  • Diane and I first met in 1991.
  • We often go to the beach in summer.

Exercise 103.4: Write sentences with "in ..."

  • 1. It's 8.25 now. The train leaves at 8.30. → The train leaves in 5 minutes.

  • 3. Today is 14 June. My exam is on 28 June. → My exam is in two weeks.
  • 4. It's 3 o'clock now. Tom will be here at 3.30. → Tom will be here in half an hour./... in 30 minutes.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "at" for precise times and specific points of time, "on" for specific days and dates, and "in" for longer periods of time such as months, years, seasons, and parts of the day. We practiced forming sentences using these prepositions to enhance clarity and precision in writing.
