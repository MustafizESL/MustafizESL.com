Unit 90: Comparatives and Superlatives

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to form and use comparatives and superlatives correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using comparatives to compare two things or people and superlatives to indicate the highest degree among three or more things or people.

Explanation

Comparatives: Comparatives are used to compare two things or people. They often end in "-er" for one-syllable adjectives and "more" for longer adjectives.

  • Example: bigger, more expensive.

Superlatives: Superlatives indicate the highest degree among three or more things or people. They often end in "-est" for one-syllable adjectives and "most" for longer adjectives.

  • Example: biggest, most expensive.

Examples

Using Comparatives:

  • A is bigger than D.
  • D is more expensive than A.

Using Superlatives:

  • A is the biggest.
  • C is the most expensive.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 90.1: Write sentences with comparatives (older etc.) and superlatives (the oldest etc.)

  • 1. big/small
    • (A/D) A is bigger than D.
    • (A) A is the biggest.
    • (B) B is the smallest.
  • 2. long/short
    • (C/A) C is longer than A.
    • (D) D is the longest.
    • (B) B is the shortest.
  • 3. young/old
    • (D/C) D is younger than C.
    • (B) B is the youngest.
    • (C) C is the oldest.
  • 4. expensive/cheap
    • (D/A) D is more expensive than A.
    • (C) C is the most expensive.
    • (A) A is the cheapest.
  • 5. good/bad
    • (A/C) A is better than C.
    • (A) A is the best.
    • (D) D is the worst.

Exercise 90.2: Complete the sentences. Use a superlative (the oldest etc.)

  • 1. This building is very old. It's the oldest building in the town.
  • 2. It was a very happy day. It was the happiest of my life.
  • 3. It's a very good film. It's the best film I've ever seen.
  • 4. She's a very popular singer. She's the most popular singer in the country.
  • 5. It was a very bad mistake. It was the worst mistake I've ever made.
  • 6. It's a very pretty village. It's the prettiest village I've ever seen.
  • 7. It was a very cold day. It was the coldest day of the year.
  • 8. He's a very boring person. He's the most boring person I've ever met.

Exercise 90.3: Write sentences with a superlative (the longest etc.). Choose from the boxes

  • Box-2: high - large - long
  • Box-3: country - city - mountain - river - state - planet
  • Box-4: Africa - Australia - the USA - South America - the world - the solar system
  • 1. Sydney is the largest city in Australia.
  • 2. Everest is the highest mountain in the world.
  • 3. Brazil is the largest country in South America.
  • 4. Alaska is the largest state in the USA.
  • 5. The Nile is the longest river in Africa. / ... in the world.
  • 6. Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use comparatives to compare two things or people and superlatives to indicate the highest degree among three or more things or people. We formed comparatives by adding "-er" for one-syllable adjectives and using "more" for longer adjectives. We formed superlatives by adding "-est" for one-syllable adjectives and using "most" for longer adjectives. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
