Unit 60: Using Possessive Pronouns

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use possessive pronouns correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using possessive pronouns to indicate ownership and relationships, and how to use them in sentences appropriately.

Explanation

Using Possessive Pronouns: Possessive pronouns indicate ownership or relationship.

  • Example: This is my book.

Common Possessive Pronouns: My, our, your, his, her, its, their.

  • Example: His car is red.

Examples

Using Possessive Pronouns:

  • I'm going to wash my hands.
  • They live with their parents.

Indicating Relationships:

  • I saw Sarah with her husband Philip.
  • Do you know Mr Watson? I don't know his wife.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 60.1: Complete the sentences in the same way

  • 1. I'm going to wash my hands.
  • 2. She's going to wash her hands.
  • 3. We are going to wash our hands.
  • 4. He's going to wash his hands.
  • 5. They're going to wash their hands.
  • 6. Are you going to wash your hands?

Exercise 60.2: Complete the sentences in the same way

  • 1. He lives with his parents.
  • 2. They live with their parents.
  • 3. We live with our parents.
  • 4. Jane lives with her parents.
  • 5. I live with my parents.
  • 6. John lives with his parents.
  • 7. Do you live with your parents?
  • 8. Most children live with their parents.

Exercise 60.3: Look at the family tree, and complete the sentences with his/her/their

  • 1. I saw Sarah with her husband Philip.
  • 2. I saw Laura and Steve with their children.
  • 3. I saw Steve with his wife, Laura.
  • 4. I saw Gary with his brother Tim.
  • 5. I saw Laura with her brother Tim.
  • 6. I saw Sarah and Philip with their son Tim.
  • 7. I saw Laura with her parents.
  • 8. I saw Emma and Robert with their parents.

Exercise 60.4: Write my/our/your/ his/her/their/its

  • 1. Do you like your job?
  • 2. I know Mr Watson but I don't know his wife.
  • 3. Alice and Tom live in London. Their son lives in Australia.
  • 4. We're going to have a party. We are going to invite all our friends.
  • 5. Anna is going out with her friends this evening.
  • 6. I like tennis. It’s my favourite sport.
  • 7. 'Is that your car?' 'No, I haven’t got a car.'
  • 8. I want to phone Maria. Do you know her phone number?
  • 9. Do you think most people are happy in their jobs.
  • 10. I'm going to wash my hair before I go out.
  • 11. This is a beautiful tree. Its leaves are a beautiful colour.
  • 12. John has a brother and a sister. His brother is 25 and his sister is 21.

Exercise 60.5: Complete the sentences. Use my/his/their etc. with these words

  • Coat - homework - house - husband - job - key - name
  • 1. Jim doesn't enjoy his job. It's not very interesting.
  • 2. I can't get in. I haven't got my key.
  • 3. Sally is married. Her husband works in a bank.
  • 4. Please take off your coat and sit down.
  • 5. 'What are the children doing?' 'They're doing their homework.'
  • 6. 'Do you know that man?' 'Yes, but I don’t know his name.'
  • 7. We live in Barron street. Our house is at the end on the left.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use possessive pronouns to indicate ownership or relationships. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
