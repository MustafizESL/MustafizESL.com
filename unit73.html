Unit 73: Geography and Use of "The"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "the" correctly with geographical names and places.

Overview: This unit focuses on the rules for using "the" with names of countries, continents, rivers, oceans, mountains, and other geographical features.

Explanation

Using "The" with Geographical Names: Some geographical names require "the," while others do not. The rules depend on the type of geographical feature and the specific name.

Examples

With "The":

  • The Atlantic is between Africa and America.
  • The Andes are mountains in South America.

Without "The":

  • Cairo is the capital of Egypt.
  • Sweden is a country in northern Europe.
