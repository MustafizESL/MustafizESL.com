Unit 28: Making Offers and Decisions

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "will" for making offers and decisions, and "shall" for making suggestions and offers.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "will" and "shall" in different contexts to express decisions, offers, and suggestions.

Explanation

Using "Will" for Offers and Decisions: Use "I'll" + base form of the verb to offer to do something or to make a decision at the moment of speaking.

  • Example: I'll carry that bag for you.

Using "Shall" for Suggestions and Offers: Use "Shall I/we" + base form of the verb to make a suggestion or offer.

  • Example: Shall we go out tonight?

Examples

Offers with "Will":

  • I'll help you with your homework.
  • I'll open the window.

Decisions with "Will":

  • I think I'll have a coffee.
  • I don't think I'll go out tonight.

Suggestions with "Shall":

  • Shall we go to the park?
  • Shall I make dinner?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 28.1: Complete the sentences. Use I'll (I will) + these verbs: carry - do - eat - send - show - sit - stay

  • 1. My backpack is really heavy. I'll carry it for you.
  • 2. Enjoy your trip! Thanks! I'll send you a postcard.
  • 3. I don't want this apple. Well, I'm hungry. I'll eat it.
  • 4. Would you like a chair? No, it's fine. I'll sit on the floor.
  • 5. Did you call Mark? Oh no, I forgot. I'll do it now.
  • 6. Are you coming along? No, I don't think so. I'll stay here.
  • 7. How do you use this gadget? Hand it over and I'll show you.
