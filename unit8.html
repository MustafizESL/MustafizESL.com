Unit 8: Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will be able to distinguish between the present simple and present continuous tenses and use them accurately in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on understanding when to use the present simple tense and when to use the present continuous tense.

Explanation

The present simple tense is used for habitual actions and general truths, while the present continuous tense is used for actions happening right now.

  • Present Simple: I work, you work, he/she/it works, we work, they work
  • Present Continuous: I am working, you are working, he/she/it is working, we are working, they are working

Examples

Present Simple:

  • I read books every day.
  • She plays the guitar.
  • They live in New York.

Present Continuous:

  • I am reading a book right now.
  • She is playing the guitar at the moment.
  • They are living in New York for the summer.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 8.1: Answer the questions using the present simple or present continuous tense:

  • 1. Does she usually read books in the evenings?
    • Yes, she does.
  • 2. Are they playing soccer in the park right now?
    • Yes, they are.
  • 3. Does he like to travel every summer?
    • Yes, he does.
  • 4. Do we visit our grandparents every Sunday?
    • Yes, we do.
  • 5. Do you think this movie is great?
    • Yes, I do.

Exercise 8.2: Complete the sentences with the present simple or present continuous form of the verb:

  • 1. (work) John usually ___ from 9 AM to 5 PM.
  • 2. (have) Look! She ___ breakfast right now.
  • 3. (go) They ___ to the gym every Monday.
  • 4. (study) At the moment, he ___ for his exams.
  • 5. (watch) We ___ TV every evening.
  • 6. (make) Listen! Someone ___ a lot of noise in the kitchen.

Exercise 8.3: Choose the correct option to complete the sentences:

  • 1. She (reads / is reading) a book right now.
  • 2. They (live / are living) in London for a year.
  • 3. He always (plays / is playing) football on Saturdays.
  • 4. I (wake up / am waking up) at 6 AM every day.
  • 5. We (have / are having) dinner at the moment.

Exercise 8.4: Rewrite the sentences using the correct tense (present simple or present continuous):

  • 1. She reads the newspaper every morning. (currently reading)
    • She is currently reading the newspaper.
  • 2. He is playing tennis every Saturday. (habit)
    • He plays tennis every Saturday.
  • 3. They are working on a new project this week. (usually)
    • They usually work on new projects.
  • 4. I am going to the gym every evening. (habit)
    • I go to the gym every evening.
  • 5. We are staying in a hotel right now. (always)
    • We always stay in a hotel.

Exercise 8.5: Write sentences about your daily routine using both tenses:

  • 1. (wake up) Every day, I wake up at 6 AM. Right now, I am waking up at 6:30 AM because of daylight savings time.
  • 2. (have breakfast) I usually have breakfast at 7 AM. This week, I am having breakfast at 8 AM.
  • 3. (go to work) I go to work at 9 AM every day. Today, I am going to work early for a meeting.
  • 4. (exercise) I exercise three times a week. Currently, I am exercising more to prepare for a marathon.
  • 5. (watch TV) I watch TV in the evenings. Tonight, I am not watching TV because I have a project to finish.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to distinguish between the present simple and present continuous tenses. We practiced forming and using both tenses in various contexts.
