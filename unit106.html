Unit 106: Using "In," "At," and "On" for Places

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to correctly use "in," "at," and "on" to describe locations and places.

Overview: This unit focuses on using prepositions of place to indicate specific locations and positions, making sentences clearer and more precise.

Explanation

Using "In": Use "in" to indicate that something is inside a larger space or area.

  • Example: Where is he? In the kitchen.

Using "At": Use "at" to indicate a specific point or place.

  • Example: Where is the bus? At the bus stop.

Using "On": Use "on" to indicate that something is on a surface or position.

  • Example: Where is the pen? On the box.

Examples

Using "In":

  • Where are the shoes? In the box.
  • Where are the horses? In the field.

Using "At":

  • Where is the bus? At the bus stop.
  • Where is he standing? At the window.

Using "On":

  • Where is the pen? On the box.
  • Where is the clock? On the wall.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "in" to indicate that something is inside a larger space or area, "at" to indicate a specific point or place, and "on" to indicate that something is on a surface or position. We practiced forming sentences using these prepositions to enhance clarity and precision in writing.
