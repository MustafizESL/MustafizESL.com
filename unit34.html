Unit 34: Making Offers and Invitations with "Would You Like"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "would you like" to make offers and invitations.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "would you like" to offer something to someone or to invite someone to do something.

Explanation

Using "Would You Like" for Offers: Use "would you like" + a noun to offer something to someone.

  • Example: Would you like a cup of tea?

Using "Would You Like to" for Invitations: Use "would you like to" + base form of the verb to invite someone to do something.

  • Example: Would you like to join us for dinner?

Examples

Offers:

  • Would you like a glass of juice?
  • Would you like some biscuits?

Invitations:

  • Would you like to come to the movies with us?
  • Would you like to visit the museum this weekend?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 34.1: What are the people in the pictures saying? Use "would you like"

  • 1. Would you like a cookie?
  • 2. Would you like a banana?
  • 3. Would you like some tea?
  • 4. Would you like some cheese?
  • 5. Would you like a burger?
  • 6. Would you like a piece of pie?

Exercise 34.2: What do you say to Sue in these situations? Use "would you like to"

  • 1. You want to go to the beach today. Perhaps Sue will join you. (go)
  • You say: Would you like to go to the beach today?
  • 2. You want to play badminton tomorrow. Perhaps Sue will play too. (play)
  • You say: Would you like to play badminton tomorrow?
  • 3. You have an extra ticket for a play next week. Perhaps Sue will come. (come)
  • You say: Would you like to come to the play next week?
  • 4. It's cold and Sue is going out. She hasn't got a scarf, but you have one. (borrow)
  • You say: Would you like to borrow my scarf?

Exercise 34.3: Which is right?

  • 1. 'Would you like a cookie?' 'Yes, please.'
  • 2. 'Do you like oranges?' 'Yes, I love them.'
  • 3. 'Would you like a milkshake?' 'No, thank you.'
  • 4. 'Would you like something to drink?' 'A cup of tea, please.'
  • 5. 'Would you like to go out for dinner?' 'Not now. Maybe later.'
  • 6. I like strawberries, but I don't eat them often.
  • 7. What time would you like to have breakfast tomorrow?
  • 8. 'Would you like something to snack on?' 'No, thanks. I'm full.'
  • 9. 'Do you like your new house?' 'Yes, I am very happy with it.'
  • 10. I'm exhausted. I'd like to rest now.
  • 11. 'I would like a salad, please.' 'Sure, what kind of salad?'
  • 12. 'What type of movies do you like?' 'All kinds.'

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "would you like" to make offers or invitations. We practiced forming sentences using "would you like" to offer something or invite someone to do something.
