Unit 39: Describing Weather, Asking About Distance, and Completing Sentences

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to describe the weather, ask about distances, and complete sentences using various phrases.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "it's" for describing weather, forming questions with "How far...?", and completing sentences with contextually appropriate phrases.

Explanation

Describing Weather: Use "It's..." to describe the current weather conditions.

  • Example: It's raining. / It's sunny.

Asking About Distance: Use "How far is it...?" to ask about the distance between two places.

  • Example: How far is it from here to the station?

Completing Sentences: Use contextually appropriate phrases to complete sentences.

  • Example: It's difficult to get up early in the morning.

Examples

Describing Weather:

  • It's raining.
  • It's cold.
  • It's windy.
  • It's sunny.
  • It's snowing.
  • It's cloudy.

Asking About Distance:

  • How far is it from the airport to the city center?
  • How far is it from the hotel to the beach?

Completing Sentences:

  • It's difficult to find a parking spot.
  • It's impossible to finish all this work in one day.

Exercise 39.2: Write "it is (it's)" or "is it"

  • 1. What time is it?
  • 2. We must go now. It's very late.
  • 3. Is it true that Bill can fly a helicopter?
  • 4. 'What day is it today? Tuesday?' 'No, it's Wednesday.'
  • 5. It's ten kilometers from the airport to the city center.
  • 6. Is it possible to phone you at your office?
  • 7. 'Do you want to walk to the hotel?' 'I don't know. How far is it?'
  • 8. It's Lisa's birthday today. She's 27.
  • 9. I don't believe it! It's impossible.

Exercise 39.3: Write questions with "How far...?"

  • 1. (here / the station) How far is it from here to the station?
  • 2. (the hotel / the beach) How far is it from the hotel to the beach?
  • 3. (New York / Washington) How far is it from New York to Washington?
  • 4. (your house / the airport) How far is it from your house to the airport?

  • 5. There was a storm last night. Did you hear it?
  • 6. I was afraid because it was very dark.
  • 7. It's often cold here, but there isn't much rain.
  • 8. It's a long way from here to the nearest shop.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to describe the weather, ask about distances, and complete sentences using various phrases. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance our communication skills.
