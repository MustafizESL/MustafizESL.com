Unit 63: Using Reflexive Pronouns and "Each Other"

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use reflexive pronouns and the phrase "each other" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using reflexive pronouns to refer back to the subject of the sentence and using "each other" to indicate mutual actions or feelings.

Explanation

Using Reflexive Pronouns: Reflexive pronouns refer back to the subject of the sentence.

  • Example: He looked at himself in the mirror.

Common Reflexive Pronouns: Myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves.

  • Example: I'm not angry with you. I'm angry with myself.

Using "Each Other": Use "each other" to indicate that two or more people do the same thing to one another.

  • Example: They like each other.

Examples

Using Reflexive Pronouns:

  • He looked at himself in the mirror.
  • She enjoyed herself.

Using "Each Other":

  • They like each other.
  • They can't see each other.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 63.1: Complete the sentences with myself/yourself etc.

  • 1. He looked at himself in the mirror.
  • 2. I'm not angry with you. I'm angry with myself.
  • 3. Karen had a good time in Australia. She enjoyed herself.
  • 4. My friends had a good time in Australia. They enjoyed themselves.
  • 5. I picked up a very hot plate and burnt myself.
  • 6. He never thinks about other people. He only thinks about himself.
  • 7. I want to know more about you. Tell me about yourself. (one person)
  • 8. Goodbye! Have a good trip and look after yourselves! (two people)

Exercise 63.2: Write sentences about by myself / by yourself etc.

  • 1. I went on holiday alone. I went on holiday by myself.
  • 2. When I saw him, he was alone. When I saw him, he was by himself.
  • 3. Don't go out alone. Don't go out by yourself.
  • 4. I went to the cinema alone. I went to the cinema by myself.
  • 5. My sister lives alone. My sister lives by herself.
  • 6. Many people live alone. Many people live by themselves.

Exercise 63.3: Write sentences with each other

  • 1. I like her. I like him.
    • They like each other.
  • 2. I can't see her. I can't see him.
    • They can't see each other.
  • 3. I phone her a lot. I phone him a lot.
    • They phone each other a lot.
  • 4. I don't know her. I don't know him.
    • They don't know each other.
  • 5. I'm sitting next to him. I'm sitting next to her.
    • They're / They are sitting next to each other.
  • 6. I gave her a present. I gave him a present.
    • They gave each other presents/a present.

Exercise 63.4: Complete the sentences. Use each other or ourselves/yourselves/themselves or us/you/them

  • 1. Paul and I live near each other.
  • 2. Who are those people? Do you know them?
  • 3. You can help Tom, and Tom can help you. So you and Tom can help each other.
  • 4. There's food in the kitchen. If you and Chris are hungry, you can help yourselves.
  • 5. We didn’t go to Emily's party. She didn’t invite us.
  • 6. When we go on holiday, we always enjoy ourselves.
  • 7. Mary and Jane were at school together, but they never see each other now.
  • 8. Diane and I are very good friends. We've known each other for a long time.
  • 9. 'Did you see Sam and Laura at the party?' 'Yes, but I didn’t speak to them.'
  • 10. Many people talk to themselves when they are alone.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use reflexive pronouns and the phrase "each other" to indicate mutual actions or feelings. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
