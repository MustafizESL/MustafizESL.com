Unit 24: Irregular Verbs and Verb Forms

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to form the past simple and past participle of both regular and irregular verbs.

Overview: This unit focuses on practicing the past simple and past participle forms of various verbs through different exercises.

Explanation

Past Simple and Past Participle Forms: The past simple and past participle forms of verbs can be regular (adding -ed) or irregular (changing the verb form completely).

Examples

Regular Verbs:

  • Past Simple: walked, watched, played
  • Past Participle: walked, watched, played

Irregular Verbs:

  • Past Simple: broke, began, ate
  • Past Participle: broken, begun, eaten

Exercise 24.3: Put the verb in the right form

  • 1. I washed my hands because they were dirty. (wash)
  • 2. Somebody has broken this window. (break)
  • 3. I feel good. I slept very well last night. (sleep)
  • 4. We saw a really good film yesterday. (see)
  • 5. It rained a lot while we were on holiday. (rain)
  • 6. I've lost my bag. Have you seen it? (lose, see)
  • 7. Anna's bicycle was stolen last week. (steal)
  • 8. I went to bed early because I was tired. (go)
  • 9. Have you finished your work yet? (finish)
  • 10. The shopping center was built about 20 years ago. (build)
  • 11. Lucas learned to drive when he was 18. (learn)
  • 12. I've never ridden a horse. (ride)
  • 13. Julia is a good friend of mine. I've known her for a long time. (know)
  • 14. Yesterday I fell and hurt my leg. (fall, hurt)
  • 15. My sister ran in the New York Marathon last year. Have you ever run in a marathon? (run, run)

Exercise 24.4: Complete these sentences. Choose from the box and put the verb into the correct form

  • 1. I have made some tea. Would you like some? (make)
  • 2. Have you told Sarah about your promotion? (tell)
  • 3. We played soccer on Saturday. We didn't play very well but we won the game. (play, win)
  • 4. I know Alex, but I have never met his brother. (know, meet)
  • 5. We were woken up by loud music in the middle of the night. (wake up)
  • 6. Stephanie jumped into the pool and swam to the other side. (jump, swim)
  • 7. 'Did you enjoy the meal?' 'Yes, I thought it was delicious.' (enjoy, think)
  • 8. Many different languages are spoken in India. (speak)
  • 9. Our trip cost a lot of money because we stayed in a luxury resort. (cost)
  • 10. Have you ever driven a sports car? (drive)
  • 11. All the tickets for the show were sold very quickly. (sell)
  • 12. A butterfly flew into the room through the open window. (fly)

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to form the past simple and past participle of both regular and irregular verbs. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in different contexts.
