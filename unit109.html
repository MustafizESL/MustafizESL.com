Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to correctly use prepositions of place to describe the location of people, objects, and places.

Overview: This unit focuses on using prepositions of place to indicate positions and relationships between different elements, making sentences clearer and more descriptive.

Explanation

Using Prepositions of Place: Prepositions of place describe the position or location of people, objects, or places in relation to each other.

  • Examples: behind, next to, in front of, between, above, below, under, on, by, beside, opposite, on the left, in the middle.

Examples

Using "Behind" and "In Front Of":

  • Colin is standing behind Frank.
  • Emma is sitting in front of Barbara.

Using "Next to/Beside/By" and "Between":

  • Frank is sitting next to/beside/by Emma.
  • Emma is sitting between Donna and Frank.

Using "Above" and "Below/Under":

  • The cupboard is above the sink.
  • The switch is below/under the window.

Using "On" and "By/Next to/Beside":

  • The plant is by/next to/beside the piano.
  • The cinema is on the right.

Summary
