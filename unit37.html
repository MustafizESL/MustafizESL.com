Unit 37: Using "There is" and "There are"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "there is" and "there are" to describe the presence or absence of things.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "there is" for singular nouns and "there are" for plural nouns to talk about what exists or does not exist.

Explanation

Using "There is" for Singular Nouns: Use "there is" to indicate the presence of a single item.

  • Example: There is a book on the table.

Using "There are" for Plural Nouns: Use "there are" to indicate the presence of multiple items.

  • Example: There are three chairs in the room.

Using Negative Forms: Use "there isn't" for singular nouns and "there aren't" for plural nouns to indicate the absence of something.

  • Example: There isn't a cat in the garden. / There aren't any apples left.

Examples

Affirmative:

  • There is a car parked outside.
  • There are many students in the classroom.

Negative:

  • There isn't a book on the shelf.
  • There aren't any flowers in the vase.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 37.1: Write sentences about Kentham with "There is/are" or "There isn't/aren't"

  • 1. There isn’t a castle.
  • 2. There are a lot of restaurants.
  • 3. There's a hospital.
  • 4. There isn’t a swimming pool.
  • 5. There are two cinemas.
  • 6. There isn’t a university.
  • 7. There aren’t any big hotels.

Exercise 37.2: Write sentences about your town (or a town that you know). Use "There is/are" or "There isn’t/aren't"

  • 1. There are several parks.
  • 2. There's a large library.
  • 3. There is a museum in the center.
  • 4. There are many small shops.
  • 5. There isn’t a train station.
  • 6. There aren’t many high-rise buildings.

Exercise 37.3: Write "there is / there isn’t / is there" or "there are / there aren't / are there"

  • 1. Kentham isn’t an old town. There aren’t any ancient buildings.
  • 2. Look! There is a picture of your sister in the magazine!
  • 3. 'Excuse me, is there a supermarket near here?' 'Yes, around the corner.'
  • 4. There are five members in my family: my parents, my brother, my sister, and me.
  • 5. 'How many players are there in the team?' 'Eleven.'
  • 6. This street is usually very busy. There isn’t much space to park.
  • 7. 'Is there a train to the city centre?' 'Yes, every 15 minutes.'
  • 8. 'Are there any seats available?' 'No, the theater is full.'
  • 9. There's nowhere to sit down because there aren’t any benches.

Exercise 37.4: Write sentences with "There are..." Choose from the boxes

  • 1. There are seven days in a week.
  • 2. There are eight planets in the solar system.
  • 3. There are fifteen players in a rugby team.
  • 4. There are twenty-six letters in the English alphabet.
  • 5. There are thirty days in September.
  • 6. There are fifty states in the USA.

Exercise 37.5: Write "there's / is there" or "it's / is it"

  • 1. There's a train at 10.30. 'Is it a fast train?'
  • 2. I'm not going to buy this jacket. It's too pricey.
  • 3. 'What's wrong?' 'There's something in my eye.'
  • 4. There's a blue car outside your house. Is it yours?
  • 5. 'Is there anything interesting on TV tonight?' 'Yes, there's a documentary at 9.'
  • 6. 'What's that building?' 'It's a museum.'
  • 7. 'Is there a cafe in this park?' 'No, I'm afraid not.'

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "there is" and "there are" to describe the presence or absence of things. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to talk about what exists or does not exist.
