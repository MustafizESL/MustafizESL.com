Unit 66: Using Plurals and Irregular Plurals

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to form regular and irregular plurals correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on the rules for forming plurals and how to use them in sentences appropriately.

Explanation

Forming Regular Plurals: Most nouns form their plurals by adding -s or -es.

  • Example: flower - flowers, address - addresses.

Forming Irregular Plurals: Some nouns have irregular plural forms.

  • Example: woman - women, foot - feet.

Examples

Regular Plurals:

  • boat - boats.
  • umbrella - umbrellas.

Irregular Plurals:

  • knife - knives.
  • city - cities.
