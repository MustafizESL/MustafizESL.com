Unit 55: Using Prepositions and Verb Forms

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use prepositions and verb forms correctly to complete sentences.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "to," "on," and "for" appropriately, as well as forming sentences with verbs in the -ing form.

Explanation

Using Prepositions: Use "to," "on," and "for" to indicate direction, location, purpose, or reason.

  • Example: I'm going to China next week.

Using the -ing Form: Use the -ing form of verbs to describe actions in progress or habitual activities.

  • Example: Richard often goes sailing.

Examples

Using Prepositions:

  • I'm going out for a walk.
  • Martin is going on holiday to Italy next week.

Using the -ing Form:

  • Richard has a boat. He often goes sailing.
  • Sarah went jogging after work yesterday.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 55.1: Write "to/on/for" where necessary

  • 1. I'm going to China next week.
  • 2. Richard often goes sailing. (no preposition)
  • 3. Sue went to Mexico last year.
  • 4. Would you like to go to the cinema this evening?
  • 5. Jack goes jogging every morning. (No preposition)
  • 6. I'm going out for a walk. Do you want to come?
  • 7. I'm tired because I went to bed very late last night.
  • 8. Martin is going on holiday to Italy next week.
  • 9. The weather was warm and the river was clean, so we went for a swim.
  • 10. The taxi drivers went on strike when I was in New York.
  • 11. I need some stamps, so I'm going to the post office.
  • 12. It's late. I have to go home now. (no preposition)
  • 13. Would you like to go on a tour of the city?
  • 14. Shall we go out for dinner this evening?
  • 15. My parents are going on a cruise this summer.

  • 2. Last Saturday Diane went fishing.
  • 3. Gary goes swimming every day.
  • 4. Nicole is going on holiday next month. She is going skiing.
  • 5. Peter is going out later. He has to go shopping.
  • 6. Sarah went jogging after work yesterday.

Exercise 55.3: Complete the sentences. Use the words in the box. Use "to/on/for" if necessary

  • a swim - holiday - Portugal - shopping - sleep - a walk home - riding - skiing - university
  • 1. The water looks nice. Let's go for a swim.
  • 2. After leaving school, Tina went to university where she studied psychology.
  • 3. I'm going shopping now. I have to buy a few things.
  • 4. I was very tired last night. I sat down in an armchair and went to sleep.

  • 6. We live near the mountains. In winter we go skiing most weekends.

  • 9. A: Are you going on holiday soon?
    • B: Yes, next month. We're going to Portugal. We have never been there before.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use prepositions and verb forms to complete sentences correctly. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
