Unit 25: Future Plans and Arrangements

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to express future plans and arrangements using the present continuous and present simple tenses.

Overview: This unit focuses on using the present continuous and present simple to talk about future activities.

Explanation

Present Continuous for Future Plans: Use "am/is/are" + the -ing form of the verb to talk about definite future plans.

  • Example: I am meeting my friend tomorrow.

Present Simple for Scheduled Events: Use the present simple for events that are on a fixed schedule.

  • Example: The train leaves at 8:00 AM.

Examples

Present Continuous:

  • I am visiting my grandparents this weekend.
  • She is having dinner with us tonight.
  • They are going to the beach tomorrow.

Present Simple:

  • The store opens at 9:00 AM.
  • Our flight departs at noon.
  • The concert starts at 8:00 PM.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 25.1: Look at the pictures. What are these people doing next Friday?

  • 1. Emily is attending a conference on Friday.
  • 2. John is going to a football match.
  • 3. Lisa is meeting her client.
  • 4. David is having dinner with Sarah.
  • 5. Anna and Mark are going to a concert.

Exercise 25.2: Write questions. All the sentences are future.

  • 1. (you / meet / your friends / tonight?) Are you meeting your friends tonight?
  • 2. (you / go / on vacation / next month?) Are you going on vacation next month?
  • 3. (what / you / do / this weekend?) What are you doing this weekend?
  • 4. (what time / the train / leave?) What time does the train leave?
  • 5. (when / your brother / come / to visit?) When is your brother coming to visit?

Exercise 25.3: Write sentences about yourself. What are you doing in the next few days?

  • 1. I'm attending a seminar tomorrow.
  • 2. I'm going to a friend's birthday party on Saturday.
  • 3. I'm meeting my colleagues for lunch on Friday.
  • 4. I'm visiting my family over the weekend.
  • 5. I'm going to the gym on Monday evening.
  • 6. I'm watching a movie with friends on Sunday.

Exercise 25.4: Put the verb in the present continuous (he is leaving etc.) or present simple (the train leaves etc.)

  • 1. 'Are you visiting (you/visit) your grandparents tomorrow?' 'Yes, I am.'
  • 2. We are attending (we/attend) a wedding tonight. It starts (it/start) at 6:00 PM.
  • 3. Have you heard about Emma? She's moving (she/move) to a new city next month.
  • 4. A: My friends are traveling (travel) abroad next week. B: That's great! Where are they going (they/go)?
  • 5. Tom is taking an online course right now. The course ends (end) next month.
  • 6. There's a BBQ party tomorrow, but I'm not going (I/not/go).
  • 7. I'm hanging out (I/hang out) with my friends tonight. Why don't you join us? We're meeting (we/meet) at the park at 7:00 PM.
  • 8. A: How are you traveling (you/travel) to the event tomorrow? By train? B: No, I'll take the bus. The last bus leaves (leave) at 10:00 PM.
  • 9. A: Do you want to join us for dinner tonight? B: Sure! What time does the restaurant open (the restaurant/open)?
  • 10. A: What are you planning (you/plan) to do tomorrow afternoon? B: I'm working (I/work).

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to express future plans and arrangements using the present continuous and present simple tenses. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in these tenses to talk about future activities.
