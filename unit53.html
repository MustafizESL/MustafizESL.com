Unit 53: Using Infinitives with "want" and Completing Sentences

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use infinitives with "want" and complete sentences using the correct form of verbs.

Overview: This unit focuses on forming sentences with "I want you to..." and "I don't want you to..." as well as completing sentences using the appropriate verb forms.

Explanation

Using Infinitives with "want": Use the base form of the verb with "to" after "want" to express desires or requests.

  • Example: I want you to come with me.

Completing Sentences with Verbs: Use the correct form of verbs, whether with or without "to," to complete sentences meaningfully.

  • Example: Please stay here. I don’t want you to go yet.

Examples

Using Infinitives with "want":

  • I want you to listen carefully.
  • Do you want me to wait for you?

Completing Sentences with Verbs:

  • I asked her to repeat it.
  • He let me borrow it.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 53.1: Write sentences beginning "I want you.../ I don't want you.../ Do you want me...?"

  • 1. (you must come with me) I want you to come with me.
  • 2. (listen carefully) I want you to listen carefully.
  • 3. (please don't be angry) I don't want you to be angry.
  • 4. (shall I wait for you) Do you want me to wait for you?
  • 5. (don't phone me tonight) I don't want you to phone me tonight.
  • 6. (you must meet Sarah) I want you to meet Sarah.

Exercise 53.2: Look at the pictures and complete the sentences

  • 1. Dan: Come on! Let's go to the cinema, it's a good film.
    • You: Um... er... OK.
    • Dan persuaded me to go to the cinema.
  • 2. You: Where's the station?
    • A woman: Turn left after the bridge.
    • I wanted to get to the station. A woman told me to turn left after the bridge.
  • 3. Brian: I'm not well.
    • You: You should go to the doctor.
    • Brian wasn't well. I advised him to go to the doctor.
  • 4. Linda: Can you help me?
    • Linda had a lot of luggage. She asked me to help her.
  • 5. You: I'm busy now. Come back in ten minutes.
    • Tom:
    • I wanted to make a phone call. I told him to come back in ten minutes.
  • 6. You: Can I use your phone?
    • Paul: Of course.
    • I wanted to make a phone call. Paul let me use his phone.
  • 7. You: Don’t phone before 8 o'clock.
    • Sue:
    • Sue is going to phone later. I told her not to phone before 8 o'clock.
  • 8. Do you play the piano?
    • Ann: Yes, my mother taught me.
    • Ann's mother taught her to play the piano.

Exercise 53.3: Complete these sentences with the verbs in the list. Sometimes "to" is necessary (to go / to wait etc.); sometimes "to" is not necessary (go/wait etc.)

  • arrive - borrow - get - go - go - make - repeat - tell - think - wait
  • 1. Please stay here. I don’t want you to go yet.
  • 2. I didn’t hear what she said, so I asked her to repeat it.
  • 3. 'Shall we begin?' 'No, let's wait a few minutes.'
  • 4. Are they already here? I expected them to arrive much later.
  • 5. Kevin's parents didn’t want him to get married.
  • 6. I want to stay here. You can't make me go with you.
  • 7. 'Is that your bicycle?' 'No, it’s John's. He let me borrow it.'
  • 8. Rachel can't come to the party. She told me to tell you.
  • 9. Would you like a drink? Would you like me to make (or to get) some coffee?
  • 10. 'Kate doesn’t like me.' 'What makes you think that?'

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use infinitives with "want" and complete sentences using the correct form of verbs. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
