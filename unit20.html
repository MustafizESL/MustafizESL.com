Unit 20: Present Perfect vs. Past: I have done and I did

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to differentiate between the present perfect and past simple tenses to describe actions that have happened in the past.

Overview: This unit focuses on the usage of the present perfect and past simple tenses and how to use them correctly in different contexts.

Explanation

Present Perfect Tense: Use "has" or "have" followed by the past participle of the verb.

  • Example: I have finished my homework. / She has visited Japan.

Past Simple Tense: Use the past form of the verb.

  • Example: I finished my homework yesterday. / She visited Japan last year.

Examples

Present Perfect:

  • I have seen that movie.
  • They have completed their project.
  • She has eaten lunch.

Past Simple:

  • I saw that movie last week.
  • They completed their project yesterday.
  • She ate lunch an hour ago.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 20.1: Complete the answers to the questions:

  • 1. Have you seen Emily? Yes, I saw her ten minutes ago.
  • 2. Have you started your new course? Yes, I started it last month.
  • 3. Have your parents arrived? Yes, they arrived at noon.
  • 4. Has Jack left for the trip? Yes, he left on Saturday.
  • 5. Have you worn your new jacket? Yes, I wore it yesterday.

Exercise 20.2: Are these sentences OK? Correct the verbs that are wrong (The verbs are underlined):

  • 1. I've lost my wallet. I can't find it. (Correct)
  • 2. Did you see Mark yesterday? (Correct)
  • 3. I finished my assignment at 3 o'clock. (Correct)
  • 4. I'm ready now. I've completed my tasks. (Correct)
  • 5. What time did you finish your meeting? (Correct)
  • 6. Sarah isn’t here. She's gone out. (Correct)
  • 7. Michael's grandfather passed away three years ago. (Correct)
  • 8. Where were you last night? or Where did you go last night? (Correct)

Exercise 20.3: Put the verb in the present perfect or past:

  • 1. My friend is a painter. He has painted (paint) many portraits.
  • 2. We didn’t travel (not/travel) anywhere last year.
  • 3. I played (play) soccer yesterday evening.
  • 4. What time did you wake up (you/wake up) this morning?
  • 5. Have you ever visited (you/ever/visit) a different country?
  • 6. The weather wasn’t (not/be) very nice yesterday.
  • 7. Lisa travels a lot. She has visited (visit) many cities.
  • 8. I locked (lock) the door before leaving the house this morning.
  • 9. I live in Chicago now, but I lived (live) in Boston for many years.
  • 10. What's Italy like? Is it beautiful? 'I don't know. I haven’t been (not/be) there.'

Exercise 20.4: Put the verb in the present perfect or past:

  • 1. A: Have you ever been (you/ever/be) to California?
  • B: Yes, we went (go) there on vacation two years ago.
  • A: Did you enjoy (you/enjoy) it?
  • B: Yes, it was (be) wonderful.
  • 2. A: Where's David? Have you seen (you/see) him?
  • B: Yes, he went (go) out a little while ago.
  • A: And Jenna?
  • B: I don’t know. I haven’t seen (not/see) her.
  • 3. Carla works in a shop. She has worked/has been working there for a year.
  • Before that, she was (be) a barista in a cafe. She worked (work) there for three years, but she didn’t like (not/like) it very much.
  • 4. A: Do you know Sam's brother?
  • B: I have seen (see) him a few times, but I have never spoken (never/speak) to him.
  • Have you ever spoken (you/ever/speak) to him?
  • A: Yes, I met (meet) him at a gathering last week. He's very friendly.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to differentiate between the present perfect and past simple tenses to describe actions that have happened in the past. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in these tenses.
