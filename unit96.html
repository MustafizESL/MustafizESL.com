Unit 96: Using "Give" and Related Verbs

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use the verb "give" and related verbs correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using the verb "give" and related verbs like "lend," "send," "pass," "show," and "get" to indicate transferring something from one person to another.

Explanation

Using "Give": The verb "give" is used to indicate transferring something from one person to another.

  • Example: He gave it to his brother.

Related Verbs: Related verbs include "lend," "send," "pass," "show," and "get."

  • Example: Can you lend me an umbrella?

Examples

Using "Give":

  • He gave it to his brother.
  • He gave them to Sarah.

Using Related Verbs:

  • Can you pass me the salt?
  • Can you lend me twenty pounds?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 96.1: Write sentences beginning "He gave..."

  • 1. What did Mark do with the armchair? He gave it to his brother.
  • 2. What did he do with the tennis racket? He gave it to Gary.
  • 3. What happened to the books? He gave them to Sarah.
  • 4. What about the lamp? He gave it to his sister.
  • 5. What did he do with the pictures? He gave them to Robert.
  • 6. And the ladder? He gave it to a neighbour.

Exercise 96.2: You gave presents to your friends. You decided to give them the things in the pictures. Write a sentence for each person.

  • 1. I gave Paul a book.
  • 2. I gave Joanna a plant.
  • 3. I gave Richard a tie.
  • 4. I gave Emma some chocolates/a box of chocolates.
  • 5. I gave Rachel some flowers/a bunch of flowers.
  • 6. I gave Kevin a pen.

Exercise 96.3: Write questions beginning "Can you give me...?" / "Can you pass me...?" etc.

  • 1. (you want the salt) (pass) → Can you pass me the salt?
  • 2. (you need an umbrella) (lend) → Can you lend me an umbrella?
  • 3. (you want my address) (give) → Can you give me your address?
  • 4. (you need twenty pounds) (lend) → Can you lend me twenty pounds?
  • 5. (you want some information) (send) → Can you send me some information?
  • 6. (you want to see the letter) (show) → Can you show me the letter?
  • 7. (you want some stamps) (get) → Can you get me some stamps?

Exercise 96.4: Which is right?

  • 1. I gave Sarah the keys.
  • 2. I'll lend you some money if you want.
  • 3. Did you send the letter to me?
  • 4. I want to buy you a present.
  • 5. Can you pass me the sugar, please?
  • 6. This is Lisa's bag. Can you give it to her?
  • 7. I showed the policeman my identity card.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use the verb "give" to indicate transferring something from one person to another. We also learned to use related verbs like "lend," "send," "pass," "show," and "get" to indicate specific types of transfers. We practiced forming sentences using these verbs to enhance communication skills.
