Unit 51: Using Infinitives and Gerunds

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use infinitives and gerunds in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on completing sentences with the correct form of verbs, whether in the infinitive form or gerund (-ing) form.

Explanation

Infinitives: Use the base form of the verb, sometimes with "to" (to work, to go).

  • Example: I want to phone Paul.

Gerunds: Use the -ing form of the verb (working, going).

  • Example: Please be quiet. I'm working.

Examples

Infinitives:

  • I want to phone Paul.
  • Would you like to go on a trip around the world?

Gerunds:

  • I'm working.
  • I'm staying at home tonight.

  • 10. You must phone Paul.

Exercise 51.2: Complete the sentences with a verb from the box. Sometimes you need the infinitive (work/go etc.) and sometimes you need -ing (working/going etc.)

  • 1. Please be quiet. I'm working.
  • 2. I feel tired today. I didn’t sleep very well last night.
  • 3. What time do you usually get up in the morning?
  • 4. 'Where are you going?' 'To the bank.'
  • 5. Did you watch television last night?
  • 6. Look at the plane. It's flying very low.
  • 7. You can turn off the radio. I'm not listening to it.
  • 8. They didn't eat anything because they weren't hungry.
  • 9. My friends were waiting for me when I arrived.
  • 10. 'Does Susan always wear glasses?' 'No, only for reading.'
  • 11. 'What are you doing tonight?' 'I'm staying at home.'

Exercise 51.3: Put the verb in the correct form. Choose from the infinitive (work/go etc.) or to... (to work/to go etc.) or -ing (working/going etc.)

  • 1. Shall I open the window? (open)
  • 2. It's late. I have to go now. (go)
  • 3. Amanda isn't working this week. She's on holiday. (work)
  • 4. I'm tired. I don't want to go out. (go)
  • 5. It might rain, so take an umbrella with you. (rain)
  • 6. What time do you have to leave tomorrow morning? (leave)
  • 7. I'm sorry I can't help you. (help)
  • 8. My brother is a student. He's studying physics. (study)
  • 9. Would you like to go on a trip around the world? (go)
  • 10. When you saw Maria, what was she wearing? (wear)
  • 11. When you go to London, where are you going to stay? (stay)
  • 12. I am hungry. I must have something to eat. (have)
  • 13. 'Where's Gary?' 'He's having a bath.' (have)
  • 14. I used to have a car, but I sold it last year. (have)
  • 15. He spoke very quietly. I couldn’t hear him. (hear)
  • 16. You don't look well. I don't think you should go to work today. (go)
  • 17. I don't know what he said. I wasn't listening to him. (listen)
  • 18. I am sorry I am late. I had to make a phone call. (make)
  • 19. I want to know what happened. (know) You must tell me. (tell)
  • 20. May I use your phone? (use)

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use infinitives and gerunds in various contexts. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
