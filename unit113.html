Unit 113: Using Prepositions with Verbs

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to correctly use prepositions like "to," "for," "at," etc., with verbs to describe actions and relationships.

Overview: This unit focuses on using these prepositions to form meaningful sentences, making sentences clearer and more precise.

Explanation

Using Prepositions with Verbs: Prepositions like "to," "for," "at," "about," etc., are used with verbs to describe actions, relationships, and states of being.

  • Example: She's looking at her watch.

Examples

Using Prepositions with Verbs in Sentences:

  • She's looking at her watch.
  • He's listening to the radio.
  • They're waiting for a taxi.
  • Paul is talking to Jane.
  • They're looking at a picture.
  • Sue is looking for Tom.

Using Prepositions to Complete Sentences:

  • Thank you very much for your help.
  • This isn’t my umbrella. It belongs to a friend of mine.
  • (on the phone) Can I speak to Steven Davis, please?
  • (on the phone) Thank you for calling. Goodbye.
  • What happened to Ella last night? Why didn’t she come to the party?
  • We’re thinking of/about going to Australia next year.
  • We asked the waiter for coffee, but he brought us tea.
  • ‘Do you like reading books?’ ‘It depends on the book.’
  • John was talking, but nobody was listening to what he was saying.
  • We waited for Karen until 2 o’clock, but she didn’t come.
  • If you want to contact me, you can write to me at this address.
  • Don’t forget to phone your mother tonight.
  • He’s alone all day. He never talks to anybody.
  • ‘How much does it cost to stay at this hotel?’ ‘It depends on the type of room.’
  • Catherine is thinking of/about changing her job.

Using Prepositions in Specific Contexts:

  • I looked at the newspaper, but I didn’t read it carefully.
  • When you are ill, you need somebody to look after you.
  • Excuse me, I’m looking for Hill Street. Can you tell me where it is?
  • Goodbye! Have a great holiday and look after yourself.
  • I want to take a photograph of you. Please look at the camera and smile.
  • Barry is looking for a job. He wants to work in a hotel.

Using "It Depends ..." to Answer Questions:

  • Do you want to go out with us? It depends where you're going.
  • Do you like eating in restaurants? It depends on the restaurant.
  • Do you enjoy watching TV? It depends on the programme.
  • Can you do something for me? It depends (on) what it is.
  • Are you going away this weekend? It depends on the weather.
  • Can you lend me some money? It depends (on) how much you want.
