Unit 19: Time Words: for, since, ago

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use the time words "for," "since," and "ago" to describe the duration and starting points of actions or events.

Overview: This unit focuses on the usage of "for," "since," and "ago" in the context of different tenses.

Explanation

Using "for": Indicates the duration of time something has happened.

  • Example: I have studied English for two years.

Using "since": Indicates the starting point of an action or situation.

  • Example: He has lived here since January.

Using "ago": Indicates how long ago something happened.

  • Example: I finished my homework two hours ago.

Examples

For:

  • I have known her for ten years.
  • They have been married for five years.

Since:

  • She has worked here since 2010.
  • We have lived in this house since we got married.

Ago:

  • I saw him two days ago.
  • They moved here three months ago.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 19.1: Write "for" or "since":

  • 1. Tim has been in New York since Monday.
  • 2. Tim has been in New York for a week.
  • 3. My cousin has lived in Canada for ten years.
  • 4. Laura is in her office. She has been there since 8 o'clock.
  • 5. Brazil has been an independent country since 1822.
  • 6. The train is late. We've been waiting for 30 minutes.
  • 7. No one lives in those houses. They have been empty for many years.
  • 8. George has been unwell for a long time. He has been in bed since last Friday.

Exercise 19.2: Answer these questions. Use "ago":

  • 1. When was the last time you exercised? Two hours ago.
  • 2. When was the last time you read a book? A month ago.
  • 3. When did you last see a movie? Two weeks ago.
  • 4. When was the last time you traveled by plane? Six months ago.
  • 5. When was the last time you went shopping? A week ago.

Exercise 19.3: Complete the sentences. Use "for" or "ago" with these words:

  • 1. Lisa arrived in Paris three days ago.
  • 2. Lisa has been in Paris for three days.
  • 3. Mike and Jane have been friends for fifteen years.
  • 4. Mike and Jane met fifteen years ago.
  • 5. John arrived an hour ago.
  • 6. I bought this jacket a few days ago.
  • 7. Sam has been learning French for six months.
  • 8. Have you known Mark for a long time?

Exercise 19.4: Complete the sentences with "for" or "since":

  • 1. Alice has been in Italy for a week.
  • 2. Peter has been here since Wednesday.
  • 3. It has been snowing for an hour.
  • 4. I've known Jack since 2005.
  • 5. Anna and David have been engaged for six months.
  • 6. Tom has been studying architecture for four years.
  • 7. Emma has played/has been playing the violin since she was eight years old.

Exercise 19.5: Write sentences about yourself. Begin your sentences with: I've lived... I've been... I've been learning... I've known... I've had...

  • 1. I've lived in this city for five years.
  • 2. I've been in this job for eight years.
  • 3. I've been learning Spanish for six months.
  • 4. I've known Alice for a long time.
  • 5. I've had this car since last summer.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use the time words "for," "since," and "ago" to describe the duration and starting points of actions or events. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs with these time words.
