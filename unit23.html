Unit 23: Auxiliary Verbs and Sentence Structure

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use auxiliary verbs (is/are, do/does, am not/isn't/aren't, don't/doesn't, was/were, did, have/has) correctly in various sentences.

Overview: This unit focuses on the usage of auxiliary verbs to form questions, negatives, and correct sentence structures.

Explanation

Auxiliary Verbs: Helping verbs used to form tenses, questions, and negatives.

  • Present Simple Questions: Use "do/does".
  • Present Continuous Questions: Use "am/is/are".
  • Present Simple Negatives: Use "don't/doesn't".
  • Present Continuous Negatives: Use "am not/isn't/aren't".
  • Past Simple Questions and Negatives: Use "did".
  • Past Simple Statements: Use "was/were".
  • Present Perfect Questions and Negatives: Use "have/has".

Examples

Present Simple Questions:

  • Do you like coffee? / Does she play tennis?

Present Continuous Questions:

  • Are you coming to the party? / Is he watching TV?

Present Simple Negatives:

  • I don't like coffee. / She doesn't play tennis.

Present Continuous Negatives:

  • I am not coming to the party. / He isn't watching TV.

Past Simple Questions and Negatives:

  • Did you go to the store? / I didn't go to the store.

Past Simple Statements:

  • He was at the meeting. / They were at the park.

Present Perfect Questions and Negatives:

  • Have you seen the movie? / She hasn't finished her homework.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 23.1: Write "is/are" or "do/does"

  • 1. Do you enjoy reading books?
  • 2. Where are they traveling?
  • 3. Why are you laughing?
  • 4. Does Mike live nearby?
  • 5. Do you enjoy cooking?
  • 6. Is the rain stopping?
  • 7. What time do the stores open?
  • 8. Is Anna working today?
  • 9. What does this sign mean?
  • 10. Are you feeling better?

Exercise 23.2: Write "am not/isn't/aren't" or "don't/doesn't". All these sentences are negative

  • 1. Sara doesn't work on weekends.
  • 2. I'm very sleepy. I don't want to stay up late tonight.
  • 3. I'm very sleepy. I'm not staying up late tonight.
  • 4. Alex isn't working this week. He's on leave.
  • 5. My friends are usually at home. They don't go out often.
  • 6. Peter has traveled a lot, but he doesn't speak any foreign languages.
  • 7. You can switch off the lights. I'm not using them.
  • 8. Laura has invited us to her party, but we aren't going.

Exercise 23.3: Write "was/were/did/have/has"

  • 1. Where were these shoes made?
  • 2. Did you visit the museum yesterday?
  • 3. What were you doing at 8:00?
  • 4. Where was your father born?
  • 5. Has Michael gone home?
  • 6. What time did he leave?
  • 7. When were these buildings constructed?
  • 8. Has Mary arrived yet?
  • 9. Why did you leave the meeting early?
  • 10. How long have they been together?

Exercise 23.4: Write "is/are/was/were/have/has"

  • 1. Jack has lost his wallet.
  • 2. This bridge was constructed five years ago.
  • 3. Have you completed your assignment yet?
  • 4. This city is always bustling. The streets are cleaned every day.
  • 5. Where were you born?
  • 6. I have just brewed some tea. Would you like some?
  • 7. Metal is made from ores.
  • 8. This is an old painting. It was painted a long time ago.
  • 9. Emma has purchased a new laptop.

Exercise 23.5: Complete the sentences. Choose from the box and put the verb into the correct form: build - rain - enjoy - go - pronounce - eat - listen - drive - open - go - understand

  • 1. I'm going to take an umbrella with me. It's raining.
  • 2. Why are you so tired? Did you go to bed late last night?
  • 3. Where are the cookies? Have you eaten them all?
  • 4. How is your new job? Are you enjoying it?
  • 5. My bike was badly damaged in the accident, but I was fine.
  • 6. Chris has a car, but he doesn't drive it very often.
  • 7. Sarah isn't at home. She has gone away for a few days.
  • 8. I don't understand the instructions. Can you explain them again?
  • 9. Martin is in his room. He is listening to music.
  • 10. I don't know how to say this word. How is it pronounced?
  • 11. How do you open this door? Can you show me?

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use auxiliary verbs to ask questions, make negatives, and create proper sentence structures in various tenses. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in these tenses.
