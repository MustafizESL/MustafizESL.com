Unit 3: Present Continuous (I am doing)

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use the present continuous tense to describe actions that are happening now.

Overview: This unit covers the formation and usage of the present continuous tense.

Explanation

Forming the Present Continuous: The present continuous tense is formed using the verb "to be" (am, is, are) + verb-ing.

  • I am (I'm) doing
  • You are (You're) doing
  • He/She/It is (He's/She's/It's) doing
  • We are (We're) doing
  • They are (They're) doing

Examples

Positive Statements:

  • I am (I'm) working.
  • You are (You're) eating.
  • He/She/It is (He's/She's/It's) running.
  • We are (We're) playing.
  • They are (They're) studying.

Negative Statements:

  • I am not (I'm not) sleeping.
  • You are not (You aren't) crying.
  • He/She/It is not (He/She/It isn't) dancing.
  • We are not (We aren't) singing.
  • They are not (They aren't) swimming.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 3.1: Describe what these people are doing. Use these verbs: reading, cooking, swimming, writing, dancing, drawing:

  • 1. She is ___ a book. (reading)
  • 2. He is ___ dinner. (cooking)
  • 3. They are ___ in the pool. (swimming)
  • 4. She is ___ a letter. (writing)
  • 5. He is ___ to the music. (dancing)
  • 6. They are ___ a picture. (drawing)

Exercise 3.2: Complete the sentences using these verbs: fixing, painting, shopping, playing, working, studying:

  • 1. Please be quiet. I'm ___ (working).
  • 2. 'Where's Tom?' 'He is ___ his bike.' (fixing)
  • 3. The children are ___ video games. (playing)
  • 4. Look! She is ___ the wall. (painting)
  • 5. They are ___ for groceries. (shopping)
  • 6. John is at home. He is ___ for his exams. (studying)

Exercise 3.3: Write sentences about Emma. Use She's -ing or She isn’t -ing:

  • 1. (read a book) Emma isn’t reading a book.
  • 2. (watch TV) She’s watching TV.
  • 3. (sit on a chair) She is sitting on a chair.
  • 4. (write an email) She isn’t writing an email.
  • 5. (play the violin) She isn’t playing the violin.
  • 6. (smile) She is smiling.
  • 7. (wear a hat) She is wearing a hat.
  • 8. (drink coffee) She isn’t drinking coffee.

Exercise 3.4: What is happening now? Write true sentences:

  • 1. (I / clean / my room) I’m not cleaning my room.
  • 2. (it / rain) It’s raining. or It isn’t raining.
  • 3. (I / sit / on the sofa) I’m sitting on the sofa.
  • 4. (I / eat) I’m not eating.
  • 5. (it / snow) It’s snowing. or It isn’t snowing.
  • 6. (I / learn / Spanish) I’m learning Spanish.
  • 7. (I / listen / to a podcast) I’m listening to a podcast.
  • 8. (the sun / shine) The sun is shining.
  • 9. (I / wear / slippers) I’m wearing slippers.
  • 10. (I / read / a newspaper) I’m not reading a newspaper.

Summary

Review: Summarize the key points learned in the unit.

  • Forming the present continuous tense.
  • Using the present continuous tense to describe actions happening now.
  • Practice with positive and negative statements.
