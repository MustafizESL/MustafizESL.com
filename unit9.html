Unit 9: Introducing I have ... and I’ve got ...

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "I have" and "I’ve got" to talk about possessions, relationships, and characteristics.

Overview: This unit covers the formation and usage of "I have", "I’ve got", "I don't have", "I haven’t got", "Do you have?", and "Have you got?".

Explanation

Forming "I have" and "I’ve got": Both "I have" and "I’ve got" are used to indicate possession. They can be used interchangeably, but "I’ve got" is more common in informal spoken English.

  • I have: I have a car.
  • I’ve got: I’ve got a car.

Forming "I don't have" and "I haven’t got": "I don't have" and "I haven’t got" are used to indicate the absence of possession. "I don't have" is more common in American English, while "I haven’t got" is more common in British English.

  • I don't have: I don't have a car.
  • I haven’t got: I haven’t got a car.

Forming Questions with "Do you have?" and "Have you got?": To ask about possession, we use "Do you have?" or "Have you got?". "Do you have?" is more common in American English, while "Have you got?" is more common in British English.

  • Do you have? Do you have a car?
  • Have you got? Have you got a car?

Examples

Positive Statements:

  • I have a cat.
  • I’ve got a cat.
  • She has a new job.
  • She’s got a new job.
  • They have two children.
  • They’ve got two children.

Negative Statements:

  • I do not (don’t) have a cat.
  • I haven’t got a cat.
  • She does not (doesn’t) have a new job.
  • She hasn’t got a new job.
  • They do not (don’t) have two children.
  • They haven’t got two children.

Questions:

  • Do you have a cat? Yes, I do. / No, I don’t.
  • Have you got a cat? Yes, I have. / No, I haven’t.
  • Does she have a new job? Yes, she does. / No, she doesn’t.
  • Has she got a new job? Yes, she has. / No, she hasn’t.
  • Do they have two children? Yes, they do. / No, they don’t.
  • Have they got two children? Yes, they have. / No, they haven’t.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 9.1: Rewrite the sentences using "I’ve got":

  • 1. I have a brother. → I’ve got a brother.
  • 2. She has a new phone. → She’s got a new phone.
  • 3. We have a big house. → We’ve got a big house.
  • 4. They have a lot of friends. → They’ve got a lot of friends.
  • 5. He has a bike. → He’s got a bike.

  • 4. They’ve got a big garden. → They have a big garden.
