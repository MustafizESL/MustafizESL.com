Unit 27: Future Predictions and Plans

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to express future predictions and plans using "will".

Overview: This unit focuses on using "will" to talk about future events and make predictions.

Explanation

Using "Will" for Future Predictions: Use "will" + base form of the verb to talk about things that are likely to happen in the future.

  • Example: Tomorrow it will rain.

Using "Will" for Future Plans: Use "will" + base form of the verb to make decisions at the moment of speaking.

  • Example: I think I'll go to the gym.

Examples

Future Predictions:

  • She'll be a great teacher.
  • It will be sunny tomorrow.
  • They'll arrive in an hour.

Future Plans:

  • I'll call you later.
  • We'll have dinner at 7:00 PM.
  • He'll visit his grandparents this weekend.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 27.1: Complete the sentences with "she was", "she's", or "she'll be"

  • 1. Yesterday she was in Madrid.
  • 2. Tomorrow she'll be in Rome.
  • 3. Last week she was in Lisbon.
  • 4. Next week she'll be in Berlin.
  • 5. At the moment she's in Vienna.
  • 6. Three days ago she was in Prague.
  • 7. At the end of her trip she'll be very happy.

Exercise 27.2: Where will you be? Write sentences about yourself. Use "I'll be...", "I'll probably be...", or "I don't know where I'll be"

  • 1. (at 10 o'clock tomorrow) I'll probably be at work.
  • 2. (one hour from now) I'll be in a meeting.
  • 3. (at midnight tonight) I'll probably be asleep.
  • 4. (at 3 o'clock tomorrow afternoon) I'll be at the gym.
  • 5. (two years from now) I don't know where I'll be.

Exercise 27.3: Put in "will ('ll)" or "won't"

  • 1. Don't drink soda before you go to bed. You won't sleep.
  • 2. 'Are you finished yet?' 'Not yet. I'll be ready in ten minutes.'
  • 3. I'm traveling for a few days. I'm leaving tonight, so I won't be at home tomorrow.
  • 4. It won't snow so you don't need to wear heavy boots.
  • 5. A: I feel a bit under the weather. B: Well, get some rest and you'll feel better in the morning.
  • 6. It's Laura's birthday next week. She'll be 30.
  • 7. I'm sorry I missed the meeting this morning. It won't happen again.

Exercise 27.4: Write sentences with "I think..." or "I don't think..."

  • 1. (Alex will pass the exam) I think Alex will pass the exam.
  • 2. (Alex won't pass the exam) I don't think Alex will pass the exam.
  • 3. (We'll win the championship) I think we will win the championship.
  • 4. (I won't be here next week) I don't think I will be here next week.
  • 5. (Emma will enjoy the concert) I think Emma will enjoy the concert.
  • 6. (they won't buy a new house) I don't think they will buy a new house.
  • 7. (you won't like the book) I don't think you will like the book.

Exercise 27.5: Which is right?

  • 1. We're going to the museum today. We've got tickets.
  • 2. 'What are you doing tomorrow afternoon?' 'Nothing special. I'm free.'
  • 3. They're flying to New York tomorrow morning. Their flight is at 8:00 AM.
  • 4. I'm sure your brother will help us move. He's very strong.
  • 5. 'Why are you putting on your jacket?' 'I'm going outside.'
  • 6. Do you think Anna is calling us tonight?
  • 7. Jeff can't join us on Saturday. He's working.
  • 8. Will you be available tomorrow evening?
  • 9. A: What are your plans for the weekend? B: My relatives are coming to visit.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to express future predictions and plans using "will". We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in different contexts.
