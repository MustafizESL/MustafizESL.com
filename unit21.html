Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to form and use the passive voice in both the present and past tenses.

Overview: This unit focuses on the formation and usage of passive voice sentences in different contexts.

Explanation

Forming the Passive Voice: Use the verb "to be" (am/is/are/was/were) followed by the past participle of the main verb.

  • Example: The letter is written by Jane. / The letter was written by Jane.

Examples

Present Passive:

  • The room is cleaned every day.
  • These books are sold in the bookstore.
  • The food is cooked by the chef.

Past Passive:

  • The room was cleaned yesterday.
  • These books were sold last week.
  • The food was cooked by the chef.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 21.1: Write sentences from these words. Some of the sentences are questions. Sentences 1-7 are present:

  • 1. (the car / wash / every week) The car is washed every week.
  • 2. (these clothes / wash / every day?) Are these clothes washed every day?
  • 3. (paper / make / from trees) Paper is made from trees.
  • 4. (newspapers / sell / in a shop) Newspapers are sold in a shop.
  • 5. (this door / not / lock / at night) This door isn’t locked at night.
  • 6. (we / allow / to enter here?) Are we allowed to enter here?
  • 7. (how / this problem / solve?) How is this problem solved?

Sentences 8-15 are past:

  • 8. (the car / wash / yesterday) The car was washed yesterday.
  • 9. (the house / renovate / last year) The house was renovated last year.
  • 10. (my wallet / steal / last week) My wallet was stolen last week.
  • 11. (two people / injure / in the accident) Two people were injured in the accident.
  • 12. (when / the bridge / construct?) When was the bridge constructed?
  • 13. (I / not / disturb / by the noise) I wasn’t disturbed by the noise.
  • 14. (how / the windows / break?) How were the windows broken?
  • 15. (you / invite / to Sam's wedding?) Were you invited to Sam's wedding?

Exercise 21.3: Complete the sentences. Use the passive (present or past) of these verbs: clean - build - invite - make - steal - take:

  • 1. The office is cleaned every night.
  • 2. I witnessed an accident yesterday. Two people were taken to the hospital.
  • 3. Cotton is used to make clothes.
  • 4. There was a flood in the city last week. Several houses were damaged.
  • 5. 'Where did you find this book?' It was given to me by my teacher.
  • 6. Many European shows are broadcast on American TV.
  • 7. 'Did Anna and Tom attend the concert?' 'No, they were invited but they couldn't make it.'
  • 8. 'When was this painting created?' 'It was painted in 1805.'
  • 9. My bicycle was stolen last month, but it was found by the police later.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to form and use the passive voice in both the present and past tenses. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in these tenses.
