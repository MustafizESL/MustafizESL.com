Unit 26: Future Intentions with "Going to"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to express future intentions and predictions using "going to".

Overview: This unit focuses on using "going to" to talk about future plans and predictions.

Explanation

Using "Going to" for Future Intentions: Use "going to" + base form of the verb to talk about planned actions.

  • Example: I am going to watch TV.

Using "Going to" for Predictions: Use "going to" + base form of the verb to make predictions based on current evidence.

  • Example: Look at those clouds! It's going to rain.

Examples

Future Intentions:

  • I am going to call my friend tonight.
  • They are going to travel to Spain next month.
  • She is going to start a new job.

Predictions:

  • The sky is dark. It's going to storm.
  • He is going to win the race; he's far ahead.
  • The vase is on the edge. It's going to fall.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 26.1: What are these people saying?

  • 1. I'm going to read a book.
  • 2. I'm going to take a shower.
  • 3. I'm going to adopt a dog.
  • 4. We're going to play basketball.

Exercise 26.2: Complete the sentences. Use "going to" + these verbs: do - eat - give - lie down - stay - walk - wash - watch - wear

  • 1. My hands are dirty. I'm going to wash them.
  • 2. What are you going to wear to the wedding?
  • 3. It's such a beautiful day. I'm going to walk to the park.
  • 4. Steve is going to Berlin next week. He's going to stay with some friends.
  • 5. I feel hungry. I'm going to eat this sandwich.
  • 6. It's Jane's birthday next week. We're going to give her a present.
  • 7. Bob says he's feeling very tired. He's going to lie down.
  • 8. There's a good documentary on TV tonight. Are you going to watch it?

Exercise 26.3: What is going to happen?

  • 1. It's going to snow.
  • 2. The shelf is going to collapse.
  • 3. The car is going to turn left.
  • 4. He is going to kick the ball.

Exercise 26.4: What are you going to do today or tomorrow? Write three sentences

  • 1. I'm going to call my friend this evening.
  • 2. I'm going to wake up early tomorrow.
  • 3. I'm going to buy groceries tomorrow.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to express future intentions and predictions using "going to". We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in different contexts.
