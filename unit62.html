Unit 62: Using Object Pronouns and Possessive Adjectives

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use object pronouns and possessive adjectives correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using object pronouns and possessive adjectives to make sentences clear and concise.

Explanation

Using Object Pronouns: Object pronouns replace nouns to avoid repetition and are used as the object of a verb or preposition.

  • Example: I know him.

Using Possessive Adjectives: Possessive adjectives show ownership and are used before a noun.

  • Example: It's my book.

Examples

Using Object Pronouns:

  • I know him, but I can't remember his name.
  • Have you seen her?

Using Possessive Adjectives:

  • We invited her to stay with us at our house.
  • He gave me his address and I gave him mine.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 62.1: Answer the question in the same way

  • 1. Do you know that man?
    • Yes, I know him, but I can't remember his name.
  • 2. Do you know that woman?
    • Yes, I know her, but can't remember her name.
  • 3. Do you know those people?
    • Yes, I know them, but I can't remember their names.
  • 4. Do you know me?
    • Yes, I know you, but I can't remember your name.

Exercise 62.2: Complete the sentences in the same way

  • 1. We invited her to stay with us at our house.
  • 2. He invited us to stay with him at his house.
  • 3. They invited me to stay with them at their house.
  • 4. I invited them to stay with me at my house.
  • 5. She invited us to stay with her at her house.
  • 6. Did you invite him to stay with you at your house?

Exercise 62.3: Complete the sentences in the same way

  • 1. I gave him my address and he gave me his.
  • 2. I gave her my address and she gave me hers.
  • 3. He gave me his address and I gave him mine.
  • 4. We gave them our address and they gave us theirs.
  • 5. She gave him her address and he gave her his.
  • 6. You gave us your address and we gave you ours.
  • 7. They gave you their address and you gave them yours.

Exercise 62.4: Write him/her/yours etc.

  • 1. Where's Amanda? Have you seen her?
  • 2. Where are my keys? Where did I put them?
  • 3. This letter is for Bill. Can you give it to him?
  • 4. We don't see our neighbours much.
  • 5. 'I can't find my pen. Can I use yours?' 'Yes, of course.'
  • 6. We are going to the cinema. Why don't you come with us?
  • 7. Did your sister pass her exams?
  • 8. Some people talk about their jobs all the time.
  • 9. Last night I went out for a meal with a friend of mine.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use object pronouns and possessive adjectives to make sentences clear and concise. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
