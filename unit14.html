Unit 14: Past Continuous vs. Past Simple

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use the past continuous and past simple tenses to describe actions that happened in the past.

Overview: This unit focuses on the formation and usage of the past continuous and past simple tenses, and how to use them together.

Explanation

Forming the Past Continuous: Use "was" or "were" followed by the -ing form of the verb.

  • Example: I was watching TV. / They were playing soccer.

Forming the Past Simple: Use the past form of the verb.

  • Example: I watched TV. / They played soccer.

Using Past Continuous and Past Simple Together: The past continuous describes a longer action that was in progress, while the past simple describes a shorter action that happened during the longer action.

  • Example: I was watching TV when the phone rang.

Examples

Past Continuous:

  • I was reading a book.
  • She was cooking dinner.
  • They were playing basketball.

Past Simple:

  • I read a book.
  • She cooked dinner.
  • They played basketball.

Past Continuous and Past Simple Together:

  • I was reading a book when she called.
  • She was cooking dinner when the power went out.
  • They were playing basketball when it started to rain.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 14.1: Put the verbs in the correct form, past continuous or past simple:

  • 1. Karen (break) broke her leg last month. It (happen) happened when she (ride) was riding her bike. She (fall) fell off.
  • 2. The bus (arrive) arrived at the stop and Tom (get) got off. His friends, Ben and Lisa, (wait) were waiting to meet him.
  • 3. Yesterday, Sam (walk) was walking down the street when he (see) saw his old friend. They (stop) stopped to chat for a while.
  • 4. The kids (play) were playing in the yard when it (start) started to rain. They (run) ran inside quickly.
  • 5. I (have) was having dinner when the power (go) went out. I (light) lit some candles to see.

Exercise 14.2: Put the verb into the past continuous or past simple:

  • 1. A: What (you/do) were you doing when the alarm (go) went off? B: I (sleep) was sleeping.
  • 2. A: Was Mary busy when you (call) called her? B: Yes, she (study) was studying.
  • 3. A: What time (the package/arrive) did the package arrive today? B: It (come) came while I (eat) was eating breakfast.
  • 4. A: Was John at the office today? B: No, he (not/go) didn’t go to work. He was sick.
  • 5. A: How fast (you/drive) were you driving when you (get) got pulled over? B: I'm not sure, but I (not/drive) wasn’t driving too fast.
  • 6. A: (your team/win) Did your team win the match last night? B: The weather was bad, so we (not/play) didn’t play.
  • 7. A: How (you/break) did you break the vase? B: We (play) were playing ball. I (throw) threw it and it (hit) hit the vase.
  • 8. A: (you/see) Did you see Peter yesterday? B: Yes, he (wear) was wearing a new jacket.
  • 9. A: What (you/do) were you doing at midnight? B: I (read) was reading a book.
  • 10. A: I (lose) lost my wallet yesterday. B: How (you/get) did you get home? A: I (borrow) borrowed money from a friend.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use the past continuous and past simple tenses to describe actions that happened in the past. We practiced forming and using both regular and irregular verbs in these tenses.
