Unit 56: Using "Get" and Verb Forms

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "get" in various contexts and complete sentences using appropriate verb forms.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "get" to form sentences that express obtaining, becoming, and reaching, as well as using "get" in phrasal verbs.

Explanation

Using "Get" to Obtain: Use "get" to express obtaining something.

  • Example: Did you get my postcard?

Using "Get" to Become: Use "get" to describe a change in state or condition.

  • Example: It's getting dark.

Using "Get" to Reach: Use "get" to express reaching a destination.

  • Example: I left home at 7:30 and got to work at 8:15.

Using "Get" in Phrasal Verbs: Use "get" in phrasal verbs to describe actions involving movement or change.

  • Example: I got on the bus.

Examples

Using "Get" to Obtain:

  • I got a new computer.

Using "Get" to Become:

  • It's getting late.

Using "Get" to Reach:

  • I left the party at 11:15 and got home at midnight.

Using "Get" in Phrasal Verbs:

  • Kate got in the car and drove away.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 56.1: Complete these sentences. Use get/gets and choose from the box

  • Box: a doctor - a lot of rain - a taxi - my postcard - the job - a good salary - a new computer - a ticket - some milk - your jacket
  • 1. Did you get my postcard? I sent it a week ago.
  • 2. Where did you get your jacket? It's very nice.
  • 3. Quick! This man is ill. We must get a doctor.
  • 4. I don't want to walk home. Let's get a taxi.
  • 5. Tom has an interview tomorrow. I hope he gets the job.
  • 6. When you go out, can you get some milk?
  • 7. 'Are you going to the concert?' 'Yes, if I can get a ticket.'
  • 8. Margaret has got a well-paid job. She gets a good salary.
  • 9. The weather is horrible here in winter. We get a lot of rain.
  • 10. I'm going to get a new computer.

Exercise 56.2: Complete these sentences. Use getting + these words: cold - dark - late - married - ready

  • 1. Drink your coffee. It's getting cold.
  • 2. Turn on the light. It's getting dark.
  • 3. 'I'm getting married next week.' 'Really? Congratulations!'
  • 4. 'Where's Karen?' 'She's getting ready to go out.'
  • 5. It's getting late. It's time to go home.

Exercise 56.3: Complete these sentences. Use get/gets/got + these words: angry - better - married - old - wet

  • 1. If you don't eat, you get hungry.
  • 2. Don't go out in the rain. You'll get wet.
  • 3. My brother got married last year. His wife's name is Sarah.
  • 4. Martin is always very calm. He never gets angry.
  • 5. We tried to find the hotel, but got lost.
  • 6. Everybody wants to stay young, but we all get old.
  • 7. Yesterday the weather wasn't so good at first, but it got better during the day.

Exercise 56.4: Write sentences with "I left..." and "got to..."

  • 1. Home / 7:30 → Work / 8:15
    • I left home at 7:30 and got to work at 8:15.
  • 2. London / 10:15 → Bristol / 11:45
    • I left London at 10:15 and got to Bristol at 11:45.
  • 3. The party / 11:15 → Home / Midnight
    • I left the party at 11:15 and got home at midnight.
  • 4. Write a sentence about yourself.
    • (Example answer) I left home at 8:30 and got to the airport at 10 o'clock.

Exercise 56.5: Write "got in / got out of / got on / got off"

  • 1. Kate got in the car and drove away.
  • 2. I got off the bus and walked to my house from the bus stop.
  • 3. Isabel got out of the car, shut the door and went into a shop.
  • 4. I made a stupid mistake. I got on the wrong train.
