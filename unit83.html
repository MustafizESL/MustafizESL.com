Unit 83: Using "Much" and "Many"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "much" and "many" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "much" and "many" to refer to different quantities, and how to use these words naturally in sentences.

Explanation

Using "Much": Use "much" with uncountable nouns to refer to a large quantity.

  • Example: I don’t drink much coffee.

Using "Many": Use "many" with countable nouns to refer to a large number.

  • Example: There aren’t many hotels in this town.

Examples

Using "Much" and "Many":

  • Do you drink much coffee?
  • There aren’t many hotels in this town.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 83.1: Write "much" or "many"

  • 1. Did you buy much food?
  • 2. There aren’t many hotels in this town.
  • 3. We haven’t got much petrol. We need to stop and get some.
  • 4. Were there many people on the train?
  • 5. Did many students fail the exam?
  • 6. Paula hasn’t got much money.
  • 7. I wasn’t hungry, so I didn’t eat much.
  • 8. I don’t know where Gary lives these days. I haven’t seen him for many years.
  • Write "How much" or "How many":
  • 9. How many people are coming to the party?
  • 10. How much milk do you want in your coffee?
  • 11. How much bread did you buy?
  • 12. How many players are there in a football team?

Exercise 83.2: Complete the sentences. Use "much" or "many" with these words: books - countries - luggage - people - time - times

  • 1. I don’t read very much. I haven’t got many books.
  • 2. Hurry up! We haven’t got much time.
  • 3. Do you travel a lot? Have you been to many countries?
  • 4. Tina hasn’t lived here very long, so she doesn’t know many people.
  • 5. 'Have you got much luggage?' 'No, only this bag.'
  • 6. I know Tokyo well. I've been there many times.

Exercise 83.3: Complete the sentences. Use "a lot of" + these words: accidents - books - fun - interesting things - traffic

  • 1. I like reading. I have a lot of books.
  • 2. We enjoyed our visit to the museum. We saw a lot of interesting things.
  • 3. This road is very dangerous. There are a lot of accidents.
  • 4. We enjoyed our holiday. We had a lot of fun.
  • 5. It took me a long time to drive here. There was a lot of traffic.

Exercise 83.4: In some of these sentences "much" is not natural. Change the sentences or write "OK"

  • 1. Do you drink much coffee? (OK)
  • 2. I drink a lot of tea. (OK)
  • 3. It was a cold winter. We had a lot of snow. (OK)
  • 4. There wasn’t much snow last winter. (OK)
  • 5. It cost a lot of money to travel around the world. (OK)
  • 6. We had a cheap holiday. It didn’t cost much. (OK)
  • 7. Do you know much about computers? (OK)
  • 8. 'Have you got any luggage?' 'Yes, a lot.' (OK)

Exercise 83.5: Write sentences about these people. Use "much" and "a lot"

  • 1. Jim loves films. (go to the cinema)
    • He goes to the cinema a lot.
  • 2. Nicole thinks TV is boring. (watch TV)
    • She doesn't watch TV much.
  • 3. Tina is a good tennis player. (play tennis)
    • She plays tennis a lot.
  • 4. Martin doesn't like driving. (use his car)
    • He doesn't use his car much. (or... a lot)
  • 5. Paul spends most of the time at home. (go out)
    • He doesn't go out much. (or... a lot)
  • 6. Sue has been all over the world. (travel)
    • She travels a lot.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "much" with uncountable nouns to refer to a large quantity, and "many" with countable nouns to refer to a large number. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
