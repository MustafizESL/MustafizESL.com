Unit 69: Using "A", "An" and "The"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "a", "an", and "the" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using the definite article "the" and the indefinite articles "a" and "an" to refer to specific and non-specific nouns.

Explanation

Using "A/An": Use "a" before words that begin with a consonant sound and "an" before words that begin with a vowel sound for singular countable nouns.

  • Example: a hat, an apple.

Using "The": Use "the" to refer to specific nouns that are known to the speaker and listener.

  • Example: the book, the car.

Examples

Using "A/An":

  • Eva is an interesting person.
  • Can I ask a question?

Using "The":

  • We enjoyed our trip. The hotel was very nice.
  • Where's Tom? He's in the garden.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 69.1: Write "a/an" or "the"

  • 1. We enjoyed our trip. The hotel was very nice.
  • 2. 'Can I ask a question?' 'Sure. What do you want to know?'
  • 3. You look very tired. You need a holiday.
  • 4. 'Where's Tom?' 'He's in the garden.'
  • 5. Eva is an interesting person. You should meet her.
  • 6. A: Excuse me, can you tell me how to get to the city centre?
    • B: Yes, go straight on to the end then take the next turning left.
  • 7. A: Shall we go out for a meal this evening?
    • B: Yes, that's a good idea.
  • 8. It's a nice morning. Let's go for a walk.
  • 9. Amanda is a student. When she finishes her studies, she wants to be a journalist. She lives with two friends in an apartment near the college where she is studying. The apartment is small, but she likes it.
  • 10. Peter and Mary have got two children, a boy and a girl. The boy is seven years old, and the girl is three. Peter works in a factory. Mary hasn't got a job at the moment.

Exercise 69.2: Complete the sentences. Use "a" or "the" + these words: airport - cup - dictionary - door - floor - picture

  • 1. Can you open the door, please?
  • 2. How far is it to the airport?
  • 3. Can I have a cup of coffee, please?
  • 4. That's a nice picture. I like it.
  • 5. Can you pass me the dictionary, please?
  • 6. Why are you sitting on the floor?

Exercise 69.3: These sentences are not correct. Put in "a/an" or "the" where necessary

  • 1. Don't forget to turn off the light when you go out.
  • 2. Enjoy your trip and don't forget to send me a postcard.
  • 3. What is the name of this village?
  • 4. Canada is a very big country.
  • 5. What is the largest city in Canada?
  • 6. I like this room, but I don't like the colour of the carpet.
  • 7. 'Are you OK?' 'No, I've got a headache.'
  • 8. We live in an old house near the station.
  • 9. What is the name of the director of the film we saw last night?

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "a", "an", and "the" to refer to specific and non-specific nouns. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
