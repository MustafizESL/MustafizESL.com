Unit 36: Using "Used to" and the Present Simple

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "used to" to talk about past habits and states, and the present simple to talk about current habits and states.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "used to" for past habits and states, and the present simple for current habits and states.

Explanation

Using "Used to" for Past Habits and States: Use "used to" + base form of the verb to talk about habits or states that were true in the past but are not true now.

  • Example: I used to play the piano when I was a child.

Using the Present Simple for Current Habits and States: Use the present simple to talk about habits or states that are true now.

  • Example: I play the piano every day.

Examples

Past Habits and States:

  • She used to have long hair.
  • He used to work at a different company.

Current Habits and States:

  • She has short hair now.
  • He works at a new company.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 36.1: Complete the sentences with "used to"

  • 1. She used to have curly hair.
  • 2. He used to play basketball.
  • 3. She used to be a chef.
  • 4. They used to live in the mountains.
  • 5. He used to wear a hat.
  • 6. This place used to be a bakery.

Exercise 36.2: Write sentences about Karen with "used to..."

  • 1. She used to jog every morning.
  • 2. She used to go out with friends in the evening.
  • 3. She used to paint as a hobby.
  • 4. She used to read novels regularly.
  • 5. She used to play the piano.
  • 6. She used to travel frequently.

Exercise 36.3: Complete these sentences. Use "used to" or the present simple (I play / he lives etc.)

  • 1. I used to play the violin. I stopped a few years ago.
  • 2. 'Do you do any exercise?' 'Yes, I run daily.'
  • 3. 'Do you have a pet?' 'No, I used to have a dog, but it passed away.'
  • 4. George used to be a teacher. Now, he is a writer.
  • 5. 'Do you commute by car?' 'Sometimes, but mostly I take the train.'
  • 6. When I was younger, I never used to like vegetables, but I enjoy them now.
  • 7. Mary loves cooking. She cooks every evening.
  • 8. We used to live by the beach, but we moved to the city center recently.
  • 9. Usually, I start my day at 6 o'clock, so I get up very early.
  • 10. What activities did you use to do when you were a kid?

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "used to" to talk about past habits and states, and the present simple to talk about current habits and states. We practiced forming sentences using "used to" and the present simple to talk about past and current habits and states.
