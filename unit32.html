Unit 32: Using "Should" for Advice

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "should" to give and ask for advice.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "should" to express recommendations and opinions.

Explanation

Using "Should" for Advice: Use "should" + base form of the verb to give advice or make recommendations.

  • Example: You should eat more vegetables.

Examples

Advice:

  • You should study harder for your exams.
  • They should save more money.
  • She should talk to her boss about the problem.

Negative Advice:

  • You shouldn't skip breakfast.
  • He shouldn't spend so much time on his phone.

Practice Exercises

Exercise 32.1: Complete the sentences. Use "you should" + these verbs: eat - go - take - visit - watch - wear

  • 1. When you play tennis, you should focus on the ball.
  • 2. It's late and you are very tired. You should get some rest.
  • 3. You should eat a balanced diet.
  • 4. If you have time, you should visit the new art gallery. It's fascinating.
  • 5. When you are driving, you should buckle up.
  • 6. It's too far to walk from here to the mall. You should take a cab.

Exercise 32.2: Write about the people in the pictures. Use "He/She shouldn't... so..."

  • 1. She shouldn't watch TV so late.
  • 2. He shouldn't eat so many sweets.
  • 3. She shouldn't work such long hours.
  • 4. He shouldn't drive so recklessly.

Exercise 32.3: You are not sure what to do, so you ask a friend. Write questions with "Do you think I should...?"

  • 1. You are in a shop. You are trying on a sweater. (buy?)
  • You ask your friend: Do you think I should buy this sweater?
  • 2. You can't swim. (learn?)
  • You ask your friend: Do you think I should learn to swim?
  • 3. You don't like your neighborhood. (move?)
  • You ask your friend: Do you think I should move?

Exercise 32.4: Write sentences with "I think... should..." and "I don't think... should..."

  • 1. We have to wake up early tomorrow. (leave now)
  • I think we should leave now.
  • 2. That shirt is too small for you. (buy it)
  • I don't think you should buy it.
  • 3. You don't use your bike. (sell it)
  • I think you should sell it.
  • 4. Kate needs a break. (take a vacation)
  • I think she should take a vacation.
  • 5. Tom and Lisa are too young. (get married)
  • I don't think they should get married.
  • 6. You are not feeling well this morning. (go to work)
  • I don't think you should go to work.
  • 7. Jack isn't well today. (see a doctor)
  • I think he should see a doctor.
  • 8. The restaurant is too pricey for us. (dine there)
  • I don't think we should dine there.

Exercise 32.5: What do you think? Write sentences with "should". Example answers

  • 1. I think everyone should learn basic first aid.
  • 2. I think everyone should have access to clean water.
  • 3. I think people should exercise regularly.
  • 4. I don't think children should have too much screen time.
  • 5. I think I should read more books.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "should" to give advice or make recommendations. We practiced forming sentences using "should" to express recommendations and opinions.
