Unit 76: Using "Some" and "Any"

Home About Courses Contact

Introduction

Objective: By the end of this unit, learners will understand how to use "some" and "any" correctly in various contexts.

Overview: This unit focuses on using "some" and "any" to refer to an unspecified quantity of something.

Explanation

Using "Some": Use "some" in positive statements, offers, and requests.

  • Example: I bought some cheese. Would you like some tea?

Using "Any": Use "any" in negative statements and questions.

  • Example: I didn't buy any bread. Do you have any questions?

Examples

Using "Some" and "Any":

  • There are some beautiful flowers in the garden.
  • Have you got any brothers or sisters?

Practice Exercises

Exercise 76.1: Write "some" or "any"

  • 1. I bought some cheese, but I didn't buy any bread.
  • 2. I'm going to the post office, I need some stamps.
  • 3. There aren't any shops in this part of town.
  • 4. Gary and Alice haven’t got any children.
  • 5. Have you got any brothers or sisters?
  • 6. There are some beautiful flowers in the garden.
  • 7. Do you know any good hotels in London?
  • 8. 'Would you like some tea?' 'Yes, please.'
  • 9. When we were on holiday, we visited some very interesting places.
  • 10. Don't buy any rice. We don't need any.
  • 11. I went out to buy some oranges, but they didn't have any in the shop.
  • 12. I'm thirsty. Can I have some water, please?

Exercise 76.2: Complete the sentences. Use "some" or "any" + the words in the box

  • Box: air - cheese - help - milk - questions - batteries - friends - languages - photographs - shampoo
  • 1. I want to wash my hair. Is there any shampoo?
  • 2. The police want to talk to you. They want to ask you some questions.
  • 3. I haven’t got my camera, so I can't take any photographs.
  • 4. Do you speak any foreign languages?
  • 5. Yesterday evening I went to a restaurant with some friends of mine.
  • 6. Can I have some milk in my office, please?
  • 7. The radio isn’t working. There aren’t any batteries in it.
  • 8. It’s hot in this office. I'm going out for some fresh air.
  • 9. A: Would you like some cheese?
    • B: No, thank you, I've had enough to eat.
  • 10. I can do this job alone. I don’t need any help.

Exercise 76.3: Complete the sentences. Use "some" or "any"

  • 1. Jane didn’t take any photographs, but I took some. (I/take)
  • 2. 'Where's your luggage?' 'I haven’t got any.' (I/not/have)
  • 3. 'Do you need any money?' 'No, thank you. I've got some / I have some. (I/have)
  • 4. 'Can you lend me some money?' 'I'm sorry, but I haven’t got any / I haven’t any / I don’t have any.' (I/not/have)
  • 5. The tomatoes in the shop didn't look very good, so I didn’t buy any. (I/not/buy)
  • 6. There were some nice oranges in the shop, so I bought some. (I/buy)
  • 7. 'How many phone calls did you make yesterday?' 'I didn’t make any.' (I/not/make)

Exercise 76.4: Write "something/somebody" or "anything/anybody"

  • 1. A woman stopped me and said something, but I didn't understand.
  • 2. 'What's wrong?' 'There's something in my eye.'
  • 3. Do you know anything about politics?
  • 4. I went to the shop but I didn't buy anything.
  • 5. Somebody/someone has broken the window. I don’t know who.
  • 6. There isn’t anything in the bag. It's empty.
  • 7. I'm looking for my keys. Has anybody/anyone seen them?
  • 8. Would you like something to drink?
  • 9. I didn't eat anything because I wasn't hungry.
  • 10. This is a secret. Don't tell anybody/anyone.

Summary

In this unit, we learned how to use "some" and "any" to refer to an unspecified quantity of something. We practiced forming sentences using these structures to enhance communication skills.
